                              <h2 class="section-title">
              What Is Business Email Compromise?
            </h2>
                                   <div class="paragraph paragraph--type--text-with-image paragraph--view-mode--default">
                                          <div class="media-position-top float-none media-top"></div>
        
  <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-paragraph-text field--type-text-long field--label-visually_hidden">
    <div class="field__label visually-hidden">Text</div>
              <div class="field__item"><p style="margin-bottom:11px;">Business Email Compromise continues to deceive people into believing they are interacting with a trusted sender. Unfortunately, by gaining trust, the cybercriminal can transfer funds to their accounts, gain access to sensitive data, or other ill-intentioned actions with great success.</p><p style="margin-bottom:11px;">BEC attacks are cunning and when combined with human error, are so successful the FBI labeled them “one of the most financially damaging online crimes.” Traditional email security practices of focusing on content and infrastructure analysis will not work because threat actors' tactics are constantly evolving within business and individual user environments, so it’s not enough to keep up with the latest malicious tactics—you need to stay one step ahead.</p></div>
          </div>

      </div>




          
              </div>
    </div>
  </div>

                <div class="paragraph paragraph--type--section paragraph--view-mode--default section row scrollable-content bg-7 dark-arrow" id="anatomy-of-an-attack">
        <div class="col-sm-12">
      <div class="container">
                              <h2 class="section-title">
              What Are the Stages of a BEC Attack?
            </h2>
                                 <div class="section-subtext text-center">
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-section-subtext field--type-text-long field--label-hidden field__item"><p>Business Email Compromise comes in many forms. These are sham security alerts, last-minute payment requests, bogus past-due statements, fraudulent wiring instructions, and more. BEC and phishing scams continue to be the primary attack vectors into organizations—and according to the <a href="https://www.ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2023_IC3Report.pdf" target="_blank">FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) report in 2023</a>, nearly $3 billion in losses were a direct result of successful BEC scams.</p><p>&nbsp;</p></div>
      </div>
                      <!-- Display Type "Tabs" -->

<div class="accordion tabbed-accordion" id="tabbedAccordion-1342">
          <div class="tabbed-accordion-item">
              <h3 class="tab-header" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-0">
                      <a class="btn" type="button" data-toggle="collapse" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-0" data-target="#tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-0">
                      STAGE 1: Preparation
          </a>
        </h3>
                    <div class="tab-content collapse show" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-0" class="collapse show" aria-labelledby="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-0" data-parent="#tabbedAccordion-1342">
                                <div class="item-text"><h4>Build Target List</h4><p>Cybercriminals start by building a target list, often using business contact databases, mining LinkedIn profiles, and even scouring the target's website to identify key individuals and relationships.</p></div>
              </div>
    </div>
  
        <div class="tabbed-accordion-item">
              <h3 class="tab-header" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-1">
                      <a class="btn collapsed" type="button" data-toggle="collapse" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-1" data-target="#tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-1">
                      STAGE 2: Execution
          </a>
        </h3>
                    <div class="tab-content collapsed collapse" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-1" class="collapse" aria-labelledby="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-1" data-parent="#tabbedAccordion-1342">
                                <div class="item-text"><h4>Launch Attack</h4><p>Attackers launch BEC campaigns, sending email to their targeted list. BEC attacks have no malicious payload and will use impersonation tactics like display name deception, spoofing, or lookalike domains to compromise your account.</p></div>
              </div>
    </div>
  
        <div class="tabbed-accordion-item">
              <h3 class="tab-header" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-2">
                      <a class="btn collapsed" type="button" data-toggle="collapse" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-2" data-target="#tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-2">
                      STAGE 3: Deception
          </a>
        </h3>
                    <div class="tab-content collapsed collapse" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-2" class="collapse" aria-labelledby="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-2" data-parent="#tabbedAccordion-1342">
                                <div class="item-text"><h4>Apply Social Engineering</h4><p>To convince the victim to take action, attackers impersonate people of authority, such as the CEO or CFO, and express urgency within the request.</p></div>
              </div>
    </div>
  
        <div class="tabbed-accordion-item">
              <h3 class="tab-header" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-3">
                      <a class="btn collapsed" type="button" data-toggle="collapse" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-3" data-target="#tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-3">
                      STAGE 4: Action
          </a>
        </h3>
                    <div class="tab-content collapsed collapse" id="tabbedAccordion-1342-collapse-3" class="collapse" aria-labelledby="tabbedAccordion-1342-heading-3" data-parent="#tabbedAccordion-1342">
                                <div class="item-text"><h4>Reap Rewards</h4><p>With trust, authority and urgency established, the victim proceeds with the request. Unfortunately, the results can end with great financial loss or a colossal data breach.</p></div>
              </div>
    </div>
  

</div>

              What Is BEC?

        <div class="card-body text-left">
                      <div class="item-text">
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-icon-text field--type-text-long field--label-hidden field__item"><p>BEC is a form of phishing that leverages social engineering methods to masquerade as executives or others to deceive people into believing they’re interacting with a trusted sender.</p></div>

              What Is the Main Goal of BEC? 
            </div>
          </a>
        </div>
            <div class="faq-content collapse" id="faqAccordion-4607-collapse-1" class="collapse" aria-labelledby="faqAccordion-4607-heading-1" data-parent="#faqAccordion-4607">

                      <div class="item-text">
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-icon-text field--type-text-long field--label-hidden field__item"><p>The primary goal of those cybercriminals using the BEC vector is to gain trust and manipulate the user into sending money to their accounts, gaining access to sensitive data, or performing other ill-intended actions. Unlike suspicious emails and malicious links, BEC attacks are more sophisticated, and therefore better at tricking recipients into believing the communication is from a trusted source or individual.</p></div>

              What Are Some Examples of BEC?
            </div>
          </a>
        </div>
            <div class="faq-content collapse" id="faqAccordion-4607-collapse-2" class="collapse" aria-labelledby="faqAccordion-4607-heading-2" data-parent="#faqAccordion-4607">

                      <div class="item-text">
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-icon-text field--type-text-long field--label-hidden field__item"><p>BEC targets can be Fortune 500 companies, charities, or even government. Here are two costly BEC examples:</p><ol><li>The finance director of Puerto Rico’s Industrial Development Company was tricked into transferring more than $2.6 million when he received an account impersonation email appearing to be from the Puerto Rico Employment Retirement System. The email stated that there had been an update to the payment methods. Fortunately, the funds were recovered with the help of the FBI.&nbsp;</li><li>Another example involves CEO fraud in which cybercriminals impersonated executives at food companies convincing suppliers to ship thousands of dollars of powdered milk to the criminals. This prompted US federal agencies to warn organizations to take the proper precautions to “protect their brand and reputation.”&nbsp;</li></ol></div>

Fortra's Cloud Email Protection does this by:</p><ul><li>Leveraging advanced data science,&nbsp;including ML models, neural networks, and more&nbsp;</li><li>Using global inbox&nbsp;intelligence to collect&nbsp;and analyze&nbsp;intel on threats happening not only in your organization, but from a host of global enterprises comprised of millions of users</li><li>Proactively monitoring for lookalike domain registrations created with the intent to prey on user inboxes</li></ul></div>
