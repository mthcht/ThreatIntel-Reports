    <h1 class="text-center">All gas, no brakes: Time to come to AI church</h1>

    <div class="text-center m-1">
        <div class="post-author">
            <span>By </span>
                <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/author/joe-marshall/">Joe Marshall</a>
        </div>

        <br/>
        <time class="post-datetime" datetime="February 5, 2026 14:00">
            Thursday, February 5, 2026 14:00
        </time>

        <div class="m-3">
                        <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/category/threat-source-newsletter/" class="category primary-category">
                            Threat Source newsletter
                        </a>
                            </div>
    </div>

    <section class="post-content-wrapper mt-5">
        <div class="post-content" id="post-content">
            <p>Welcome to this week’s edition of the Threat Source newsletter.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>Brothers and&nbsp;sisters,&nbsp;gather close for a moment. We are all security followers here gathered in fellowship and community,&nbsp;with one joyful spirit to fight the good fight and do good out there in the security world. &nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>It is with that spirit that I&nbsp;have to&nbsp;mention&nbsp;<a href="https://openclaw.ai/" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>Clawdbot</u></a>.&nbsp;Clawdbot&nbsp;(aka&nbsp;Moltbot&nbsp;or&nbsp;OpenClaw) is a locally&nbsp;run&nbsp;open-source&nbsp;agentic application&nbsp;that&nbsp;acts on your behalf.&nbsp;Want&nbsp;to check into a flight? Reply to an&nbsp;email?&nbsp;Vibe code&nbsp;Skynet?&nbsp;Clawdbot's got&nbsp;you.&nbsp;As&nbsp;of&nbsp;writing this, it has 157k stars on&nbsp;Github.&nbsp;To&nbsp;make it work,&nbsp;the only teeny tiny thing you have to&nbsp;do&nbsp;is feed&nbsp;Clawdbot&nbsp;all of your private information (like&nbsp;logins,&nbsp;passwords,&nbsp;and API&nbsp;keys)&nbsp;and&nbsp;you’re&nbsp;off to the races.&nbsp;No big deal,&nbsp;right?&nbsp;It completely acts on&nbsp;your behalf,&nbsp;with little input if&nbsp;that’s&nbsp;what you&nbsp;desire.&nbsp;If&nbsp;that just made the hair on&nbsp;the back of your&nbsp;neck stand up a little,&nbsp;yeah, me too.</p><p>By now, the security hot mess&nbsp;that is&nbsp;Clawdbot&nbsp;has made its way from obscurity into the mainstream&nbsp;news,&nbsp;<a href="https://cybersecuritynews.com/clawdbot-chats-exposed/" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>and it’s all bad</u></a>. Shocker.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>This is important. I cannot stress this enough. Everyone&nbsp;in the&nbsp;room who&nbsp;ran as fast as possible and&nbsp;installed&nbsp;Clawd/Moltbot, I need you to rethink things.&nbsp;To make&nbsp;this agentic platform&nbsp;act&nbsp;on&nbsp;your&nbsp;request and/or autonomously, you mustsurrender private information&nbsp;to&nbsp;an&nbsp;unvetted, unsecured agentic engine.&nbsp;Now, as a result,&nbsp;your logins, passwords,&nbsp;and more&nbsp;are&nbsp;<a href="https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/27/clawdbot_moltbot_security_concerns/" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>sitting in a plaintext file</u></a>,&nbsp;ripe for easy stealing.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>And then&nbsp;there’s&nbsp;the Skills.&nbsp;You can teach your&nbsp;wildly productive agent to do new things!&nbsp;Edit a spreadsheet!&nbsp;Write&nbsp;GPOs!&nbsp;Play a&nbsp;game of&nbsp;<a href="https://youtu.be/KXzNo0vR_dU?si=Od4a8Ld5LYrCFlHS" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>global&nbsp;thermonuclear war</u></a>!&nbsp;The sky is the limit.&nbsp;All it requires&nbsp;is you&nbsp;to&nbsp;give over&nbsp;complete&nbsp;system&nbsp;admin/root access to your&nbsp;Clawd&nbsp;agent.&nbsp;Just understand that&nbsp;Skills are unvetted and unsecured, and already are&nbsp;<a href="https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/researchers-find-341-malicious-clawhub.html" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>being actively exploited</u></a>.</p><p>As disciples of security, we understand installing first and asking questions later is&nbsp;practically asking to get&nbsp;pwnt.&nbsp;It has never panned out well for the end user, but usually&nbsp;<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2025/05/21/lumma-stealer-breaking-down-the-delivery-techniques-and-capabilities-of-a-prolific-infostealer/" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>quite well for attackers</u></a>&nbsp;who very much understand the threat landscape.&nbsp;Clawdbot&nbsp;is no exception. &nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>I&nbsp;need you to be highly skeptical of any AI tool rush.&nbsp;Do not be consumed by The Hype.&nbsp;Much like&nbsp;OpenAI’s&nbsp;Atlas, AI tools are being aggressively&nbsp;released to&nbsp;the market and installed, often with&nbsp;<a href="https://www.axios.com/2025/10/28/atlas-chatgpt-openai-web-browser-security-privacy" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>security vulnerabilities everywhere</u></a>.&nbsp;Resist the urge to throw yourself upon tools or platforms that have rushed to address a market need&nbsp;— they usually had&nbsp;no forethought about&nbsp;security, or&nbsp;just push an unreasonable assumption of risk on the end user.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>Security is being sacrificed on the altar of convenience, as AI outpaces our ability to secure it. Brothers and sisters,&nbsp;I’m&nbsp;not asking you to reject the future. AI is going to neat&nbsp;places.&nbsp;I’m&nbsp;asking&nbsp;you to guard yourself as you walk into it.&nbsp;</p><h2 id="the-one-big-thing">The one&nbsp;big thing&nbsp;</h2><p>In Talos’&nbsp;<a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/knife-cutting-the-edge/" rel="noreferrer noopener"><u>latest blog</u></a>, we share the discovery of "DKnife," a modular Linux-based attack framework that compromises routers and edge devices to intercept network traffic, steal credentials, and deliver malware. Active since at least 2019, DKnife can hijack legitimate software updates and bypass endpoint security, posing a significant risk to both users and organizations.&nbsp;</p><h3 id="why-do-i-care">Why do I care?&nbsp;</h3><p>DKnife can take over routers and edge devices, letting attackers spy on users, steal passwords, and install malware without being easily noticed. Because it can break through traditional antivirus defenses and target many types of devices, even networks with good security could be at risk if these gateway devices are not protected.&nbsp;</p><h3 id="so-now-what">So now what?&nbsp;</h3><p>Review and harden the security of routers, gateways, and other Linux-based edge devices.&nbsp;Audit&nbsp;for unauthorized firmware or binaries,&nbsp;make sure&nbsp;you’re&nbsp;enforcing&nbsp;strong authentication and certificate validation, and&nbsp;monitor&nbsp;for&nbsp;unusual traffic patterns or update behaviors.&nbsp;Implement network segmentation and make sure your devices are getting updates directly from trusted vendors.&nbsp;</p><h2 id="top-security-headlines-of-the-week">Top security headlines of the week&nbsp;</h2><p>You mean, other than the&nbsp;mess that is&nbsp;Clawdbot?&nbsp;Sorry, the first headline shows&nbsp;we’re&nbsp;not escaping that any time soon:&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Weaponized VS Code add-on</strong>&nbsp;<strong>ClawdBot</strong>&nbsp;<strong>sneaks in</strong>&nbsp;<strong>ScreenConnect</strong>&nbsp;<strong>RAT</strong>&nbsp;<br>Security researchers flagged a malicious VS Code extension named “ClawdBot&nbsp;Agent” on the Visual Studio Marketplace.     const coverageTable = document.getElementById('coverage-block');
    const sidebar = document.getElementById('side-bar');
    const postContent = document.getElementById('post-content');
    const currentWidth = window.innerWidth;  
  
    if (coverageTable && sidebar && postContent) {
      if (currentWidth < 1000) {
        // For viewport less than 1000px, append to post content
        postContent.appendChild(coverageTable);
      } else {
        // For viewport 1000px and wider, check for subscribe button
        const subscribeButton = sidebar.querySelector('.subscribe-button');
        
        if (subscribeButton) {
          // Insert coverage block after the subscribe button
          subscribeButton.insertAdjacentElement('afterend', coverageTable);
        } else {
          // No subscribe button found, prepend to top of sidebar
          sidebar.prepend(coverageTable);
        }
      }
    }
  }

  window.addEventListener("load", coverageView);
