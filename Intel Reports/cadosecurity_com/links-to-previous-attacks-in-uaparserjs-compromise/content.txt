 <!-- wp:paragraph --></p> 
<!--more--><p>A very popular npm library called <a href="https://www.npmjs.com/package/ua-parser-js">UAParser</a> was <a href="https://therecord.media/malware-found-in-npm-package-with-millions-of-weekly-downloads/">compromised</a> this week. The author of the library, Faisal Salman, said:</p> 
<p> 
 <!-- /wp:paragraph --></p> 
<p> 
 <!-- wp:paragraph --></p> 
<p><em>“I believe someone was hijacking my npm account and published some compromised packages (0.7.29, 0.8.0, 1.0.0) which will probably install malware.”</em></p> 
<p> 
 <!-- /wp:paragraph --></p> 
<p> 
 <!-- wp:paragraph --></p> 
<p>The compromised package installs a monero miner on Linux and Windows systems. Advisories are available from the package <a href="https://github.com/faisalman/ua-parser-js/issues/536">author</a>, <a href="https://github.com/advisories/GHSA-pjwm-rvh2-c87w">GitHub</a> and <a href="https://us-cert.cisa.gov/ncas/current-activity/2021/10/22/malware-discovered-popular-npm-package-ua-parser-js">CISA</a>.</p> 
<p> 
 <!-- /wp:paragraph --></p> 
<p> 
 <!-- wp:paragraph --></p> 
<p>When we analysed the malware - we found that clear links to earlier stages of the attack from an attacker named “wozheqirsplu”, described below.</p>
