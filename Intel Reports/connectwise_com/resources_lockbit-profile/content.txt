<h1 class="multilevel-header">Threat Profile: LockBit</h1>
            </div>

                    <div class="module__text module__text--left" style="color: #000000;" >
                <p>In the <a href="/resources/ebook-2022-msp-threat-report">2022 MSP Threat Report</a>, the CRU identified the top 5 ransomware threats targeting MSPs in 2021 and provided a brief description of each. This page includes supplemental material with a more detailed breakdown of the TTPs and suggested mitigation techniques.</p>
<h2>LockBit</h2>
<ul>
<li>Ransomware-as-a-service provider that first appeared in September 2019, originally dubbed "ABCD"</li>
<li>Known for their fast encryption, they claim to have the fastest encryption of any ransomware</li>
<li>Uses the double extortion method of encrypting files and threatening to leak stolen data</li>
</ul>
<h2>MITRE ATT&amp;CK<sup>&reg;</sup> mapping</h2>
<p><img src="/contentassets/0b814b9440ff47adabd8905bb4ca8355/lockbitluckydaylockbit_20abcd-2.svg" alt="LockBitLuckyDayLockBit_20ABCD (2).svg" width="768" height="384" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>LockBit TTPs and Mitigations</h2>
<h1>&nbsp;</h1>
<table border="1" width="942">
<tbody>
<tr>
<td width="138">
<p><strong>ATT&amp;CK Tactic</strong></p>
</td>
<td width="354">
<p><strong>ATT&amp;CK Technique</strong></p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><strong>Mitigations</strong></p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="138">
<p>Reconnaissance</p>
</td>
<td width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1595/001/">T1595.001</a> &ndash; Active Scanning &ndash; Scanning IP Blocks:<br />Adversaries may scan victim IP blocks to gather information that can be used during targeting. Public IP addresses may be allocated to organizations by block, or a range of sequential addresses.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1056/">M1056</a> &ndash; Pre-compromise:<br />This technique cannot be easily mitigated with preventive controls since it is based on behaviors performed outside of the scope of enterprise defenses and controls. Efforts should focus on minimizing the amount and sensitivity of data available to external parties.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="138">
<p>Resource Development</p>
</td>
<td width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1584/001/">T1584.001</a> &ndash; Compromise Infrastructure &ndash; Domains:</p>
<p>Adversaries may hijack domains and/or subdomains that can be used during targeting. Domain registration hijacking is the act of changing the registration of a domain name without the permission of the original registrant.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1056/">M1056</a> &ndash; Pre-compromise:<br />This technique cannot be easily mitigated with preventive controls since it is based on behaviors performed outside of the scope of enterprise defenses and controls. Efforts should focus on minimizing the amount and sensitivity of data available to external parties.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="19" width="138">
<p>Initial Access</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="6" width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1190/">T1190</a> &ndash; Exploit Public-Facing Application:<br />Adversaries may attempt to take advantage of a weakness in an Internet-facing computer or program using software, data, or commands in order to cause unintended or unanticipated behavior. The weakness in the system can be a bug, a glitch, or a design vulnerability.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1048/">M1048</a> - Application Isolation and Sandboxing:<br />Application isolation will limit what other processes and system features the exploited target can access.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1050/">M1050</a> - Exploit Protection:</p>
<p>Web Application Firewalls may be used to limit exposure of applications to prevent exploit traffic from reaching the application.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1030/">M1030</a> - Network Segmentation:</p>
<p>Segment externally facing servers and services from the rest of the network with a DMZ or on separate hosting infrastructure.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1026/">M1026</a> - Privileged Account Management:<br />Use least privilege for service accounts will limit what permissions the exploited process gets on the rest of the system.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1051/">M1051</a> &ndash; Update Software:<br />Update software regularly by employing patch management for externally exposed applications.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1016/">M1016</a> &ndash; Vulnerability Scanning:<br />Regularly scan externally facing systems for vulnerabilities and establish procedures to rapidly patch systems when critical vulnerabilities are discovered through scanning and through public disclosure.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="4" width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1133/">T1133</a> &ndash; External Remote Services:<br />Adversaries may leverage external-facing remote services to initially access and/or persist within a network.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1042">M1042</a> - Disable or Remove Feature or Program:</p>
<p>Disable or block remotely available services that may be unnecessary.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1035">M1035</a> - Limit Access to Resource Over Network:</p>
<p>Limit access to remote services through centrally managed concentrators such as VPNs and other managed remote access systems.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1032">M1032</a> - Multi-factor Authentication:</p>
<p>Use strong two-factor or multi-factor authentication for remote service accounts to mitigate an adversary's ability to leverage stolen credentials, but be aware of Two-Factor Authentication Interception techniques for some two-factor authentication implementations.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1030">M1030</a> - Network Segmentation:<br />Deny direct remote access to internal systems through the use of network proxies, gateways, and firewalls.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="5" width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1566/">T1566</a> &ndash; Phishing:</p>
<p>Adversaries may send phishing messages to gain access to victim systems. All forms of phishing are electronically delivered social engineering.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1049">M1049</a> - Antivirus/Antimalware:<br />Anti-virus can automatically quarantine suspicious files.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1031">M1031</a> - Network Intrusion Prevention:</p>
<p>Network intrusion prevention systems and systems designed to scan and remove malicious email attachments or links can be used to block activity.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1021">M1021</a> - Restrict Web-Based Content:</p>
<p>Determine if certain websites or attachment types (ex: .scr, .exe, .pif, .cpl, etc.) that can be used for phishing are necessary for business operations and consider blocking access if activity cannot be monitored well or if it poses a significant risk.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1054">M1054</a> - Software Configuration:</p>
<p>Use anti-spoofing and email authentication mechanisms to filter messages based on validity checks of the sender domain (using SPF) and integrity of messages (using DKIM). <p>Adversaries may exfiltrate data to a cloud storage service rather than over their primary command and control channel. Cloud storage services allow for the storage, edit, and retrieval of data from a remote cloud storage server over the Internet.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1021">M1021</a> - Restrict Web-Based Content:</p>
<p>Web proxies can be used to enforce an external network communication policy that prevents use of unauthorized external services.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="8" width="138">
<p>Impact</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="2" width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1486/">T1486</a> - Data Encrypted for Impact:</p>
<p>Adversaries may encrypt data on target systems or on large numbers of systems in a network to interrupt availability to system and network resources. They can attempt to render stored data inaccessible by encrypting files or data on local and remote drives and withholding access to a decryption key.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1040">M1040</a> - Behavior Prevention on Endpoint: On Windows 10, enable cloud-delivered protection and Attack Surface Reduction (ASR) rules to block the execution of files that resemble ransomware.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1053">M1053</a> - Data Backup: Consider implementing IT disaster recovery plans that contain procedures for regularly taking and testing data backups that can be used to restore organizational data. Ensure backups are stored off system and is protected from common methods adversaries may use to gain access and destroy the backups to prevent recovery. Consider enabling versioning in cloud environments to maintain backup copies of storage objects.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="2" width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1490/">T1490</a> - Inhabit System Recovery:</p>
<p>Adversaries may delete or remove built-in operating system data and turn off services designed to aid in the recovery of a corrupted system to prevent recovery. Operating systems may contain features that can help fix corrupted systems, such as a backup catalog, volume shadow copies, and automatic repair features. Adversaries may disable or delete system recovery features to augment the effects of Data Destruction and Data Encrypted for Impact.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1053">M1053</a> - Data Backup: Consider implementing IT disaster recovery plans that contain procedures for taking regular data backups that can be used to restore organizational data. Ensure backups are stored off system and is protected from common methods adversaries may use to gain access and destroy the backups to prevent recovery.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1028">M1028</a> - Operating System Configuration: Consider technical controls to prevent the disabling of services or deletion of files involved in system recovery.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="4" width="354">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1489/">T1489</a> - Service Stop:</p>
<p>Adversaries may stop or disable services on a system to render those services unavailable to legitimate users. Stopping critical services or processes can inhibit or stop response to an incident or aid in the adversary's overall objectives to cause damage to the environment.</p>
</td>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1030">M1030</a> - Network Segmentation: Operate intrusion detection, analysis, and response systems on a separate network from the production environment to lessen the chances that an adversary can see and interfere with critical response functions.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1022">M1022</a> - Restrict File and Directory Permissions: Ensure proper process and file permissions are in place to inhibit adversaries from disabling or interfering with critical services.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1024">M1024</a> - Restrict Registry Permissions: Ensure proper registry permissions are in place to inhibit adversaries from disabling or interfering with critical services.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td width="450">
<p><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/mitigations/M1018">M1018</a> - User Account Management: Limit privileges of user accounts and groups so that only authorized administrators can interact with service changes and service configurations.</p>
</td>
