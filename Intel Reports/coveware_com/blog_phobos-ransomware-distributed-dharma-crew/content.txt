      <h1 class="BlogItem-title" data-content-field="title">Phobos Ransomware, A Combo of CrySiS and Dharma </h1>
    

    <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1547818305232" id="item-5c41d3dcbba2233ca9080bed"><div class="row sqs-row"><div class="col sqs-col-2 span-2"><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1579034947169_122698"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-8 span-8"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-6c15ab28425a37aee45d"><div class="sqs-block-content">

<div class="sqs-html-content">
  <p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong><em>Updated 01/29/2019</em></strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><em>We are researching the possibility that the primary distribution group behind Phobos may have been disrupted by the recent </em><a href="https://www.coveware.com/blog/xdedic-marketplace-rdp-credentials-ransomware-attacks-taken-down" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline"><em>xDedic takedown</em></span></a><em>. At approximately the same time that xDedic was taken down, all known email addresses associated with Phobos attacks became disabled and inbound emails bounced. &nbsp;We are unsure at this time if the ransomware distributors themselves disabled their accounts, or if their email hosting provider disabled the accounts as part of the law enforcement action. &nbsp;We will further update this post as we learn more.</em>  </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong><em>Original Phobos Post below:</em></strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">A new strain of ransomware has been impacting businesses globally since mid December. &nbsp;The ransomware, dubbed Phobos by the distributors (possibly after the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phobos_(mythology)"><span style="text-decoration:underline">gree</span></a><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phobos_(mythology)" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">k</span></a><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phobos_(mythology)"><span style="text-decoration:underline"> god of fear</span></a>), shares both technical and operational similarities to <a href="https://www.coveware.com/blog/2018/11/26/why-new-dharma-ransomware-is-more-dangerous-than-ever" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">several recent Dharma variants</span></a>. &nbsp;The attack vectors being leveraged by Phobos distributors are well worn, open or <a href="https://www.coveware.com/blog/dont-become-a-ransomware-target-secure-rdp" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">weakly secured RDP ports</span></a>. As usual, the attacks are exacerbated when companies either have no backups, or have not properly partitioned them from the network with strong administrative controls. &nbsp;</p><h2 style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Rebranded Dharma Ransom Note and Same Encrypted File Extension Format</h2><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Most ransomware leaves behind an obvious ransom note so that the victim can find it and contact the hacker. Typically, these notes vary significantly depending upon the ransomware strain. However, both <a href="https://www.coveware.com/blog/remove-dharma-ransomware" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">Dharma</span></a> and <a href="https://www.coveware.com/phobos-ransomware-payment" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">Phobos</span></a> use the same ransom note. The only observable difference is that Phobos added a bit of branding to the top and bottom as seen in the below image. </p>
</div>

<div class="sqs-html-content">
  <p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Other than that, the text and composition is identical. &nbsp;The encrypted file name format is also the same as Dharma variants. It is constructed by concatenating the original file name, a unique ID number, hacker email, and the .phobos file extension.</p><h2 style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Emails Offer Security Advice when Paying for Decryption Keys</h2><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">When a victim of ransomware contacts the email address in the <a href="https://www.csoonline.com/article/3313330/ransomware/7-best-practices-for-negotiating-ransomware-payments.html?upd=1539870159312" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">ransom notice to negotiate</span></a>, the first response elicited is often a cut and paste standard response. The first response from Phobos is a verbatim match to first responses of several Dharma variants including .bip, .gamma, and .adobe. &nbsp;This group’s first responses are unique in that they offer a friendly ‘upsell’, in addition to extorting the victim for safe decryption of data. </p><h3 style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong><em>“we also offer service to you. full of advice for protecting against attacks? - the price of 0.1 BTC, and remember our work is very hard. and it requires a lot of time and costs.”</em></strong></h3><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">The above phrase is at the end of the first response email, and offers security advice for the low low price of 0.1 BTC. &nbsp;This phrase has been consistent across Dharma variants and Phobos. To our knowledge, no one has taken them up on this <span style="text-decoration:underline"><em>generous</em></span> offer. &nbsp;</p><h2 style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Technically, Phobos Ransomware is only Slightly Different from Dharma</h2><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Topically, Phobos appears to a largely cut+paste variant of Dharma.. However, from a technical perspective, Phobos carries some subtle differences from active Dharma variants. Both type of ransomware draw their lines from the <a href="https://www.mcafee.com/enterprise/en-us/threat-center/threat-landscape-dashboard/ransomware-details.crysis-ransomware.html" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">CySis ransomware family</span></a> and commonly used AV software will identify a Phobos executable sample as CrySis. The differences observed in a &nbsp;recent <a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/forums/t/688649/phobos-ransomware-help-topic-phobos-phoboshta/page-2" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration:underline">analysis</span></a> by <a href="https://twitter.com/demonslay335?lang=en"><span style="text-decoration:underline">@Demonslay335</span></a> note that the file marker structure of Phobos is significantly different from Dharma variants. What is clear is that while the ransomware type may be different, the group distributing Phobos, the exploit methods, ransom notes and communications remain the nearly identical to Dharma.</p>
