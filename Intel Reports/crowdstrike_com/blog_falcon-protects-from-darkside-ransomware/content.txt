<div class="cmp-text" id="text-6561b61ec8" data-cmp-name="cmp-text-" data-cmp-is="text" data-target-location="false">
    
       <span><ul><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The CrowdStrike Falcon® platform provides CrowdStrike clients with protection from DarkSide ransomware</span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">DarkSide is a ransomware as a service (RaaS) associated with an eCrime group tracked by CrowdStrike as CARBON SPIDER</span></li></ul>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Ransomware incidents such as the DarkSide attack that disrupted a major fuel pipeline — one that transports almost half of all fuel consumed on the East Coast of the United States — underscore the fragility of our critical infrastructure, and the magnitude of the problems faced by the public and private sector leaders who are charged with protecting it.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">“The vulnerability of infrastructure is significant to our national security and the operations of this country,” said Shawn Henry, CrowdStrike CSO and President of CrowdStrike Services, in an </span><a href="https://t.co/kTiPfoxtdq?amp&#61;1"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">interview for MSNBC</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">. “These organized crime groups have been making billions of dollars by extorting U.S. companies and global companies that have not been able to protect themselves from this debilitating ransomware.”</span></p>
<h2><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Who Was Behind the Pipeline Attack?</span></h2>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">DarkSide is associated with a criminal group tracked by CrowdStrike Intelligence as </span><a href="https://www.crowdstrike.com/adversaries/carbon-spider/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CARBON SPIDER</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">. Security researchers, customers and anyone interested in learning more about the technical tradecraft, the targeted verticals and the origin of CARBON SPIDER can explore the </span><a href="https://adversary.crowdstrike.com"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CrowdStrike Adversary Universe</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> for intelligence on all tracked adversaries. This information is consistently updated, and enterprises can use it to defend their organizations against some of the most persistent adversaries active now.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CARBON SPIDER first emerged in 2013 and is known to conduct several different financially motivated operations. In the summer of 2020, it began using ransomware and ultimately built and marketed its own ransomware as a service (RaaS), which it dubbed “DarkSide.”</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">DarkSide operators traditionally focused on Windows machines and have recently </span><a href="/en-us/blog/carbon-spider-sprite-spider-target-esxi-servers-with-ransomware/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">expanded to Linux</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">, targeting enterprise environments running unpatched VMware ESXi hypervisors or stealing vCenter credentials to log in to management consoles directly, to encrypt virtual machine files. This tactic further increases the scope of affected systems, placing additional pressure on victims to give in to ransom demands.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">DarkSide operators seemed to pride themselves on their ability to vet affiliates that match a strictly defined set of criteria and adhere to their code of conduct. While the eCrime group claimed in the past that it will not target medical, educational or government institutions, it is clear that either its vetting process is not strict or some affiliates may not share their beliefs. DarkSide ransomware operators to whom this incident was attributed </span><a href="https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/statement-suspected-us-pipeline-hackers-say-they-dont-want-cause-problems-2021-05-10.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">issued a statement</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> claiming their goals are strictly financially motivated, with no political affiliation or intent to cause societal problems.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">(According to Krebs on Security, the DarkSide ransomware affiliate program behind the pipeline attack later “announced it was closing up shop after its servers were seized and someone drained the cryptocurrency from an account the group uses to pay affiliates.”)</span></p>
<p> </p>
<h2><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Falcon vs. DarkSide: How CrowdStrike Protects Customers</span></h2>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The CrowdStrike Falcon® platform incorporates intelligence derived from continuous monitoring of the tactics, techniques and procedures of over 160 identified threat actors and numerous unnamed groups, enabling us to protect organizations from sophisticated attacks, including </span><b>DarkSide ransomware.</b> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CrowdStrike employs a layered approach when it comes to detecting malware, including machine learning as well as </span><a href="/en-us/cybersecurity-101/threat-intelligence/ioa-vs-ioc/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">indicators of attack (IOAs)</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">. As the screenshot below shows, the Falcon sensor is able to kill the ransomware process as soon as the file encryption behavior is seen.</span></p>
<div class="wp-caption" id="attachment_30125"><a href="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2021/05/DarkSide-Pic-1.png" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><img class="wp-image-30125 size-full" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2021/05/DarkSide-Pic-1.png" alt="" width="2960" height="874"/></a>(Click to enlarge)</div>
<p><a href="https://share.vidyard.com/watch/ii9Fp8FCJnKEdWoZPY9F2t?"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">This video demonstrates</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> how the DarkSide ransomware sample is immediately blocked and quarantined by Falcon upon execution. CrowdStrike’s machine learning engine is part of the Falcon agent and can protect the system online or offline. In addition to machine learning, CrowdStrike Falcon®’s built-in behavioral detection also identifies the rapid encryption of files and blocks the ransomware execution to protect the system.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CrowdStrike takes layered security to the next level by integrating machine learning and behavioral detection within a single lightweight agent to protect those systems critical to our customers.</span></p>
<h2><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Recommendations</span></h2>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Organizations that are being hit with DarkSide or other </span><a href="/en-us/cybersecurity-101/ransomware/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">ransomware</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> risk a significant impact to their business operations for a protracted period of time. Companies have to protect themselves, making sure they have the right technology in place and the right visibility into their environments so that they can disrupt these operations through their security infrastructure.</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The U.S. government, law enforcement and security companies understandably </span><a href="https://us-cert.cisa.gov/ncas/current-activity/2021/05/11/joint-cisa-fbi-cybersecurity-advisory-darkside-ransomware"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">recommend</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> that companies do not give in to ransomware and extortion demands. Unfortunately, public and private entities are sometimes stuck in a situation where they cannot reconstitute their environment, making an attack an existential threat to the organization.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Therefore, it’s vitally important for companies to protect themselves before an incident occurs, especially as ransomware is a prolific business that adversaries will continue to invest in.</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Protecting our customers against threats like DarkSide ransomware and sophisticated adversaries like CARBON SPIDER is something the CrowdStrike platform does every day. Organizations currently leveraging the </span><a href="/en-us/endpoint-security-products/falcon-platform/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;

<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Independent third-party validation also supports the strength of our platform. CrowdStrike Falcon® has recently been named a leader in the </span><a href="https://go.crowdstrike.com/2021-Gartner-Magic-Quadrant-Epp-Report.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;

">. In addition, CrowdStrike Falcon® has consistently proven its detection and protection capabilities in tests performed by leading independent testing organizations, such as MITRE, SE Labs, AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">By deploying the Falcon platform and following the recommended “1-10-60” benchmark time (one minute to detect an incident, 10 minutes to investigate and one hour to remediate), organizations will have the best available protection for their operations and data from ransomware attacks like DarkSide.</span></p>
