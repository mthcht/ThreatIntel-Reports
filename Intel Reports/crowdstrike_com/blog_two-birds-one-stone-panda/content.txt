        <h1>Two Birds, One STONE PANDA</h1>
        
        <div class="publish_info">
            <p>August 30, 2018</p>
            <span>|</span>
            <a href="/en-us/blog/author.kozy/" title="Posts by kozy" rel="author">
                kozy</a>
            <span>|</span>
                
            <a href="/en-us/blog/category.counter-adversary-operations/" title="Counter Adversary Operations">Counter Adversary Operations</a>
        </div>
        <div class="post_image">
            <img width="1060" height="698" src="https://assets.crowdstrike.com/is/image/crowdstrikeinc/Stone-panda-blog-1" class="attachment-post-thumbnail size-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="" decoding="async" fetchpriority="high" srcset="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-panda-blog-1.jpg 530w,https://assets.crowdstrike.com/is/image/crowdstrikeinc/Stone-panda-blog-1-300x198 300w" sizes="(max-width: 1060px) 100vw, 1060px"/>

    <div style="display: none;">

<div class="text text--blog-content aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12">

  

<div class="cmp-text" id="text-429fefb879" data-cmp-name="cmp-text-" data-cmp-is="text" data-target-location="false">
    
       <span><h3><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Introduction</span></h3>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">In April 2017, a previously unknown group calling itself </span><a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">IntrusionTruth</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> began releasing blog posts detailing individuals believed to be associated with major Chinese intrusion campaigns. Although the group’s exact motives remain unclear, its initial tranche of information exposed individuals connected to long-running GOTHIC PANDA (APT3) operations, culminating in a </span><a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com/2017/05/09/apt3-is-boyusec-a-chinese-intelligence-contractor.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">connection</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> to the Chinese firm Boyusec (博御信息) and, ultimately, Chinese Ministry of State Security (MSS) entities in Guangzhou.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Recently, in July and August 2018, IntrusionTruth has returned with new reporting regarding actors with ties to historic STONE PANDA (APT10) activity and has ultimately </span><a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com/2018/08/15/apt10-was-managed-by-the-tianjin-bureau-of-the-chinese-ministry-of-state-security.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">associated</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> them with the MSS Tianjin Bureau (天津市国家安全局). Though CrowdStrike® Falcon Intelligence™</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">is currently unable to confirm all of the details provided in these most recent posts with a high degree of confidence, several key pieces of information can be verified.</span></p>
<ul><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Several of the named individuals have been active registering domains as recently as June 2018, and they responded to the IntrusionTruth blog posts by scrubbing their social media or by following IntrusionTruth’s Twitter account.</span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Named individuals ZHANG Shilong</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">and GAO Qiang have significant connections to known Chinese hacking forums, and they have sourced tools currently in use by China-based cyber adversaries. </span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">ZHANG has registered several sites with overlapping registrant details that show both his affiliation with several physical technology firm addresses as well as his residence in Tianjin. </span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Named firm Huaying Haitai has been connected to a Chinese Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) sponsored attack and defense competition; this is similar to GOTHIC PANDA’s ties to an active defense lab sponsored by China Information Technology Evaluation Center (CNITSEC).</span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Huaying Haitai has previously hired Chinese students with Japanese language skills; this is significant, as STONE PANDA has engaged in several campaigns targeting Japanese firms. </span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The MSS Tianjin Bureau is confirmed to be located at the described address, not far from many of the registrant addresses listed by ZHANG as well the firms GAO was likely recruiting for.</span></li></ul>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">More details that may further illuminate these findings and provide a higher confidence in connecting STONE PANDA to the MSS Tianjin Bureau are likely to emerge.</span></p>
<h3><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Background</span></h3>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Throughout May 2017, using a variety of historical information and open-source intelligence (OSINT), IntrusionTruth released several blog posts identifying </span><a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com/2017/05/02/who-is-mr-wu.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">several</span></a> <a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com/2017/05/05/who-is-mr-dong.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">individuals</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> connected to Boyusec. Though CrowdStrike&#39;s <a href="/en-us/endpoint-security-products/falcon-x-threat-intelligence/">Threat Intelligence team</a> had suspected GOTHIC PANDA was an MSS contractor for several years, the IntrusionTruth posts and subsequent research by </span><a href="https://www.recordedfuture.com/chinese-mss-behind-apt3/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">RecordedFuture</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> into MSS ties to the China Information Technology Evaluation Center (CNITSEC/中国信息安全测评中心) corroborated additional details from various sources and provided a higher degree of confidence. Confidence in these findings was further boosted when the U.S. Department of Justice named Boyusec and several of the described individuals in an </span><a href="https://www.justice.gov/opa/press-release/file/1013866/download"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">indictment</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">, and detailed GOTHIC PANDA tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) in detail.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">CrowdStrike Falcon® Intelligence was able to independently verify the majority of this information and concluded that not only is CNITSEC associated with the MSS, but its former director WU Shizhong (吴世忠) was simultaneously dual-hatted as the director of the MSS Technology/13th Bureau (国家安全部科技局局长)<sup>1 2 3</sup>,</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">implying that the MSS plays a crucial role in China’s code review of foreign products and is now able to cherry pick high-value vulnerabilities from its own capable domestic bug hunting teams. CNITSEC’s role in code review for foreign entities has led to its access to Microsoft’s source code dating back to </span>2003<span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> and the use by KRYPTONITE PANDA of a high-value vulnerability (CVE-2018-0802), discovered by Chinese firm Qihoo 360, a month before it was publicly revealed.</span></p>
<div class="wp-caption" id="attachment_21230"><img class="wp-image-21230" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-1.png" alt="" width="587" height="444"/>WU Shizhong Presenting on the “Digital Silk Road” at the Second Wuzhen World Internet Conference in 2015</div>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">As research into the IntrusionTruth leads on STONE PANDA continues, Falcon Intelligence has already observed some consistencies with known MSS operations.</span></p>
<h3><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Sinking Like a STONE<p> </p>
<p> </p>
</span></h3>
<h4><b>GAO Qiang (高/郜 强)</b></h4>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Many of the personal details for GAO were scrubbed shortly after IntrusionTruth’s post </span><a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com/2018/08/02/who-is-mr-gao.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">introducing him</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> went live, including his<img class="wp-image-21232 alignright" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-2.png" alt="" width="156" height="173"/> Tencent QQ account. The blog connects him to the moniker </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">fisherxp</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> via an initial spear-phishing campaign from 2010 previously attributed to STONE PANDA. Multiple sites with profile pictures appear to show the owner of the </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">fisherxp </span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">accounts, though this has yet to be independently confirmed as GAO. </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Fisherxp</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">’s QQ shows his alternate username as 肥猪 or “big porker”. IntrusionTruth later links GAO to several </span><a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com/2018/08/15/apt10-was-managed-by-the-tianjin-bureau-of-the-chinese-ministry-of-state-security.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">documented Uber rides</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> to the MSS Tianjin Bureau’s office address where both his first name, Qiang/强, and 猪 are used by the app to identify him and tie him to the QQ number 420192. CrowdStrike cannot confirm the validity of these Uber receipts at this time.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">However, </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">fisherxp</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">’s account on popular Chinese technology forum 51CTO is still active and shows that he has downloaded not only the open-source </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">DarkComet</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> RAT and numerous password cracking tools, but more importantly, several favorite tools used by a plethora of known Chinese cyber adversaries including </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Gh0st RAT 3.6</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">, </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">zxarps</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> (an ARP-spoofing tool by legacy hacker LZX), and </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">lcx.exe</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> (a port-forwarding tool by legacy hacker LCX)<sup>4</sup>.</span></p>
<h4><b>ZHANG Shilong (张世龙)</b></h4>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">ZHANG was originally </span><a href="https://intrusiontruth.wordpress.com/2018/08/06/who-is-mr-zhang.html"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">introduced</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> by IntrusionTruth as a reciprocal follower of </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">fisherxp</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">’s Twitter account via his own &#64;baobeilong account. </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Baobeilong</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> (宝贝龙/”Baby Dragon”) also maintained a GitHub account that had forked both the </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Quasar<img class="wp-image-21233 alignright" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-3.png" alt="" width="164" height="190"/></span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> and </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Trochilus</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> RATs, two open-source tools historically used by STONE PANDA, but the account has since been scrubbed. This information was verified by CrowdStrike before being removed completely. Falcon Intelligence recently independently conducted detailed analysis of the </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">RedLeaves</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> malware used to target numerous Japanese defense groups and found it was directly sourced from Trochilus code, but it has undergone several evolutions and contains prefixes suggesting it could also be used to target Russia and the DPRK. There is no conclusive evidence at this time that RedLeaves is solely attributed to STONE PANDA.</span> <i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Baobeilong</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
"> did maintain a Flickr account with numerous pictures that proved key in identifying his location later, similar to how </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">cpyy</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">’s photos helped identify his affiliation to the People’s Liberation Army (PLA) in CrowdStrike’s </span><a href="/en-us/blog/hat-tribution-pla-unit-61486/"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">PUTTER PANDA report</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">IntrusionTruth then drew connections from </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">baobeilong</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">’s other online accounts to registrant details for </span><i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">xiaohong&lt;.&gt;org</span></i><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">, which dated back to 2007 and revealed ZHANG’s full name—ZHANG Shilong. From there, a trail of overlapping registrant details reveals ZHANG’s hanzi characters for his name (张世龙), likely one of his personal home addresses, potential work addresses and several email addresses:</span></p>
<ul><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">long&#64;xiaohong&lt;.&gt;org</span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">baobei&#64;xiaohong&lt;.&gt;org</span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">atreexp&#64;yahoo&lt;.&gt;com.cn </span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">robin4700&#64;foxmail&lt;.&gt;com</span></li><li style="	font-weight: 400;
"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">eshilong&#64;vip.qq&lt;.&gt;com<p> </p>
<p> </p>
</span></li></ul>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Specifically tracing registrant details from atreexp → robin4700 → eshilong shows that ZHANG was active registering sites as recently as June 5, 2018, including a personal blog where his picture and name features prominently along with several technology-related blog posts.</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<div class="wp-caption" id="attachment_21235"><img class="wp-image-21235" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-4.png" alt="" width="850" height="516"/>A picture from baobeilong’s Flickr account shows a fire at the Tianjin Medical Center 120</div>
<h4><b>Laoying Baichen Instruments</b></h4>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The original blog post on GAO lists his contact information in recruitment postings for two separate companies, one of which is Laoying Baichen Instruments (characters unknown at the time of this writing). No records could be found for such a firm, however, IntrusionTruth lists the address associated with it as Room 1102, Guanfu Mansion, 46 Xinkai Road, Hedong District, Tianjin (天津市河东区新开路46号冠福大厦1102).</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">During the course of investigating Laoying and the Guanfu mansion, Falcon Intelligence noticed that the Guanfu Mansion is also the registered address of a firm called Tianjin Henglide Technology Co., Ltd. (恒利德天津科技有限公司), which is listed as one of only a few “review centers” certified by CNITSEC in Tianjin<sup>5</sup>. Laoying and Henglide are listed as being on different floors, however having a CNITSEC review center in the same building is noteworthy given CNITSEC’s connection to MSS and previous linkage to Boyusec/GOTHIC PANDA.</span></p>
<div class="wp-caption" id="attachment_21234"><img class="wp-image-21234 size-full" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-5.png" alt="" width="974" height="679"/>Zhang is believed to have taken the photo of the fire from the Wanchan Meizhuan Mansion. This is relatively close to both the Yuyang Complex (one of Zhang’s listed registrant addresses) and the Guanfu Mansion, Laoying Baichen’s listed address.</div>
<h4><b>Tianjin Huaying Haitai Science and Technology Development Company</b></h4>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The other firm GAO appears to have been recruiting for is Huaying Haitai (天津华盈海泰科技发展有限公司). As the IntrusionTruth blog post mentions, it is a registered firm with two listed representatives, Fang Ting (方亭) and Sun Lei (孙杰), and a listed address of 1906 Fuyu Mansion (天津市河西区解放南路中段西侧富裕大厦1-1906).</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Searches for more information on Huaying Haitai turned up two interesting government documents. One is a recruitment Excel sheet detailing recent graduates, their majors and their new employers and addresses. Huaying Haitai is listed as having hired a recently graduated female student from Nankai University in 2013 who majored in Japanese. This is interesting considering STONE PANDA’s extensive targeting of Japanese defense firms after this time period, but it is by no means conclusive evidence that the firm is connected to STONE PANDA.</span> <img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21236" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-6.png" alt="" width="974" height="181"/> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The second government document lists Huaying Haitai as the co-organizer of a Network Security Attack and Defense competition with the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology’s (MIIT) national training entity, NSACE<sup>6</sup>. It was open for all students of Henan Province.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">NSACE appears to be a national education body that teaches network information security, including offensive activity<sup>7</sup>. This information is particularly interesting given Boyusec’s previous work at CNITSEC’s Guangdong subsidiary setting up a joint active defense lab<sup>8</sup>.</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p>It suggests that these technology firms act as both shell companies and recruitment grounds for potential MSS use in cyber operations. <span style="	font-weight: 400;
"><img class="size-full wp-image-21239 aligncenter" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-7-2.png" alt="" width="450" height="463"/></span></p>
<p> </p>
<h3><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">MSS Tianjin Bureau</span></h3>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">The most recent IntrusionTruth post assesses that GAO’s Uber rides frequently took him from Huaying’s address at the Fuyu Mansion to 85 Zhujiang Road (珠江道85号).</span> <img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21240" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-8.png" alt="" width="974" height="764"/> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">When observed closely, the compound is a striking one complete with towers, a fenced perimeter with surveillance cameras, guarded entrances, and a building with a significant number of satellite dishes.</span> <img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21241" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-9.png" alt="" width="974" height="645"/> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">There are no markers on the building and no government listed address; however, it is apparently difficult for locals to determine where the Tianjin Bureau’s location is as well. There are several Baidu questions asking what transportation routes are best to get to that specific address. Three separate ones specifically mention the 85 Zhujiang Road address as the headquarters for the MSS’s Tianjin Bureau and the difficulty in finding its location<sup>9 10 11</sup>.</span> <img class="alignnone wp-image-21242 aligncenter" src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-10.png" alt="" width="856" height="453"/> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">As with most cyber-enabled operations, satellite arrays are often indicative of installations with significant signals intelligence (SIGINT) capabilities. The Tianjin Bureau appears to have the potential for such capabilities, housing several large arrays that appear to have existed since at least January 2004.</span></p>
<div class="wp-caption" id="attachment_21259"><img class="wp-image-21259 " src="/content/dam/crowdstrike/www/en-us/wp/2018/08/Stone-Panda-fig-11-12-1024x311.png" alt="" width="942" height="286"/>Barely visible satellite dishes from the street view of 85 Zhujiang Road outside the compound</div>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">There are still significant intelligence gaps that prevent Falcon Intelligence from making an assessment about STONE PANDA’s potential connections to the MSS Tianjin Bureau with a high degree of confidence. However, additional information is likely to materialize either directly from IntrusionTruth or from other firms in the infosec community who are undoubtedly looking at this material as well and may have unique insight of their own. Ultimately, IntrusionTruth’s prior releases on GOTHIC PANDA proved accurate and led to a U.S. Department of Justice indictment resulting in the dismantling of Boyusec. From their latest post, which contains GAO’s Uber receipts, it is clear the group’s information likely goes beyond merely available OSINT data.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">It cannot be ignored that there are striking similarities between the entities associated with GOTHIC PANDA and the actors and firms mentioned in the blogs about STONE PANDA. In addition, FalconIntelligence notes that following the late 2015 Sino-U.S. brief cyber detente, much of the responsibility for western cyber intrusion operations was handed to the MSS as the PLA underwent an extensive reform that is still currently underway, and which is consolidating its military cyber forces under the Strategic Support Force.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Though the detente saw an initial drop in Chinese intrusion activity, it has steadily been increasing over the past several years, with a majority of the intrusions into western firms being conducted by suspected contractors. These adversaries are tracked by CrowdStrike as GOTHIC PANDA, STONE PANDA, WICKED PANDA, JUDGMENT PANDA, and KRYPTONITE PANDA. Many of these adversaries have begun targeting supply chain and upstream providers to establish a potential platform for future operations and enable the collection of larger sets of data.</span> <span style="	font-weight: 400;
">While the APT1, PUTTER PANDA, and Operation CameraShy reports all exposed PLA units at a time when Chinese military hacking against western firms was rampant, the attention has now swung toward identifying MSS contractors. The exposure of STONE PANDA as an MSS contractor would be another blow to China’s current cyber operations given STONE PANDA’s prolific targeting of a variety of sectors, and may prompt an additional U.S. investigation at a tenuous time for Sino-U.S. relations during an ongoing trade war. However, it is important to note that such public revelations often force these actors to cease operations, improve their operational security (OPSEC), and then return stronger than before. As such, CrowdStrike Falcon® Intelligence assesses that although Boyusec may have shuttered, elements of GOTHIC PANDA are likely to still be active. The same is likely to be true for STONE PANDA following a period of silence.</span></p>
<p> </p>

">Aerospace &amp; Defense</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">, </span><a href="https://falcon.crowdstrike.com/intelligence/actors?filter&#61;target_industries.value.raw%3A%27Government%27"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
">Government</span></a><span style="	font-weight: 400;

</span><a href="https://falcon.crowdstrike.com/intelligence/actors?filter&#61;target_industries.value.raw%3A%27Healthcare%27"><span style="	font-weight: 400;
