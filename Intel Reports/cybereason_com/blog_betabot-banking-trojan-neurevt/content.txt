      <h1><span id="hs_cos_wrapper_name" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="text">New Betabot campaign under the microscope</span></h1>
      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">
      <div id="hubspot-author_data" class="hubspot-editable cr-mln__post-meta" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author">
        
          <span class="descriptor">Written By</span>
          <p><span class="author">Cybereason Nocturnus</span></p>
         
      </div>
    </div>
     
    </div>
    
    <!-- Sticky Author and Social Box -->
    
    <!-- END Sticky Author and Social Box -->
      <div class="container-is-blog columns is-multiline page-center cr-mln__blog-post--body">
        
        <div class="column is-7-fullhd is-7-desktop is-10-tablet is-10-mobile is-offset-1-fullhd is-offset-1-desktop is-offset-1-tablet is-offset-1-mobile">
          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><h2>Research by: Assaf Dahan</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the past few weeks, the Cybereason SOC has detected multiple Betabot (aka </span><a href="https://www.virusbulletin.com/virusbulletin/2014/05/neurevt-botnet-new-generation" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Neurevt</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">) infections in customer environments. Betabot is a sophisticated infostealer malware that’s evolved significantly since it first appeared in late 2012. The malware began as a banking Trojan and is now packed with features that allow its operators to practically take over a victim’s machine and steal sensitive information.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Want to start threat hunting like the pros?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><!--HubSpot Call-to-Action Code --><span class="hs-cta-wrapper" id="hs-cta-wrapper-bd3d67f0-ef17-4381-acb0-a0f9a958ebc6"><span class="hs-cta-node hs-cta-bd3d67f0-ef17-4381-acb0-a0f9a958ebc6" id="hs-cta-bd3d67f0-ef17-4381-acb0-a0f9a958ebc6"><!--[if lte IE 8]><div id="hs-cta-ie-element"></div><![endif]--><a href="https://cta-redirect.hubspot.com/cta/redirect/3354902/bd3d67f0-ef17-4381-acb0-a0f9a958ebc6"><img class="hs-cta-img" id="hs-cta-img-bd3d67f0-ef17-4381-acb0-a0f9a958ebc6" style="border-width:0px;" src="https://no-cache.hubspot.com/cta/default/3354902/bd3d67f0-ef17-4381-acb0-a0f9a958ebc6.png" alt="Check out our webinar on how to generate a hypothesis in a threat hunt."></a></span><script charset="utf-8" src="/hs/cta/cta/current.js"></script><script type="text/javascript"> hbspt.cta._relativeUrls=true;hbspt.cta.load(3354902, 'bd3d67f0-ef17-4381-acb0-a0f9a958ebc6', {"useNewLoader":"true","region":"na1"}); </script></span><!-- end HubSpot Call-to-Action Code --></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Betabot’s main features include:</span></p>
<!--more-->
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Browsers </span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Form_grabbing" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Form Grabber</span></a></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">FTP and mail client stealer</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Banker module</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Running DDOS attacks</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">USB infection module</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Robust Userland Rootkit (x86/x64)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Arbitrary command execution via shell</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The ability to download additional malware </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Persistence</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Crypto-currency miner module (added 2017)</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Betabot exploits an 18-year-old vulnerability in the Equation Editor tool in Microsoft Office. The vulnerability has been around since 2000 when Equation Editor was added to Office. However, it wasn’t discovered by researchers and </span><a href="https://portal.msrc.microsoft.com/en-US/security-guidance/advisory/CVE-2017-11882" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">patched by Microsoft</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> until 2017.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most modern malware have self-defense features designed to bypass detection and thwart analysis. These features include anti-debugging, anti-virtual machine/sandbox, anti-disassembly and the ability to detect security products and analysis tools. It is not uncommon for malware to take a more aggressive approach and disable or uninstall antivirus software. Other programs remove malware and bots that are already on a person’s machine, eliminating the competition with heuristic approaches that would put many security products to shame.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image31-2.png?width=625&amp;name=image31-2.png" alt="image31-2" width="625" style="width: 625px; display: block; margin: 0px auto;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image31-2.png?width=313&amp;name=image31-2.png 313w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image31-2.png?width=625&amp;name=image31-2.png 625w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image31-2.png?width=938&amp;name=image31-2.png 938w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image31-2.png?width=1250&amp;name=image31-2.png 1250w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image31-2.png?width=1563&amp;name=image31-2.png 1563w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image31-2.png?width=1875&amp;name=image31-2.png 1875w" sizes="(max-width: 625px) 100vw, 625px"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Betabot stands out because it implements all of these self-defense features and has an exhaustive blacklist of file and process names, product IDs, hashes and domains from major antivirus, security and virtualization companies. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This blog will use Cybereason telemetry data gathered from multiple customer endpoints to look at the infection chain. We’ll also delve into Betabot’s self-defense mechanisms.</span></p>
<h2>Infection Vector: CVE-2017-11882 Exploit-Weaponized Document</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Betabot infections seen in our telemetry originated from phishing campaigns that used social engineering to persuade users to download and open what appears to be a Word document that is attached to an email.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This screenshot shows the infection vector from Lotus Notes email client:</span></p>

<h2>Anti-debugging</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Betabot uses several techniques to ensure that it’s not being debugged and to prevent debuggers from attaching to its process, such as: &nbsp;</span></p>

</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image17-6.png?width=643&amp;name=image17-6.png" alt="image17-6" width="643" style="width: 643px; display: block; margin: 0px auto;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image17-6.png?width=322&amp;name=image17-6.png 322w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image17-6.png?width=643&amp;name=image17-6.png 643w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image17-6.png?width=965&amp;name=image17-6.png 965w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image17-6.png?width=1286&amp;name=image17-6.png 1286w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image17-6.png?width=1608&amp;name=image17-6.png 1608w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image17-6.png?width=1929&amp;name=image17-6.png 1929w" sizes="(max-width: 643px) 100vw, 643px"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Instead of using the obvious </span><a href="https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/windows/desktop/ms680345(v=vs.85).aspx" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">IsDebuggerPresent</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> API, Betabot will use the segment register to query the PEB structure (Process Environment Block) by calling “fs:[30h]” and then looking for the BeingDebugged flag (0x02).</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Preventing debuggers to attach to the Betabot process by patching NTDLL.DLL’s </span><a href="https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-hardware/drivers/ddi/content/wdm/nf-wdm-dbgbreakpoint" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">DbgBreakPoint</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, by replacing the INT3 interrupt instruction (0x0CC) with NOP (0x90):<img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image21-4.png?width=600&amp;name=image21-4.png" alt="image21-4" width="600" style="width: 600px; display: block; margin: 0px auto;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image21-4.png?width=300&amp;name=image21-4.png 300w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image21-4.png?width=600&amp;name=image21-4.png 600w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image21-4.png?width=900&amp;name=image21-4.png 900w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image21-4.png?width=1200&amp;name=image21-4.png 1200w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image21-4.png?width=1500&amp;name=image21-4.png 1500w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image21-4.png?width=1800&amp;name=image21-4.png 1800w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"><span style="color: #252729; font-family: DIN-MediumCondensed, sans-serif; font-size: 40px; text-transform: uppercase; text-indent: -0.7em;"></span></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="color: #252729; font-family: DIN-MediumCondensed, sans-serif; font-size: 40px; text-transform: uppercase; text-indent: -0.7em;"></span></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="color: #252729; font-family: DIN-MediumCondensed, sans-serif; font-size: 40px; text-transform: uppercase; text-indent: -0.7em;"></span></span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="color: #252729; font-family: DIN-MediumCondensed, sans-serif; font-size: 40px; text-indent: -0.7em; text-transform: uppercase;">Detection of antivirus vendors</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Betabot will attempt to detect (and in some cases disable or remove) 30 different security products by looking for process names, specific files, folders, registry keys and services. Those products and vendors are: </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ahnlab v3 Lite, ArcaVir, Avast!, AVG, Avira, BitDefender (on minimal configuration), BKAV, BullGuard, Emsisoft Anti-Malware, ESET NOD32 / Smart Security, F-PROT, F-Secure IS, GData IS, Ikarus AV, K7 AntiVirus, Kaspersky AV/IS (older versions only), Lavasoft Adaware AV, MalwareBytes Anti-Malware, McAfee, Microsoft Security Essentials, Norman AntiVirus, Norton AntiVirus (Vista+ only), Outpost Firewall Pro, Panda AV/IS, Panda Cloud AV (free version), PC Tools AntiVirus, Rising AV/IS, Sophos Endpoint AntiVirus, Total Defense, Trend Micro, Vipre,Webroot SecureAnywhere AV, Windows Defender, ZoneAlarm IS</span></p>

<h2>Measures to Prevent Betabot Infections</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Here are some best practices to minimize the risk of infection:</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 16px;">1. Avoid clicking links and downloading or opening attachments from unknown senders.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 16px;">2. Look for misspellings, typos and other suspicious content in emails and attachments and report any abnormalities to IT or information security.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 16px;">3. Keep your software up-to-date and install Microsoft security patches, especially&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://portal.msrc.microsoft.com/en-US/security-guidance/advisory/CVE-2017-11882" rel="noopener" target="_blank">https://portal.msrc.microsoft.com/en-US/security-guidance/advisory/CVE-2017-11882</a></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 16px;">4. Consider disabling the Equation Editor feature in Microsoft Office by editing the following registry entries:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">[HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Office\Common\COM Compatibility\{0002CE02-0000-0000-C000-000000000046}]</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Compatibility Flags"=dword:00000400</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">[HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Wow6432Node\Microsoft\Office\Common\COM Compatibility\{0002CE02-0000-0000-C000-000000000046}]&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
