      <h1><span id="hs_cos_wrapper_name" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="text">Cybereason vs. Conti Ransomware</span></h1>
      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">
      <div id="hubspot-author_data" class="hubspot-editable cr-mln__post-meta" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author">
        
          <span class="descriptor">Written By</span>
          <p><span class="author">Cybereason Nocturnus</span></p>
         
      </div>
    </div>
     
    </div>

    <!-- END Sticky Author and Social Box -->
      <div class="container-is-blog columns is-multiline page-center cr-mln__blog-post--body">
        
        <div class="column is-7-fullhd is-7-desktop is-10-tablet is-10-mobile is-offset-1-fullhd is-offset-1-desktop is-offset-1-tablet is-offset-1-mobile">
          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p><a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.conti" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Conti </span></a>is a relatively new player in the ransomware field. Since first emerging in May 2020, the ransomware operators (aka. the Conti Gang) claim more than 150 successful attacks, which equates to millions of dollars in extortion fees.</p>
<!--more-->
<p>Like other ransomware syndicates that have emerged recently, the Conti gang follows the <a href="/blog/cybereason-vs.-conti-ransomware" rel="noopener" target="_blank">growing trend of double extortion</a>: they steal sensitive files and information from their victims and later use it to extort their victims by threatening to publish the data unless the ransom is paid.</p>
<h3 style="font-size: 36px;">Key Details</h3>
<p style="line-height: 1.5;"><strong>Emerging Threat</strong>: In a short amount of time, Conti ransomware has caused a great deal of damage and made headlines across the world.</p>
<p style="line-height: 1.5;"><strong>High Severity</strong>: The Cybereason Nocturnus Team assesses the threat level as HIGH given the destructive potential of the attacks</p>
<p style="line-height: 1.5;"><strong>Low-and-Slow: </strong>Prior to the deployment of the ransomware, the attackers attempt to infiltrate and move laterally throughout the organization, carrying out a fully-fledged hacking operation, or RansomOp.</p>
<p style="line-height: 1.5;"><strong>Rapid Development Cycle</strong>: In just a few months, the Conti gang has released 3 new versions of the ransomware, improving the malware in each version.</p>
<p style="line-height: 1.5;"><strong>The Successor of Ryuk:</strong> The Conti Gang collaborated with the TrickBot Gang, which are now using Conti as their ransomware of choice.</p>
<p style="line-height: 1.5;"><strong>Spreading across the network:</strong> Conti is not satisfied with causing damage to just the infected machines. Instead, it spreads in the network via SMB and encrypts files on remote machines as well.</p>
<p style="line-height: 1.5;"><strong>Detected and Prevented</strong>: <a href="https://www.cybereason.com/platform"><span>The Cybereason Defense Platform</span></a> fully detects and prevents the Conti ransomware.</p>
<div class="hs-embed-wrapper" data-service="fast.wistia" data-script-embed="true" data-responsive="true" style="position: relative; overflow: hidden; width: 100%; height: auto; padding: 0px; max-width: 658px; min-width: 256px; display: block; margin: auto; max-height: 370px;"><div class="hs-embed-content-wrapper"><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/embed/medias/5gokbjwtcb.jsonp" async>&nbsp;</script><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/assets/external/E-v1.js" async></script><div class="wistia_responsive_padding" style="padding:56.25% 0 0 0;position:relative;"><div class="wistia_responsive_wrapper" style="height:100%;left:0;position:absolute;top:0;width:100%;"><span class="wistia_embed wistia_async_5gokbjwtcb popover=true popoverAnimateThumbnail=true videoFoam=true" style="display:inline-block;height:100%;position:relative;width:100%">&nbsp;</span></div></div></div></div>
<p style="font-size: 18px;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="font-size: 18px;">Similar to ransomware such as <a href="https://www.cybereason.com/blog/cybereason-vs-egregor-ransomware"><span>Egregor</span></a> (“Egregor News”) and Maze (“Maze News”), the Conti Gang has their own website, “Conti News,” which stores a list of their victims, and it is where they publish the stolen data:</p>
<p style="font-size: 18px;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-1.png?width=846&amp;name=conti-blog-1.png" alt="conti-blog-1" width="846" style="width: 846px; display: block; margin: 0px auto;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-1.png?width=423&amp;name=conti-blog-1.png 423w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-1.png?width=846&amp;name=conti-blog-1.png 846w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-1.png?width=1269&amp;name=conti-blog-1.png 1269w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-1.png?width=1692&amp;name=conti-blog-1.png 1692w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-1.png?width=2115&amp;name=conti-blog-1.png 2115w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-1.png?width=2538&amp;name=conti-blog-1.png 2538w" sizes="(max-width: 846px) 100vw, 846px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Conti News website</em></p>
<p>Conti is a very destructive threat. Besides the double extortion that puts information and reputation at risk, the Conti operators equip it with a spreading capability, which means that Conti not only encrypts the files on the infected host but also spreads via SMB and encrypts files on different hosts, potentially compromising the entire network. The rapid encryption routine takes just a few seconds to minutes due to its use of multithreading, which also makes it very difficult to stop once the encryption routine starts.</p>
<p>Another major factor that contributes to the popularity of <a href="https://www.databreachtoday.com/trickbot-rebounds-after-takedown-a-15213"><span>Conti is the collaboration with the TrickBot Gang</span></a>. Conti is sold as a Ransomware-as-a-Service in underground forums to exclusive buyers and partners such as the TrickBot gang, which replaced Ryuk and adopted Conti as their new ransomware of choice.</p>
<p>In addition to the sophisticated capabilities and the collaboration with the TrickBot gang, the increased number of Conti attacks against big companies such as Advantech, which was extorted for $13.8M, and other attacks against big North American based companies as listed in <a href="https://www.blackfog.com/the-state-of-ransomware-in-2020/"><span>this article</span></a>, contributed to Conti making its way into the news this year. With a rapid development cycle that keeps the malware up-to-date and equipped with advanced capabilities, along with the promotion done by the TrickBot gang, it is no wonder why Conti is referred to as the successor of <a href="https://www.cybereason.com/blog/cybereason-vs.-ryuk-ransomware"><span>Ryuk</span></a>.</p>
<h3 style="font-size: 36px; font-weight: normal;">Breaking Down the Attack</h3>
<h2 style="font-size: 24px; line-height: 1.75;"><span style="color: #2d3236;">From Bazar Backdoor to Ransomware&nbsp;</span></h2>
<p>The TrickBot Gang was known to use their infamous TrickBot malware to start interactive hacking operations and deploying secondary payloads such as <a href="https://www.cybereason.com/blog/triple-threat-emotet-deploys-trickbot-to-steal-data-spread-ryuk-ransomware"><span>Ryuk</span></a> and <a href="https://www.cybereason.com/blog/dropping-anchor-from-a-trickbot-infection-to-the-discovery-of-the-anchor-malware"><span>Anchor</span></a>. Earlier this year, the group shifted to using the Bazar backdoor to launch an interactive attack and deploy Ryuk, and since July 2020 their new ransomware of favor has been Conti.</p>
<p>Although the payloads and tools of the TrickBot Gang have changed over time, the initial infection vector for the Bazar loader and backdoor has remained the same: a phishing email containing a link to Google Drive which stores the payload:</p>

<p>Conti uses a multithreading technique to fast encrypt all the files. This routine takes seconds to just a few minutes depending on the number of files on the machine. Each sample has a unique extension that the malware adds to the encrypted files. While using Cybereason with prevention mode off to allow investigation of the ransomware execution, it is possible to see the encryption activity and the creation of new files:&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-15.png?width=890&amp;name=conti-blog-15.png" alt="conti-blog-15" width="890" style="width: 890px; display: block; margin: 0px auto;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-15.png?width=445&amp;name=conti-blog-15.png 445w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-15.png?width=890&amp;name=conti-blog-15.png 890w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-15.png?width=1335&amp;name=conti-blog-15.png 1335w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-15.png?width=1780&amp;name=conti-blog-15.png 1780w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-15.png?width=2225&amp;name=conti-blog-15.png 2225w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/conti-blog-15.png?width=2670&amp;name=conti-blog-15.png 2670w" sizes="(max-width: 890px) 100vw, 890px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #859da6;">File Events feature in the Cybereason Defense Platform shows the encryption of the files</span></em></p>
<p>After the files are encrypted, the malware leaves the ransom note in every folder, making sure it is noticeable to the victim. The Conti Gang usually sets a deadline for the victim to pay the ransom, and if the deadline passes without payment, they leak the victim data on their website “Conti News.”</p>
