      <h1><span id="hs_cos_wrapper_name" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="text">Cybereason vs. Quantum Locker Ransomware</span></h1>
      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">
      <div id="hubspot-author_data" class="hubspot-editable cr-mln__post-meta" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author">

        <div class="column is-7-fullhd is-7-desktop is-10-tablet is-10-mobile is-offset-1-fullhd is-offset-1-desktop is-offset-1-tablet is-offset-1-mobile">
          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p>The Quantum Locker is a ransomware strain that was first discovered in July 2021. Since then, the ransomware was observed used in fast ransomware attacks, in some cases even Time-to-Ransom (TTR) of less than 4 hours, leaving defenders little time to react.</p>
<!--more-->
<h2 style="font-size: 24px;"><span style="color: #000000;">Key Details</span></h2>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Time-to-Ransom (TTR) of less than 4 hours</strong>: From initial infection to encryption takes even less than 4 hours, leaving a very short window for defenders to successfully defend against the threat.</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>High Severity</strong>: The <a href="/blog/authors/cybereason-nocturnus" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Cybereason Nocturnus Team</span></a> assesses the threat level as HIGH given the destructive potential of the attacks.</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Human Operated Attack: </strong>Prior to the deployment of the ransomware, the attackers attempt to infiltrate and move laterally throughout the organization, carrying out a fully-developed <a href="/blog/white-paper-inside-complex-ransomops-and-the-ransomware-economy" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><em><span>RansomOps </span></em></a>attack.</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Detected and Prevented</strong>: The <a href="/platform" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>AI-Driven Cybereason XDR Platform</span></a> fully detects and prevents the Quantum Locker.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<div class="hs-embed-wrapper" data-service="fast.wistia" data-script-embed="true" style="position: relative; overflow: hidden; width: 100%; height: auto; padding: 0px; max-width: 625px; max-height: 351px; min-width: 256px; display: block; margin: auto;"><div class="hs-embed-content-wrapper"><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/embed/medias/kw9mt6i4v7.jsonp" async>&nbsp;</script><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/assets/external/E-v1.js" async></script><div class="wistia_responsive_padding" style="padding:56.25% 0 0 0;position:relative;"><div class="wistia_responsive_wrapper" style="height:100%;left:0;position:absolute;top:0;width:100%;"><span class="wistia_embed wistia_async_kw9mt6i4v7 popover=true popoverAnimateThumbnail=true videoFoam=true" style="display:inline-block;height:100%;position:relative;width:100%">&nbsp;</span></div></div><script src="https://fast.wistia.net/assets/external/E-v1.js" async></script></div></div>
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 16px;"><em>Cybereason Blocks Quantum Locker</em></p>
<p>The Quantum ransomware is another rebranding of the notorious <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.mount_locker" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>MountLocker</span></a> ransomware, which launched back in September 2020. Since then, the ransomware gang has rebranded its operation to various names, including <a href="https://news.sophos.com/en-us/2021/03/31/sophos-mtr-in-real-time-what-is-astro-locker-team/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>AstroLocker</span></a>, <a href="https://thedfirreport.com/2021/10/18/icedid-to-xinglocker-ransomware-in-24-hours/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>XingLocker</span></a>, and now in its current phase, the Quantum Locker:</p>

<h2 style="font-size: 24px;"><span style="color: #000000;">Breaking Down the Attack</span></h2>
<h3 style="font-size: 20px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #434343;">Initial Infection Vector - IcedID</span></h3>
<p>The infamous malware, <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.icedid" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>IcedID</span></a>, that started as a banking trojan back in 2017, is observed being utilized as the initial access by various ransomware gangs. Among those gangs are <a href="https://www.techtarget.com/searchsecurity/news/252515561/Conti-ransomware-deployed-in-IcedID-banking-Trojan-attack" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Conti</span></a>, <a href="https://twitter.com/abuse_ch/status/1376570314348494850" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>REvil</span></a>, and the former brand of Quantum - <a href="https://thedfirreport.com/2021/10/18/icedid-to-xinglocker-ransomware-in-24-hours/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>the Xing Locker</span></a>. As for now, the gang seems to continue with this method with the Quantum Locker as well; “If it ain't broke don’t fix it.”</p>
<p><a href="https://www.intezer.com/blog/research/conversation-hijacking-campaign-delivering-icedid/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>The campaign of IcedID</span></a> observed ending in Quantum Locker execution starts with a phishing attack via email. The email contained an .iso image file that contains the IcedID loader payload in the form of a DLL (dar.dll) and shortcut file - an .LNK file - that targets the IcedID payload and masquerades as a document.&nbsp;</p>
<p>When mounting the .iso file, the end user only sees the shortcut file named “document”, and the DLL itself is hidden. After the user clicks on the shortcut, the IcedID DLL is executed:</p>

<h3 style="font-size: 20px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #434343;">Moving to an Interactive Attack</span></h3>
<p>The next phase of the attack starts after IcedID sends the reconnaissance output back to the C2. <a href="https://thedfirreport.com/2022/04/25/quantum-ransomware/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>In some cases</span></a>, it started just two hours after the user clicks on the .lnk file. In this phase, the threat actor starts an interactive attack in the breached network. To do so, they use the initial IcedID implant to download and execute another implant. In most cases the gang used Cobalt Strike beacon to launch the interactive phase.</p>
<p>First, the threat actor wants to perform additional and more in-depth reconnaissance activity. They execute a script named <em>adfind.bat</em> that uses the tool <a href="https://www.joeware.net/freetools/tools/adfind/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>AdFind</span></a> to collect information about the Active Directory. In addition, they also run a batch script named <em>ns.bat</em> which runs nslookup for each host in the domain.</p>
<p>The <em>AdFind.bat</em> script is dropped in the %temp% directory, along with the AdFind.exe binary and 7Zip binary named 7.exe.&nbsp; The output is saved into .txt files and sent to the C2. After that, the batch file removes tracks by deleting the script, the AdFind binary, the .txt files and the 7Zip binary:</p>
