      <h1><span id="hs_cos_wrapper_name" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="text">No Rest for the Wicked: Evilnum Unleashes PyVil RAT</span></h1>
      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">
      <div id="hubspot-author_data" class="hubspot-editable cr-mln__post-meta" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author">

          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p>Over the course of the last few months, the Cybereason Nocturnus team has been investigating the activity of the <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/js.evilnum"><span>Evilnum</span></a> group. The group first emerged in 2018, and since then, Evilnum’s activity has been varied, with <a href="https://www.welivesecurity.com/2020/07/09/more-evil-deep-look-evilnum-toolset/"><span>recent reports</span></a> using different components written in Javascript and C# as well as tools bought from the <a href="https://www.lastline.com/blog/malware-as-a-service-the-9-to-5-of-organized-cybercrime/"><span>Malware-as-a-Service</span></a> provider <a href="https://medium.com/@quoscient/golden-chickens-uncovering-a-malware-as-a-service-maas-provider-and-two-new-threat-actors-using-61cf0cb87648"><span>Golden Chickens</span></a>.</p>
<!--more-->
<p><span class="highlight">The group’s operations appear to be highly targeted, as opposed to a widespread phishing operation, with a focus on <a href="https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2020/07/163938-fintech-firms-in-the-uk-europe-australia-canada-being-targeted-through-malware-by-evilnum-an-online-threat-group-report/"><span>the FinTech market</span></a> by way of abusing the <a href="https://complyadvantage.com/knowledgebase/kyc/"><span>Know Your Customer regulations (KYC)</span></a>, documents with information provided by clients when business is undertaken.</span>&nbsp;Since its first discovery, the group’s mainly targeted different companies across the UK and EU.</p>
<p>In recent weeks, the Nocturnus team has observed new activity by the group, including several notable changes from tactics observed previously. These variations include a change in the chain of infection and persistence, new infrastructure that is expanding over time, and the use of a new Python-scripted Remote Access Trojan (RAT) Nocturnus dubbed PyVil RAT.&nbsp;</p>
<p>PyVil RAT possesses different functionalities, and enables the attackers to exfiltrate data, perform keylogging and the taking of screenshots, and the deployment of more tools such as <a href="https://github.com/AlessandroZ/LaZagne"><span>LaZagne</span></a> in order to steal credentials.</p>
<p>In this write-up, we dive into the recent activity of the Evilnum group and explore its new infection chain and tools.</p>
<a id="key-findings" data-hs-anchor="true"></a>
<h3>Key Findings</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Evilnum</strong>: The Cybereason Nocturnus team is tracking the operations of the Evilnum group, which has been active for the past two years, using a variety of tools.</li>
<li><strong>Targeting the Financial Sector</strong>: The group is known to <a href="https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2020/07/163938-fintech-firms-in-the-uk-europe-australia-canada-being-targeted-through-malware-by-evilnum-an-online-threat-group-report/"><span>target FinTech companies</span></a>, and is abusing the usage of the <a href="https://complyadvantage.com/knowledgebase/kyc/"><span>Know Your Customer( KYC)</span></a> procedure in order to start the infection.</li>
<li><strong>New Tricks</strong>: In this research, we see a deviation from the infection chain, persistence, infrastructure, and tools observed previously, including:</li>
<ul style="font-size: 18px;">
<li><strong>Modified versions of legitimate executables</strong> employed in an attempt to remain undetected by security tools.</li>
<li><strong>Infection chain shift</strong> from a JavaScript Trojan with backdoor capabilities to a multi-process delivery procedure of the payload.&nbsp;</li>
<li><strong>A newly discovered Python-scripted RAT</strong> dubbed <em>PyVil RAT</em> that was compiled with py2exe, which has the capability to download new modules to expand functionality.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
</ul>

<a id="overview-of-the-group" data-hs-anchor="true"></a>
<h3>Overview of the Group</h3>
<p>The Evilnum group has been reported to <a href="https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2020/07/163938-fintech-firms-in-the-uk-europe-australia-canada-being-targeted-through-malware-by-evilnum-an-online-threat-group-report/"><span>target financial technology companies</span></a>, mostly located in the UK and other EU countries. The main goal of the group is to spy on its infected targets and steal information such as passwords, documents, browser cookies, email credentials and more.</p>
<p>Aside from the group’s own proprietary tools, Evilnum has been observed deploying <em>Golden Chickens</em> tools in some cases, <a href="https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/REXBtbnI2zXj4H3u6ofMMw"><span>as reported in the past</span></a>. <a href="https://medium.com/@quoscient/golden-chickens-uncovering-a-malware-as-a-service-maas-provider-and-two-new-threat-actors-using-61cf0cb87648"><span>Golden Chickens</span></a> is a Malware-as-a-Service (MaaS) provider that is known to have been used by groups such as FIN6 and Cobalt Group. Among the tools used by the Evilnum group are <a href="https://attack.mitre.org/software/S0284/"><span>More_eggs</span></a>, <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.terrapreter"><span>TerraPreter</span></a>, <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.terra_stealer"><span>TerraStealer</span></a>, and <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.terra_tv"><span>TerraTV</span></a>.</p>
<p>The Evilnum group’s activity was <span>first identified</span> in 2018, when they used the first version of their infamous JavaScript Trojan. The script extracts C2 addresses from sites like GitHub, DigitalPoint and Reddit by querying specific pages created for this purpose. This technique enables the attackers to change the C2 address of deployed agents easily while keeping the communications masked as requests are made to legitimate known sites.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Since then, the group has been <a href="https://blog.prevailion.com/2020/05/phantom-in-command-shell5.html"><span>mentioned </span></a>several times, in different attacks, each time upgrading its toolset with new capabilities as well as adding new tools to the group’s arsenal.</p>
<p>The initial infection vector of Evilnum typically begins with spear phishing emails, with the goal of delivering ZIP archives that contain <a href="https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/REXBtbnI2zXj4H3u6ofMMw"><span>LNK </span></a>files masquerading as photos of different documents such as driving licenses, credit cards, and utility bills. These documents are likely to be stolen and belong to real individuals.</p>
<p>Once an LNK file is opened, it deploys the JavaScript Trojan, which in turn replaces the LNK file with a real image file, making this whole operation invisible to the user.</p>
<p>Up to this date, as described in this<a href="https://www.welivesecurity.com/2020/07/09/more-evil-deep-look-evilnum-toolset/"><span> publication</span></a>, six different iterations of the JavaScript trojan have been observed in the wild, each with small changes that don’t alter the core functionality. The JavaScript agent has functionalities such as upload and download files, steal cookies, collect antivirus information, execute commands and more.&nbsp;</p>
<p>In addition to the JavaScript component, as described in a previous <a href="https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/cardinal-rat-sins-again-targets-israeli-fin-tech-firms/"><span>research</span></a>, the group has been observed deploying a C# Trojan, that possesses similar functionality to the former JavaScript component.</p>

<a id="conclusion" data-hs-anchor="true"></a>
<h3>Conclusion</h3>
<p>In this write-up, we examined a new infection chain by the Evilnum group - threat actors who have started to make a name for themselves. Since the first reports in 2018 through today, the group’s TTPs have evolved with different tools while the group has continued to focus on&nbsp; FinTech targets.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Evilnum group employed different types of tools along its career, including JavaScript and C# Trojans, malware bought from the <a href="https://www.lastline.com/blog/malware-as-a-service-the-9-to-5-of-organized-cybercrime/"><span>malware-as-a-service</span></a> <a href="https://medium.com/@quoscient/golden-chickens-uncovering-a-malware-as-a-service-maas-provider-and-two-new-threat-actors-using-61cf0cb87648"><span>Golden Chickens</span></a>, and other existing Python tools. With all these different changes, the primary method of gaining initial access to their FinTech targets stayed the same: using fake <a href="https://complyadvantage.com/knowledgebase/kyc/"><span>Know your customer (KYC)</span></a> documents to trick employees of the finance industry to trigger the malware.&nbsp;</p>
<p>In recent weeks we observed a significant change in the infection procedure of the group, moving away from the JavaScript backdoor capabilities, instead utilizing it as a first stage dropper for new tools down the line. During the infection stage, Evilnum utilized modified versions of legitimate executables in an attempt to stay stealthy and remain undetected by security tools.</p>
<p>The group deployed a new type of Python RAT that Nocturnus researchers dubbed PyVil RAT which possesses abilities to gather information, take screenshots, keylog data, open an SSH shell and deploy new tools. These tools can be a Python module such as LaZagne or an executable, and thus adding more functionality for the attack as required. This innovation in tactics and tools is what allowed the group to stay under the radar, and we expect to see more in the future as the Evilnum group’s arsenal continues to grow.</p>
