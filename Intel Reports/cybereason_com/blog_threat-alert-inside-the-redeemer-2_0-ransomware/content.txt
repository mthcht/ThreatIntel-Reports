          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p><span style="color: #666666;">The </span><a href="/blog/authors/cybereason-global-soc-team" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Cybereason Global Security Operations Center (SOC) Team</span></a><span style="color: #666666;"> issues </span><a href="/blog/category/research" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Cybereason Threat Alerts</span></a><span style="color: #666666;"> to inform customers of emerging impacting threats. <!--more-->
<h2><span style="color: #666666; font-size: 30px;">What's Happening?</span></h2>
<p><span style="color: #666666;">The malware dubbed Redeemer 2.0 is an updated variant of the Redeemer ransomware. This version differs from its older variants as it:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Infects machines running the Windows 11 Operating Systems (OS).&nbsp;</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Keeps the OS safe from unintended damage (outside of the file encryption).</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Changes the icons of encrypted files</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="color: #666666;">In this article, Cybereason Security Research Team analyzed the new release of this ransomware and how to defend against it through the </span><a href="/platform#graphic" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Cybereason Defense Platform</span></a><span style="color: #666666;">.</span></p>
<h2><span style="color: #666666; font-size: 30px;">Key Observations</span></h2>
<ul>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">In July 2022, a new version of Redeemer (v2.0) was released on an underground forum</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">The new variant is advertised as “easy-to-use” and supports Windows 11</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">The </span><a href="/platform/xdr" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Cybereason Defense Platform</span></a><span style="color: #666666;"> includes Anti-Ransomware and Anti-Malware capabilities that detects and prevents the ransomware payload execution.</span></li>
</ul>
<h2><span style="color: #666666;">Analysis</span></h2>
<h3 style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #434343; font-size: 24px;">Redeemer 2.0 Toolkit Release</span></h3>
<p><span style="color: #666666;">A new and improved Redeemer 2.0 ransomware version was released on an underground forum. The author, calling himself “Cerebrate”, describes the new version as a “C++ no dependency ransomware with no privacy intrusions”, targeting the Windows OS:</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png?width=1707&amp;name=image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png" alt="image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM" width="1707" loading="lazy" style="width: 1707px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png?width=854&amp;name=image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png 854w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png?width=1707&amp;name=image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png 1707w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png?width=2561&amp;name=image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png 2561w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png?width=3414&amp;name=image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png 3414w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png?width=4268&amp;name=image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png 4268w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png?width=5121&amp;name=image4-Aug-18-2022-06-01-00-24-PM.png 5121w" sizes="(max-width: 1707px) 100vw, 1707px"><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #666666;">Underground forum screenshot</span></em></span></p>
<h3 style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #434343; font-size: 24px;">Redeemer Toolkit and Build</span></h3>
<p><span style="color: #666666;">The Redeemer 2.0 ransomware build can be generated using the toolkit as shown in this video:</span></p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #666666;">Redeemer 2.0 Ransomware Builder</span></em></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #666666;">The build of the ransomware copies itself into the Windows directory with legitimate file names and executes itself as a new process, for example </span><em><span style="color: #666666;">sqlserver1.exe, svchost.exe</span></em><span style="color: #666666;">, etc.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #666666;">The Cybereason Defense Platform Anti-Malware capability detects and prevents the ransomware execution:</span></p>

<h2><span style="color: #666666; font-size: 30px;">Cybereason Recommendations</span></h2>
<p><span style="color: #666666;">The </span><a href="/platform#graphic" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Cybereason Defense Platform</span></a><span style="color: #666666;"> detects and prevents Redeemer 2.0 infections through the Anti-ransomware feature. Cybereason recommends the following:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Enable Anti-Malware and set the Anti-Malware &gt; Signatures mode to Prevent, Quarantine, or Disinfect</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Enable Anti-Ransomware, set Anti-Ransomware to Prevent mode and enable canary files (with default settings)</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Enable Application Control</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Keep systems fully patched: Make sure your systems are patched in order to mitigate vulnerabilities</span></li>
<li style="color: #666666;" aria-level="1"><span style="color: #666666;">Regularly backup files and create a backup process and policy : Restoring your files from a backup is the fastest way to regain access to your data</span></li>
</ul>
