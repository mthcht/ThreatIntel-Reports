<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-is-an-incident-response"><strong>What is an Incident Response?</strong></h2>



<p>Incident response refers to an organization’s strategy for responding to and managing a cyberattack. </p>



<p>A cyberattack or security violation may lead to chaos, copyright claims, a drain on overall organizational resources and time, and a decline in brand value.</p>



<p>An incident response aims to mitigate damage and speedily return to normalcy.</p>



<p> A well-defined incident response plan can restrict attack damage and save money and time after a cyber attack.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-why-are-incident-response-tools-important"><strong>Why are Incident Response Tools Important?</strong></h2>



<p>Incident response manages the repercussions of an IoT security breach or failure. </p>



<p>It is crucial to have a response procedure in place before an incident occurs. This will reduce the amount of damage the event causes and save the organization time and money during the recovery process.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-incident-response-phases"><strong>Incident Response Phases</strong></h2>



<p>The incident response methods are based on six important steps: <strong>preparation, identification, containment, eradication, recovery, and lesson.</strong></p>



<figure class="wp-block-table"><table><tbody><tr><td><strong>Incident Response Phases</strong></td><td><strong>How to Respond</strong></td></tr><tr><td><strong>Preparation</strong></td><td>This will require figuring out the exact members of the response team and the stimulates for internal partner alerts.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Identification</strong></td><td>&nbsp;This is the process of finding threats and responding effectively and quickly.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Containment</strong></td><td>After figuring out what to do, the third step is to limit the damage and stop it from spreading.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Eradication</strong></td><td>This step entails eliminating the threat and restoring internal systems as precisely as possible to their initial state.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Recovery</strong></td><td>Security experts must ensure that all compromised systems are no longer risky and can be put back online.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Lesson</strong></td><td>One of the most important and often forgotten steps. The incident response team and its partners get together to talk about how to improve their work in the future.</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>
