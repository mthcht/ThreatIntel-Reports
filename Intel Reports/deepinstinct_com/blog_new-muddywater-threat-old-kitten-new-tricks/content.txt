Mercury, is a cyber espionage group that’s most likely a subordinate element within Iran&#x27;s Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS).</p><p>Since at least 2017 MuddyWater has targeted a range of government and private organizations across sectors, including telecommunications, local government, defense, and oil and natural gas organizations, in the Middle East, Asia, Africa, Europe, and North America.</p><p>MuddyWater has various campaigns that are entirely different from each other. In this post we will focus on the most recent changes and observations of their campaign which utilizes spearphishing with legitimate remote administration tools.</p><h3>Executive summary:</h3><ul><li>Deep Instinct’s Threat Research team has identified a new campaign of the MuddyWater group.</li><li>The campaign has been observed targeting Armenia, Azerbaijan, Egypt, Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Oman, Qatar, Tajikistan, and United Arab Emirates.</li><li>The campaign exhibits updated TTPs to previously reported MuddyWater activity.</li></ul><p></p><figure style="margin:auto;text-align:center;width:autopx"><img asset_uid="blt080f6f4279204a12" alt="Figure 1: Campaign overview" overview</figcaption></figure><p></p><h3>MuddyWater Exploiting Legitimate Tools</h3><p><a href="https://www.trendmicro.com/en_us/research/21/c/earth-vetala---muddywater-continues-to-target-organizations-in-t.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Previous</a> research has shown that in 2020 MuddyWater sent spearphishing emails with direct links as well as PDF and RTF attachments containing links to archives hosted at “ws.onehub.com.”</p><p>Those archives contained the installer for “RemoteUtilities,” a legitimate remote administration tool.</p><p>Since the beginning of 2021, MuddyWater has been <a href="https://www.anomali.com/blog/probable-iranian-cyber-actors-static-kitten-conducting-cyberespionage-campaign-targeting-uae-and-kuwait-government-agencies" target="_blank" installers for ScreenConnect, another legitimate remote administration tool.</p><p>This activity was <a href="https://urlscan.io/result/2d5e994b-6c2b-462d-8ed1-5946c0799767/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">observed</a> intermittently through the end of <a href="https://app.any.run/tasks/7b4bddb7-35d1-4546-9516-3c59c3b73575/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">2021</a> and until July <a href="https://app.any.run/tasks/45f48fa1-5b39-4f80-9ea1-11d8d12aac9b/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">2022</a>.</p><p>In July 2022 a <a href="https://twitter.com/k3yp0d/status/1543883858294210560" target="_blank" rel="noopener">potential</a> file related to this campaign was observed, but it contained Atera Agent instead of the usual ScreenConnect, potentially signaling the threat actor switched to another remote administration tool to avoid detection of their long running campaign.</p><h3>A new discovery: The current MuddyWater campaign</h3><p>The most recent MuddyWater campaign was observed by Deep Instinct in the beginning of October and <a href="https://www.virustotal.com/gui/file/76ab046de18e20fd5cddbb90678389001361a430a0dc6297363ff10efbcb0fa8" target="_blank" href="https://syncromsp.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Syncro</a>.”￼</p><p>A new lure in the form of an HTML attachment was observed, along with the addition of other providers for hosting the archives containing the installers of the remote administration tool.</p><p>The previous July sample with ScreenConnect mentioned earlier, was named “promotion.msi.”</p><p>In the current campaign there was a sample that had few names; one of them was also “promotion.msi.”</p><p>The above ScreenConnect sample was communicating with “instance-q927ui-relay.screenconnect.com.” This instance was communicating with another MuddyWater MSI installer named “<a href="https://www.virustotal.com/gui/file/01e924ae71e12e629d09f2867fa1768ed40fa4dfc810f33f7caae7025dd0e25d" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ertiqa.msi</a>” which is a name of a Saudi <a href="https://ertiqa.org/index.php/en/about-us" target="_blank" rel="noopener">organization</a>.</p><p>In the current wave, MuddyWater used the same name “<a href="https://www.virustotal.com/gui/file/eae0acba9c9e6a93ce2d5b30a5f21515e8ccca0975fbd0e7d8862964fdfa1468" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ertiqa.msi</a>,” but with Syncro installer.</p><p>The target geolocations and sectors also align with previous targets of MuddyWater. It has also been observed recently in <a href="https://twitter.com/1ZRR4H/status/1575364101148114944" target="_blank" rel="noopener">BatLoader</a> and <a href="https://blog.sygnia.co/luna-moth-false-subscription-scams" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Luna Moth</a> campaigns.</p><p>Syncro is a fully-featured platform for Managed Service Provider’s (MSPs) to run their business.</p><p></p><figure style="margin:auto;text-align:center;width:autopx"><img asset_uid="bltf8d3a7d2658f9f42" alt="Figure 9: Sycnro Installer inside the MSI" src="https://www.deepinstinct.com/image/bltf8d3a7d2658f9f42/63912c8e2851885ebf746b42/fig09-syncro-installer-inside-msi.png"/><figcaption style="text-align:center">Figure 9: Sycnro Installer inside the MSI</figcaption></figure><p></p><p>Syncro provides an agent for MSPs to manage any device that has Syncro installed with the custom-made provided MSI file that includes the customerID.</p><p></p><figure style="margin:auto;text-align:center;width:autopx"><img asset_uid="bltccdf1d678053e120" src="https://www.deepinstinct.com/image/bltccdf1d678053e120/63912c8ec32187107ea7f846/fig10.1-syncro-installation-process-with-customerid-apikey.png"/></figure><figure style="margin:auto;text-align:center;width:autopx"><img asset_uid="blt4694685e2d226cbc" alt="Figure 10: Syncro installation process with the customerID and ApiKey" src="https://www.deepinstinct.com/image/blt4694685e2d226cbc/63912ca093e8785fc5c81511/fig10.2-syncro-installation-process-with-customerid-apikey.png"/><figcaption style="text-align:center">Figure 10: Syncro installation process with the customerID and ApiKey</figcaption></figure><p></p><p>Syncro has a 21-day trial offer. You choose the subdomain to be used by your MSP:</p><p></p><figure style="margin:auto;text-align:center;width:autopx"><img asset_uid="blta4890bbb17a35e55" alt="Figure 11: Syncro trial sign-up screen with choice of syncromsp.com subdomain" src="https://www.deepinstinct.com/image/blta4890bbb17a35e55/63912ca0b6458e671e0c0fad/fig11-syncro-trial-sign-up-screen.png"/><figcaption style="text-align:center">Figure 11: Syncro trial sign-up screen with choice of syncromsp.com subdomain</figcaption></figure><p></p><p>While investigating some of the installers that MuddyWater used, we see that for each unique mail a new MSI was used. In most cases MuddyWater used a single subdomain with a single MSI installer.</p><p>It seems that most of the subdomains don’t have any useful meaning, although a few are clear:</p><ul><li>mohammadosman6060 and osmandembele4040 are football players</li><li>netanyahu8585 and benet5050 are the current and former prime ministers of Israel</li><li>Cham Wings is the name of a Syrian airline</li></ul><p>The trial version contains the fully featured web GUI which allows complete control over a computer with the Syncro agent installed:</p><p></p><figure style="margin:auto;text-align:center;width:autopx"><img asset_uid="blt699b750f27b64ddb" alt="Figure 12: Web GUI of Syncro with available remote administration features" src="https://www.deepinstinct.com/image/blt699b750f27b64ddb/63912ca0c32187107ea7f84a/fig12-web-gui-syncro-available-remote-admin-feature.png"/><figcaption style="text-align:center">Figure 12: Web GUI of Syncro with available remote administration features</figcaption></figure><p></p><p>Those features are standard for remote administration tools, such as terminal with SYSTEM privileges, remote desktop access, full file system access, tasks, and services manager.</p><p>All those features combined with a signed MSI installer creates the perfect weapon for a threat actor to gain initial access and start performing recon on the target. Later, they enable the threat actors to deploy additional backdoors, exfiltrate files, or hand-off access to other threat actors. A threat actor that has access to a corporate machine via such capabilities has nearly limitless options.</p><h3>Recommendations:</h3><p>We have recently <a href="https://www.deepinstinct.com/blog/the-russian-spyagent-a-decade-later-and-rat-tools-remain-at-risk" target="_self">described</a> other dual-use tools that are being abused for malicious purposes. We recommend that security teams monitor for remote desktop solutions that are not common in the organization as they have a higher chance of being abused.</p><p></p><h3>MITRE
