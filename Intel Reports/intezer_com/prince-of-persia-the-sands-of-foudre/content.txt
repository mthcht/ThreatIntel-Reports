<h3>Get Free Account</h3><div class="join-btn">Join Now</div></a></div></div></div><div class="col-md-9 blog-main"><div class="single-post-content"><h4><strong>Introduction</strong></h4>
<p>In the past couple years, Palo Alto Networks reported on the “Prince of Persia” malware campaign which is believed to be of Iranian origin and ongoing for more than 10 years. The original research, published in 2016, called the malware <a href="https://researchcenter.paloaltonetworks.com/2016/05/prince-of-persia-infy-malware-active-in-decade-of-targeted-attacks/">Infy</a> and their second report, published in 2017, named the upgraded malware <a href="https://researchcenter.paloaltonetworks.com/2017/08/unit42-prince-persia-ride-lightning-infy-returns-foudre/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">Foudre</a>. The name &#8220;Foudre&#8221; comes from a string in the binary used to check if the computer is already infected. At the time of their blog post, Palo Alto Networks stated the version of Foudre they observed were versions 1 and 2. We have found new evidence of the Prince of Persia campaign active by finding a new version of the Foudre malware, version 8.</p>
<p>In this blog post, we are only going to focus on the new, unique, interesting features of the new version of Foudre, and its related campaign</p>
<p><a href="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_version_8.png" data-slb-group="post-images" data-slb-active="1" data-slb-asset="1452425588" data-slb-internal="0" data-slb-group="slb"><img fetchpriority="high" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-1901 size-full" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20585%20203'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="new version of Foudre," width="585" height="203" data-lazy-srcset="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_version_8.png 585w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_version_8-300x104.png 300w " data-lazy-sizes="(max-width: 585px) 100vw, 585px" data-lazy-src="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_version_8.png" /><noscript><img fetchpriority="high" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-1901 size-full" src="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_version_8.png" alt="new version of Foudre," width="585" height="203" srcset="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_version_8.png 585w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_version_8-300x104.png 300w " sizes="(max-width: 585px) 100vw, 585px" /></noscript></a></p>

<h4><strong>No to The Forced Hijab</strong></h4>
<p>Similarly to the samples noted in previous reports, this new malware also comes packaged in a WinRAR SFX archive including multiple malicious binaries and a media file. The media file in this case is a video in the MP4 format showing a woman in Iran walking around and at the end pulling off her hijab. In the video, there is text written in Farsi with a hashtag, بنه به حجاب اجباری#, literally translating to “no to the forced hijab.” This hashtag is in reference to protesters in Iran who are protesting the mandatory use of the hijab for women and the video is meant to distract the victim while the Foudre malware gets installed in the background.</p>
<p><a href="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video.png" data-slb-group="post-images" data-slb-active="1" data-slb-asset="528833286" data-slb-internal="0" data-slb-group="slb"><img decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-1902 size-full" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201302%20711'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="No to The Forced Hijab" width="1302" height="711" data-lazy-srcset="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video.png 1302w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-300x164.png 300w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-768x419.png 768w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-595xh.png 595w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-253x139.png 253w " data-lazy-sizes="(max-width: 1302px) 100vw, 1302px" data-lazy-src="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-1902 size-full" src="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video.png" alt="No to The Forced Hijab" width="1302" height="711" srcset="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video.png 1302w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-300x164.png 300w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-768x419.png 768w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-595xh.png 595w , https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_video-253x139.png 253w " sizes="(max-width: 1302px) 100vw, 1302px" /></noscript></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">(Screenshot from the video bundled in the malware)</p>
<p>Foudre is a remote access tool and has the ability to remotely execute commands, steal information about the infected target (such as keystrokes, process information, etc), and auto-update itself. Most of the code and functionality from the previous versions of Foudre and Infy was reused and can be read about in the reports linked above, so we are only going to focus on the new, unique, interesting features and the linkage of code reuse from previous versions.</p>
<h4><strong>Code Reuse</strong></h4>

<h4><strong>New Features/Changes</strong></h4>
<p>First of all, the main binary of the upgraded Foudre malware is mostly undetected on VirusTotal with only 3/67 detections.</p>
<p><a href="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_vt2.png" data-slb-group="post-images" data-slb-active="1" data-slb-asset="1966306302" data-slb-internal="0" data-slb-group="slb"><img decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-1903 size-large" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201024%20755'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="First of all, the main binary of the upgraded Foudre malware is mostly undetected on VirusTotal with only 3/67 detections." width="1024" height="755" data-lazy-src="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_vt2-1024x755.png" /><noscript><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-1903 size-large" src="https://149520725.v2.pressablecdn.com//wp-content/uploads/2018/08/foudre_vt2-1024x755.png" alt="First of all, the main binary of the upgraded Foudre malware is mostly undetected on VirusTotal with only 3/67 detections." width="1024" height="755" /></noscript></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">(<a href="https://www.virustotal.com/#/file/c7279a32329ebb1ab5c1cdbfbddb5a167e1505340c3ca72e837a222ff92665a6/detection">VirusTotal</a>)</p>
<p>In the latest version of Foudre, there are 2 modules. One of the modules (i7234.dll) has the export “D1” and the other module (d388) exports “D2” as a function. We are going to refer to the different binaries based on their exports, D1 and D2. The third binary never gets launched and is still under investigation. We will release more details about it on a further date. The WinRAR SFX and D1 module only get executed once. The following features/changes are spread across the WinRAR SFX, D1, and D2:</p>
<p>WinRAR SFX Dropper</p>
<ol style="color: #627d98;">
<li style="font-weight: 400;">WinRAR SFX has icon of girl with hijab from video</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Extracts files</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Launches D1 ( i7234.dll) with rundll32 and executes export D1</li>
</ol>

<ol style="color: #627d98;">

<li style="font-weight: 400;">Checks if finds a window “TNRRDPKE2” means it’s already running</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Copies D2 and key to %APPDATA% with filenames a.n and p.k and creates a shortcut in the folder named “an.lnk” C:\WINDOWS\system32\rundll32.exe a.n D2 838238125</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Deletes these files from TEMP folder</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Stores name of D2 (a.n) in HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\temp in key called “ran2”</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Checks HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run for SnailDriver</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Creates autorun for an.lnk</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Checks for %PROGRAMFILES%\Kaspersky Lab</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Launches D2 with rundll32 “C:\WINDOWS\system32\rundll32.exe a.n D2 838238125”</li>

<h4><strong>Domain Generation Algorithm (DGA)</strong></h4>
<p>The DGA used by version 8 of Foudre has only changed slightly from the previous versions.</p>
<p>In the previous versions, the DGA algorithm was calculated by the following algorithm (credit to <a href="https://twitter.com/xedi25" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">Esmid Idrizovic</a>):</p>
<p>ToHex(CRC32(&#8220;NRV1&#8221; + year + month + week_number)) + (&#8220;.space&#8221;|&#8221;.net&#8221;|&#8221;.top&#8221;)</p>
<p>There are two minor differences now when calculating the C2. NRV1 was replaced with NRTV1 and .dynu.net was added as suffix to the domain making the algorithm now:</p>
<p>ToHex(CRC32(&#8220;NRTV1&#8221; + year + month + week_number)) + (&#8220;.space&#8221;|&#8221;.net&#8221;|&#8221;.top&#8221;|”.dynu.net”)</p>
<p>A few of the calculated domains were added to the bottom of the report in the IoCs section. The domains up to week of September 9, 2018 (week 35) have been registered in Panama and resolve to the same IP address 185[.]61[.]154[.]26. The oldest domain using this algorithm we could find that was registered was registered for the week of November 5, 2017.</p>

<h4></h4>
<h4><strong>Conclusion</strong></h4>
<p>Due to the content of the video and the information from the reports on previous versions of Foudre, we believe the targets are mostly Iranian citizens. We have registered some of the future generated domains to prevent the attack, and will update the post with information in regards to the infected victims.</p>
