nn no np nq nr he bl"><strong class="mw hm">Why this is different from the raw stream?</strong></p><p id="8599" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl"><em class="qp">Data are always complete<br/></em>This stream have only two types of event: <em class="qp">create</em> and <em class="qp">delete</em>. This allows to push only events containing the full data in STIX 2.1 format. There is no partial update/patching ontology as it is in the raw stream.</p><p id="75bf" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl"><em class="qp">Data dependencies are implicitly injected</em><br/>That’s a huge difference between the raw stream and any other approach like the TAXII one. This stream takes care for consumer of the data dependencies. Basically, if you create a live stream filtered to only get the “<strong class="mw hm">Malwares</strong>”, the stream will also published all data attached to this malware by transitivity.</p><p id="af0e" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl">Using this capability, the client does not need to process everything from the beginning to have consistent data but can just start consuming now and have all the required knowledge before going “live”.</p><p id="4c90" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl"><em class="qp">Back pressure management<br/></em>Not yet available in raw stream (this is the subject of on-going works), the live stream have the capability to handle some commands to manage the back pressure. I will explain a bit more the mechanism in the synchronization section. Please continue to read if you are interested 😀.</p><p id="894e" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl"><strong class="mw hm">Current internal usages of the live streams</strong></p><p id="1254" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl"><em class="qp">Backup and restore </em><br/>Using the connector “<a class="ah pv" href="https://github.com/OpenCTI-Platform/connectors/tree/master/stream/backup-files" rel="noopener ugc nofollow" target="_blank">backup-files</a>”, it’s possible to use live streams to backup all OpenCTI data in STIX 2.1 JSON files (and raw files associated to element) in a directory. Also, a “<a class="ah pv" href="https://github.com/OpenCTI-Platform/connectors/tree/master/external-import/restore-files" rel="noopener ugc nofollow" target="_blank">restore-files</a>” connector is available to restore the file from the a directory containing “<a class="ah pv" href="https://github.com/OpenCTI-Platform/connectors/tree/master/stream/backup-files" rel="noopener ugc nofollow" target="_blank">backup-files</a>” data.</p><p id="1d10" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl"><em class="qp">Share data to other systems</em><br/>Obviously this stream can be used to react on OpenCTI data in third party systems.</p><p id="7a59" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mu mv hl mw b mx my mz na nb nc nd ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr he bl"><em class="qp">Platform synchronization<br/></em>Since OpenCTI 5.0.0, a built-in synchronization management feature is available in the user interface. To create a new synchronizer, an administrator should know the remote OpenCTI URL, token and live stream ID to consume. This, the embedded process will connect to the live stream of the remote OpenCTI, get the data as soon as its published in order to “create/update/delete” the elements.</p></div></div><div hm">https://remote/stream/live</strong> and get a [connection_id] as a reference.<br/>Data is stored in a memory internal queue.</li><li id="a6cd" class="mu mv hl mw b mx rh mz na nb ri nd ne nf rj nh ni nj rk nl nm nn rl np nq nr re rf rg bl">Synchronizer processes the data in order of the internal queue.</li><li id="e03b" class="mu mv hl mw b mx rh mz na nb ri nd ne nf rj nh ni nj rk nl nm nn rl are now received at low speed.</li><li id="94d7" class="mu mv hl mw b mx rh mz na nb ri nd ne nf rj nh ni nj rk nl nm nn rl np nq nr re rf rg bl">Synchronizer continue to process the queue that now
