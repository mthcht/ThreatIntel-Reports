					<h1 class="entry-title">Carding ecosystem: The fall of traditional financial cybercrime</h1>					<div class="post-info">
						<span class="post-category">
							<a href="https://outpost24.com/blog/category/research-and-threat-intel/">Research &amp; Threat Intel</a>							</span>
							<span class="post-date">Last updated: 26 Sep 2025</span>
						</div>
					</div>
											<div class="article-author">
							<span class="written-by">Written By</span>
																							<div class="author-container">
									<span class="author-name"><a href="https://outpost24.com/blog/authors/marcelo-ruano/">Marcelo Ruano</a></span>
									<span class="author-title">Threat Intelligence Researcher, Outpost24</span>
								</div>
													</div>
										
<p>Carding is a type of cybercrime where attackers steal or illegally buy credit card information and use it to make unauthorized transactions. It often involves testing stolen card numbers with small purchases before making larger fraudulent charges. Criminals typically exchange or sell these stolen details on underground forums or dark web marketplaces.</p>



<p>Outpost24’s Threat Intelligence team, KrakenLabs, carried out a previous public analysis of the underground <a href="https://outpost24.com/blog/credit-card-fraud-investigation/">card fraud ecosystem</a> in 2022. Three years later, we’re bringing you an updated overview of the current state of carding.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-key-takeaways-about-the-carding-ecosystem"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="key-takeaways-about-the-carding-ecosystem"></span>Key takeaways about the carding ecosystem<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Carding is in decline. After doing a deep analysis of the situation, we have uncovered the following key factors, which we consider may have led to the current situation.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Carding under pressure</strong>: Law enforcement agencies, global payment networks, banks, and regulators have been gradually implementing advanced measures that make traditional carding techniques far less effective.</li>



<li><strong>Erosion of trust:</strong> A plethora of internal scams coupled with forum shutdowns have severely weakened confidence within carder communities, reducing their stability.</li>



<li><strong>Operational difficulties:</strong> Constant disruption of Telegram accounts and channels, together with bigger entry barriers, undermines carders ability to establish and threatens the long-term sustainability of their operations.</li>



<li><strong>Evolving tactics:</strong> Established carders are experimenting with AI agents and advanced methods to adapt to a new and complex environment. However, the lack of skilled operators and failure to attract new ones raise doubts about the community’s long-term resilience.</li>



<li><strong>Existence of fraud alternatives: </strong>Cybercriminals are increasingly turning to the use of synthetic identities, account takeovers, and cryptocurrency-related scams, which seem to pose fewer hurdles and achieve greater success.</li>



<li><strong>Overall decline:</strong> Established carders moving to more profitable ventures, new cybercriminals not getting into the ecosystem, and those remaining struggling to combat and adapt against new and improved security measures have led to the current situation, with way less visible activity and fewer fresh data dumps<ins>,</ins> turns carding into a less attractive business and further increasing the aforementioned problems, which seems to be leading to an inevitable “demographic collapse” highlight the diminishing appeal and profitability of carding in 2025.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-carding-forums"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="carding-forums"></span>Carding forums<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Specialized carding forums play a crucial role in this type of fraud, serving as hubs for activities such as:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The sale and sharing of stolen cards, both individually and in bulk.</li>



<li>Announcement and sharing of new carding tools and techniques.</li>



<li>Tutorials and specific training for newcomers.</li>



<li>Hosting debates about carding techniques and trends.</li>



<li>Promotion of active card shops in banners or in specific threads.</li>
</ul>



<p>Given its decades-long presence, carding has given rise to hundreds of forums in various languages and regions, most notably within Russian and English-speaking communities.</p>



<p>Some of the most active carding specialized forums are:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Altenens</li>



<li>Club2Crd</li>



<li>Carders[.]biz</li>



<li>WWH-Club</li>
</ul>



<p>The activity level in these forums and within the different sections differs. However, most of the public activity is, in general, relatively unsophisticated.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="carding-markets"></span>Carding markets<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Most carding markets share a similar structure, with several sections in common:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Stolen cards section, with most of the cards being sold individually for a range of prices between US$ 3 to US$ 150.</li>



<li>Dumps section, giving the option to buy dumps in bulk.</li>



<li>Fullzs: This section includes both personal and financial information about the victim.</li>



<li>Checker tools to evaluate if the cards are still active and valid.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="carding-trends-and-overall-atmosphere"></span>Carding trends and overall atmosphere<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>During the last year, we have seen a significant decrease in the volume of stolen credit cards being sold in these underground carding markets and forums. Where large batches of hundreds or even thousands of cards were once common, the offerings have shifted towards smaller, more selective offerings, with some markets even selling individual cards at higher prices depending on their quality. &nbsp;</p>



<p>Carders themselves seem to be aware of the mounting difficulties in sustaining their illicit activities. Discussions on underground forums and marketplaces reveal that many cybercriminals acknowledge how measures such as AI-driven fraud detection, biometric verification, tokenized payments, and stronger regulatory frameworks are eroding the effectiveness of traditional tactics.</p>

<p>Furthermore, the rise of scams and mistrust among carders is another growing challenge. Many underground forums and marketplaces have become riddled with fraudulent sellers who advertise invalid or recycled card data, or who simply scam others by disappearing after receiving payment without providing any product. This environment of deception forces even experienced carders to doubt the reliability of their peers, leading to disputes, negative reputation scores, and frequent forum shutdowns. As trust erodes, cybercriminals must spend more time verifying sources, using escrow services, or migrating between platforms, which not only increases their operational costs but also weakens the overall resilience of the carding community.</p>

We assess that tighter fraud defenses and repeated law enforcement disruptions have discouraged many lower-level actors, leaving forums quieter and less vibrant than in previous years.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="fraud-fighting-strategies"></span>Fraud-fighting strategies<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Combating card fraud has become a top priority for global payment networks and financial institutions alike. Companies such as <a href="https://investor.visa.com/news/news-details/2025/Visa-Unveils-its-Scam-Disruption-Practice-Helping-Protect-Consumers-and-the-Financial-Ecosystem-Globally/default.aspx" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow">Visa</a> and <a href="https://newsroom.mastercard.com/news/press/2024/may/mastercard-accelerates-card-fraud-detection-with-generative-ai-technology/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow">Mastercard</a> are investing billions in advanced fraud prevention, combining AI-driven analytics, biometric authentication, tokenization, and real-time monitoring to stay ahead of increasingly sophisticated cybercriminals.</p>



<p>Many traditional carding schemes are now quickly identified and blocked before fraudulent transactions can be successful. Even when criminals attempt more sophisticated methods, such as linking stolen data to mobile wallets or executing social engineering scams, multi-factor authentication and predictive AI monitoring substantially reduce their effectiveness. As a result, the profitability of carding is diminishing, forcing threat actors to take greater risks for lesser rewards.</p>



<p>Additionally, regulations and organizations, such as the PCI Security Standards Council, play a significant role in fortifying security protocols aimed at detecting and preventing fraudulent and malicious operations. This is particularly evident in frameworks like PCI DSS, which, when combined with emerging technologies, enable more effective monitoring and real-time anomaly detection to prevent illicit transaction attempts.</p>



<p>These advancements have a direct impact on carding operations, forcing the underground market to refocus its efforts on less-secured assets—such as the commonly referenced “non-VBV” cards—and regions with less stringent regulatory frameworks, such as parts of Africa and Asia, where compliance with international standards may be less enforced but also economic profits are smaller, reducing the economic incentives of carding.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="law-enforcement-operations-%e2%80%93-global-fight-against-cybercrime-intensifies"></span>Law enforcement operations – Global fight against cybercrime intensifies<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Law enforcement agencies conducted several major operations resulting in the shutdown of multiple relevant criminal networks, ranging from the seizure of markets to the dismantling of ransomware groups.</p>



<p>As far as carding is concerned, one of the most recent examples is the <a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/bidencash-carding-market-domains-seized-in-international-operation/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">seizure of BidenCash</a> market during June 2025, as part of an operation led by the United States Secret Service and the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and supported by other agencies and organizations worldwide. This market alone totaled up to millions of leaked credit cards, translating to more than US$ 17 million in revenue since its inception in 2022.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="835" height="559" src="//outpost24.com/wp-content/plugins/a3-lazy-load/assets/images/lazy_placeholder.gif" data-lazy-type="image" data-src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer.png" alt="" class="lazy lazy-hidden wp-image-9776" srcset="" data-srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer.png 835w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer-300x201.png 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer-768x514.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 835px) 100vw, 835px" /><noscript><img decoding="async" width="835" height="559" src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer.png" alt="" class="wp-image-9776" srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer.png 835w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer-300x201.png 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-10-BidenCash-Seizure-Source-BleepingComputer-768x514.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 835px) 100vw, 835px" /></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption"><em>Figure 10. BidenCash Seizure. Source: BleepingComputer</em></figcaption></figure>



<p>Influential cybercriminal forums have also been successfully targeted. For instance, during July 2025, an investigation led by the French Police in cooperation with Europol led to the arrest of a key figure behind XSS, one of the most notorious cybercrime forums. With these seizures, cybercriminals are continuously being forced to seek out new places to communicate, exchange data, and promote their services, all whilst being aware of the potential risk of incoming law enforcement actions.</p>



<p>Furthermore, after the arrest of the founder and CEO of Telegram, Telegram <a href="https://www.wired.com/story/pavel-durov-arrest-telegram-content-moderation/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">began revising its privacy stance</a>—signaling willingness to provide IP addresses and phone numbers to law enforcement upon valid court orders. Alongside these policy shifts, the platform has been actively shutting down cybercrime-related channels and accounts, including the <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/china/2-massive-black-market-services-blocked-by-telegram-messaging-app-says-2025-05-15/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">removal of significant black-market</a> groups like Haowang (Huione) Guarantee, estimated to have facilitated over US$ 27 billion in illicit crypto and money laundering activity. This shift in Telegram’s policies, combined with the periodic deletion of criminal channels and accounts, has introduced greater uncertainty for cybercriminals, further hindering cybercriminal operations.</p>



<p>This law enforcement and messaging platforms pressure has created an environment where almost every new platform –be it a market, forum, Telegram channels, etc.– is questioned for its confidentiality, privacy and integrity.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="carders-next-steps"></span>Carders next steps<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>In response to traditional fraud methods becoming obsolete, some experienced carders propose that the solution lies in improving their technical knowledge and skills, encouraging their peers to study advanced bypass techniques, stay updated on evolving security systems, and learn how to exploit new digital platforms more effectively.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="937" height="385" src="//outpost24.com/wp-content/plugins/a3-lazy-load/assets/images/lazy_placeholder.gif" data-lazy-type="image" data-src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD.png" alt="" class="lazy lazy-hidden wp-image-9777" srcset="" data-srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD.png 937w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD-300x123.png 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD-768x316.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 937px) 100vw, 937px" /><noscript><img decoding="async" width="937" height="385" src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD.png" alt="" class="wp-image-9777" srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD.png 937w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD-300x123.png 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Figure-11-Proposed-solution-to-carding-decline-Source-Club2CRD-768x316.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 937px) 100vw, 937px" /></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption"><em>Figure 11. <p>The evolution in carding methods has already been noticed by payment services providers. According to <a href="https://usa.visa.com/services/visa-innovation-and-design/evolution-of-payment-fraud.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow">Michael Jibbara</a>, Global Head of Fraud Services at Visa, they observed a particular shift in criminal behavior: “[threat actors] moved upstream from a data perspective, so they are no longer going after payment data, they are going after account data. They&#8217;re going after the username and password, your name, your social security number, your email address, so they can create synthetic identities.”</p>



<p>Furthermore, facing stronger defenses and shrinking opportunities, high-skilled carders are experimenting with innovative methods to sustain their operations. A growing trend involves the exploration of AI-powered agents to automate phishing, credential harvesting, and fraud execution, with the goal of bypassing detection systems and scaling their illicit activities more efficiently.<del></del></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="is-carding-on-the-decline"></span>Is carding on the decline?<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>In recent years, the overall level of carding activity has noticeably declined. Underground forums that once thrived with daily dumps of stolen payment cards now show fewer listings and lower engagement. Law enforcement takedowns, stronger security measures from payment networks, and periodic platform disruptions have discouraged many lower-level actors. As a result, carding no longer attracts the same volume of opportunistic cybercriminals it once did.</p>



<p>However, while the number of active carders has decreased, those who remain seem to be more sophisticated and technically skilled. Instead of relying on bulk stolen card data, they experiment with advanced tactics such as the use of synthetic identities, account takeover fraud, and even AI-driven automation. These operators focus on quality over quantity, carefully adapting to bypass stronger defenses. However, few newcomers are able to reach this high level of expertise, raising questions about the long-term sustainability of the carding ecosystem.</p>



<p>While traditional card fraud shows this not so optimistic future for cybercriminals, the adoption of cryptocurrencies is <a href="https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/2025-global-crypto-adoption-index/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow">increasing worldwide</a>. New regulations, the rise of stablecoins for payments<ins>,</ins> and the growing number of <a href="https://coinlaw.io/cryptocurrency-wallet-adoption-statistics/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow">active crypto wallets</a> especially among young generations, signal a shift into a more crypto-related future of finance. Cybercrime goes in parallel, and as traditional carding declines, crypto-related crime is also on the rise, offering cybercriminals new opportunities with fewer restrictions and broader global reach.</p>
