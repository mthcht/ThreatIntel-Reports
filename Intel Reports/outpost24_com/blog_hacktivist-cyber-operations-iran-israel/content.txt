					<h1 class="entry-title">How hacktivist cyber operations surged amid Israeli-Iranian conflict</h1>					<div class="post-info">
						<span class="post-category">
							<a href="https://outpost24.com/blog/category/cybersecurity-news/">Cybersecurity News</a><a href="https://outpost24.com/blog/category/research-and-threat-intel/">Research &amp; Threat Intel</a>							</span>
							<span class="post-date">Last updated: 01 Jul 2025</span>
						</div>
					</div>
											<div class="article-author">
							<span class="written-by">Written By</span>
																							<div class="author-container">
									<span class="author-name"><a href="https://outpost24.com/blog/authors/lidia-lopez-sanz-2/">Lidia López Sanz</a></span>
									<span class="author-title">Senior Threat Intelligence Analyst </span>
								</div>
													</div>
										
<p>In June 2025, Israel carried out airstrikes against key Iranian military and nuclear facilities. Iran swiftly retaliated, escalating regional tensions to unprecedented levels. This military confrontation has not only unfolded in conventional warfare but also triggered a massive <strong>surge in cyber operations</strong>.</p>



<p>Almost immediately after the kinetic attacks, numerous hacktivist groups began launching cyberattacks primarily targeting Israel and its international allies. The vast majority of these groups present themselves as pro-Iranian and pro-Palestinian actors, motivated by ideological, political, and religious narratives.</p>



<p>However, cybersecurity analysts caution that certain attacks may involve nation-state actors disguising their activity behind the facade of hacktivism. This phenomenon is widely referred to as <strong>faketivism</strong>.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-summary-of-hacktivist-activity-in-the-israel-iran-conflict"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="summary-of-hacktivist-activity-in-the-israel-iran-conflict"></span>Summary of hacktivist activity in the Israel-Iran conflict<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>This article provides a comprehensive, evidence-based analysis of the hacktivist activity surrounding the Israeli-Iranian conflict, examining public statements, claimed attacks, profiles of the key actors involved, and emerging patterns suggesting state-sponsored faketivist involvement.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Over <strong>80 distinct hacktivist groups</strong> are actively conducting or supporting offensive cyber operations targeting Israel and its allies. To a lesser extent, there are some hacktivist groups supporting Israel as well.</li>



<li>Primary motivations of these groups include <strong>pro-Palestinian solidarity, anti-Western sentiment, Islamic ideological alignment</strong>, and in some cases, direct <strong>support for Iranian geopolitical objectives</strong>.</li>



<li>Attacks span across government, military, defense contractors, judicial systems, energy infrastructure, satellite operations, ICS/OT systems, social media platforms, and financial institutions.</li>



<li>Several hacktivist entities (e.g., <em>Keymous+, Mr Hamza, Mysterious Team, Inteid, Cyber Islamic Resistance</em>) demonstrate coordination, sharing resources and intelligence, and form operational alliances.</li>



<li>Suspected <strong>faketivist entities </strong>such as <em>CyberAv3ngers</em>, <em>Handala</em>, and <em>Predatory Sparrow </em>are likely operating with state support or directly under state direction.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-hacktivist-groups-statements-and-strategic-alliances"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="hacktivist-groups-statements-and-strategic-alliances"></span>Hacktivist groups’ statements and strategic alliances<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Following the Israeli military strikes on Iranian targets, dozens of hacktivist groups responded almost immediately with statements of solidarity and coordinated calls for cyber retaliation. Most of these communications occurred via <strong>Telegram</strong>, with some activity spilling onto X (formerly Twitter). </p>



<p>These public declarations are ideologically charged, often referencing anti-Zionist, pan-Islamic, and anti-Western narratives. Below is an overview of<strong> key statements and alliances</strong> made:</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-mysterious-team-bangladesh"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="mysterious-team-bangladesh"></span>Mysterious Team Bangladesh<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>This group expressed full support for Iran, calling for &#8220;Operation True Promise 3&#8221; against Israel. The group warned Jordan, Egypt, and Saudi Arabia to avoid interfering with Iranian operations or risk retaliatory cyberattacks against their own infrastructure.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-team-insane-pakistan"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="team-insane-pakistan"></span>Team Insane Pakistan<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>The pro-Pakistan hacktivist group expressed solidarity, urging Iran to aggressively challenge Israel. They compared the conflict to Pakistan’s recent confrontations with India.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-z-alliance"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="z-alliance"></span>Z-Alliance<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>In a statement written in Russian, <em>Z-Alliance</em> openly declared their full support for Iran. They rejected political neutrality and stated their readiness to conduct offensive operations without constraint.</p>

<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img decoding="async" width="827" height="579" src="//outpost24.com/wp-content/plugins/a3-lazy-load/assets/images/lazy_placeholder.gif" data-lazy-type="image" data-src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance.jpg" alt="Z-Alliance openly declaring support for Iran and threatening cyber retaliation against perceived opponents." class="lazy lazy-hidden wp-image-8979" srcset="" data-srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance.jpg 827w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance-300x210.jpg 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance-768x538.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 827px) 100vw, 827px" /><noscript><img decoding="async" width="827" height="579" src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance.jpg" alt="Z-Alliance openly declaring support for Iran and threatening cyber retaliation against perceived opponents." class="wp-image-8979" srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance.jpg 827w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance-300x210.jpg 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Z-Alliance-768x538.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 827px) 100vw, 827px" /></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption"><em>Figure 3: Z-Alliance openly declaring support for Iran and threatening cyber retaliation against perceived opponents.</em></figcaption></figure></div>


<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-server-killers"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="server-killers"></span>Server Killers<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p><em>Server Killers</em> publicly aligned themselves with the anti-Israel campaign and initiated attacks against Israeli judicial, educational, and governmental web infrastructure. They simultaneously encouraged widespread participation from external supporters.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img decoding="async" width="540" height="675" src="//outpost24.com/wp-content/plugins/a3-lazy-load/assets/images/lazy_placeholder.gif" data-lazy-type="image" data-src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/ServerKillers.jpg" alt="Server Killers explicitly calling for cyberattacks against Israeli governmental websites." class="lazy lazy-hidden wp-image-8980" srcset="" data-srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/ServerKillers.jpg 540w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/ServerKillers-240x300.jpg 240w" sizes="(max-width: 540px) 100vw, 540px" /><noscript><img decoding="async" width="540" height="675" src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/ServerKillers.jpg" alt="Server Killers explicitly calling for cyberattacks against Israeli governmental websites." class="wp-image-8980" srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/ServerKillers.jpg 540w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/ServerKillers-240x300.jpg 240w" sizes="(max-width: 540px) 100vw, 540px" /></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption"><em>Figure 4: Server Killers explicitly calling for cyberattacks against Israeli governmental websites.</em></figcaption></figure></div>

<p><em>Akatsuki Cyber Team</em> positioned itself within the pan-Muslim hacktivist narrative, fully rejecting Israel’s legitimacy as a state and expressing intentions to unite additional actors for a broader coordinated cyber offensive.</p>

<p><em>GhostSec</em> expanded its already ongoing campaigns against Israeli infrastructure, presenting a detailed summary of previously compromised Israeli water, industrial control, and satellite systems. The group outlined its ongoing multiphase attacks, reflecting both persistent operational capabilities and a continued escalation model.</p>

</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-arabian-ghosts"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="arabian-ghosts"></span>Arabian Ghosts<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Focus</strong>: Regional and ideological hacktivism; initially anti-Iran/Russia, now aligned with anti-Israel operations.</li>



<li><strong>Activities</strong>: DDoS, defacements, ICS/OT disruption, data leaks. Claimed Red Alert app attack; announced but unconfirmed ransomware project <em>BadLadyBird</em>.</li>



<li><strong>Origin Hypothesis</strong>: Likely Syrian; self-identified as &#8220;Syrian Ghosts&#8221; supporting Anonymous Syria.</li>



<li><strong>Alliances</strong>: No formal ties, but recurrent references to <em>DieNet, Mr. Hamza, SYLHET_GANG_SG</em>, and others suggest informal collaboration.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-laneh-dark"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="laneh-dark"></span>Laneh Dark<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Focus</strong>: Pro-Iran, anti-Israel, anti-Gulf states; ideologically driven hacktivism with elements of cyber extortion.</li>



<li><strong>Activities</strong>: Data theft and ransom-style pressure tactics on companies in Saudi Arabia, Jordan, UAE, and Azerbaijan; public leaks of sensitive infrastructure data; political messaging aligned with Iranian strategic interests; alleged mass device access via “All Seeing Eye” vulnerability.</li>



<li><strong>Tools</strong>: Custom leak operations; extortion demands; Telegram/X for psychological ops; claims of undisclosed vulnerability exploitation tools affecting 45,000 global systems.</li>



<li><strong>Alliances</strong>: NoName057(16); part of “Nest Security Group” alliance with Russian, Palestinian, and Iranian-affiliated actors.</li>



<li><strong>Origin Hypothesis</strong>: Likely Iranian-managed, ideologically motivated with indirect state alignment; emerged in 2024 with increasing visibility post-June 2025.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-are-nation-states-disguising-themselves-as-hacktivists"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="are-nation-states-disguising-themselves-as-hacktivists"></span>Are nation-states disguising themselves as hacktivists?<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>While many hacktivist groups appear motivated by ideological or political convictions, several actors involved in the Israeli-Iranian conflict are suspected of being state-controlled or heavily state-influenced, blurring the lines between grassroots hacktivism and state-sponsored cyber operations.</p>



<p>These so-called faketivist entities serve as plausible deniability fronts for nation-states, complicating attribution and escalating geopolitical risks. These groups blur the line between independent hacktivism and state-sponsored cyber warfare, complicating attribution and increasing the geopolitical risks of escalation.</p>



<p>Notable faketivist entities that analysts continuously monitor include:</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-cyber-av3ngers"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="cyber-av3ngers"></span>Cyber Av3ngers<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Attribution</strong>: Iran-linked faketivist group likely operated under IRGC (Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps) control.</li>



<li><strong>Focus</strong>: Disruption of Israeli and U.S. critical infrastructure (ICS/OT, energy, water systems).</li>



<li><strong>Activities</strong>: Targeting Israeli companies (e.g., BAZAN Group), U.S. water plants (Unitronics PLCs), oil &amp; gas (Sophos firewall exploits); data exfiltration, limited sales of compromised data in underground forums.</li>



<li><strong>Tools</strong>: IOControl embedded Linux backdoor with wiper capabilities; AI-assisted vulnerability research; ICS/SCADA exploit development.</li>



<li><strong>Alliances</strong>: May have indirect links with other Iranian groups such as Moses Staff; unconfirmed relations with IRGC&#8217;s &#8220;<em>Soldiers of Solomon</em>&#8220;.</li>



<li><strong>Commercial Activity</strong>: Participated in data sales via Breached and DarkForums, though with limited underground presence.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-handala-hack-team"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="handala-hack-team"></span>Handala Hack Team<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Attribution</strong>: Likely Iranian-controlled faketivist persona posing as pro-Palestinian hacktivist group.</li>



<li><strong>Focus</strong>: Phishing-based wiper deployment using social engineering around high-profile events.</li>



<li><strong>Activities</strong>: Claimed &#8220;Operation HamsaUpdate&#8221; (Hatef/Hamsa wipers exploiting F5 vulnerabilities) and phishing during the 2024 CrowdStrike outage.</li>



<li><strong>Indicators of State Backing</strong>: High sophistication in targeting, Hebrew language proficiency, victim data inclusion, consistent with state-level preparation.</li>



<li><strong>Tools</strong>: Hatef (Windows wiper), Hamsa (Linux wiper); social engineering campaigns leveraging trending security incidents.</li>



<li><strong>Underground Presence</strong>: Brief activity in BreachForums and RAMP after December 2023 creation; maintains active Telegram channels and TOR domains.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-preadtory-sparrow-aka-gonjeshke-darande"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="preadtory-sparrow-aka-gonjeshke-darande"></span>Preadtory Sparrow (aka <a href="https://outpost24.com/blog/gonjeshke-darande-attacks-iranian-nobitex/">Gonjeshke Darande</a>)<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Attribution</strong>: Highly likely Israeli state-sponsored offensive actor masquerading as an Iranian opposition group. Read our recent analysis into their attack on the Nobitex crypto exchange.</li>



<li><strong>Focus</strong>: Strategic disruption of Iranian critical infrastructure.</li>



<li><strong>Activities</strong>: Sabotage of Iran’s railway (Meteor wiper), gas stations (payment system attacks), steel industry (Khouzestan mill sabotage), energy infrastructure.</li>



<li><strong>Tools</strong>: Custom wipers (Meteor, Stardust, Comet), lockscreen message payloads; highly tailored attacks involving prior detailed reconnaissance.</li>



<li><strong>Narrative Control</strong>: Justifies operations via Telegram/X accounts with anti-IRGC (Quds Force) rhetoric, emphasizing civilian protection.</li>



<li><strong>Tactics</strong>: Long periods of dormancy between highly impactful operations, often coinciding with major regional escalations.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-can-we-expect-next-in-this-evolving-cyber-conflict"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="what-can-we-expect-next-in-this-evolving-cyber-conflict"></span>What can we expect next in this evolving cyber conflict?<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>The dramatic rise in hacktivist cyber operations following recent <strong>geopolitical escalations</strong> between Israel and Iran underscores the increasingly central role cyber conflict plays within modern warfare. <strong>Ideologically-driven hacktivists</strong>, alongside possible <strong>nation-state faketivists</strong>, have clearly demonstrated their readiness to exploit geopolitical tensions to pursue diverse <strong>strategic objectives</strong>.</p>



<p>Given that the<a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/jun/24/trump-israel-iran-ceasefire-netanyahu" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> Israeli-Iranian confrontation is still developing</a>, it’s hard to predict whether a ceasefire will hold. If cyber activity re-intensifies, expected trends would likely include:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Increased DDoS campaigns</strong> targeting Israeli energy, telecom, defense, and government systems.</li>



<li><strong>Escalation towards allies</strong> such as the US, UK, and Arab states aligned with Israel.</li>



<li><strong>Emerging retaliatory operations</strong> by pro-Israeli groups such as Predatory Sparrow, already engaging in offensive activity against Iranian systems.</li>



<li><strong>Sustained propaganda and psychological operations</strong>, further exploiting public fear and political instability.</li>
</ul>



<p>Organizations across all geopolitical fault lines are advised to adopt heightened cybersecurity postures to defend against what may evolve into a prolonged and highly disruptive cyber conflict.</p>
