      return;

          if (cookieName.startsWith(prefix)) {
            document.cookie = `${cookieName}=; expires=Thu, 01 Jan 1970 00:00:00 UTC; path=/;`;

          }
        });
      }

<p>Over the course of 2020, the number of ransomware attacks has increased enormously. As an emerging ransomware family, Nefilim has caused dozens of high profile breaches since March 2020. In this blog post, we provide tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs) utilized by the Nefilim threat actors, since detecting and blocking TTPs used by a threat is the most effective method to prevent that threat. TTPs allow us to detect potential intrusions and analyze the behavior of those attempting to intrude.</p>
<p>Our analysis uses the MITRE Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge (ATT&amp;CK®) version 8 framework. See the <a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/enterprise/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>ATT&amp;CK for Enterprise version 8.1&nbsp;</span></a>for all referenced threat actor tactics and techniques.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">This blog describes:</p>
<ul>
<li>TTPs used by the Nefilim ransomware, categorized under MITRE ATT&amp;CK tactics and techniques.</li>
<li>An example threat in the <strong><span style="color: #c63f1f;">Picus Threat Library</span></strong> that can be used to simulate the TTP.</li>
<li>An example mitigation rule in the <strong><span style="color: #00537d;">Picus Mitigation Library</span></strong><span style="color: #00537d;"> t</span>hat can be used to prevent/detect the given TTP.<br><span></span></li>

<p dir="ltr" role="presentation" style="font-size: 18px; padding-left: 40px;"><em><strong>9.1 MITRE ATT&amp;CK T1486 Data Encrypted for Impact</strong></em></p>

<p dir="ltr"><span>We analyzed dozens of Nefilim ransomware samples to determine tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs) utilized by Nefilim.
