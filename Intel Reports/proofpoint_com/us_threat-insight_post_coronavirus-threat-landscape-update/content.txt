      Coronavirus Cyber Threat Landscape Update
  </h1>

        <div class="blog-content__metadata blog-content__metadata-author">
      <span class="blog-content__date">  <time datetime="2020-03-18T15:55:58Z">March 18, 2020</time>

</span>
        <span class="blog-content__author">
                Axel F, Sam Scholten

    <div class="node-full__body blog-content__body">  <h1 style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span class="Heading1Char" style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#262626"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif">Overview</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></strong></h1>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Proofpoint researchers have tracked attackers leveraging the coronavirus pandemic since January 29. This blog serves as an update of the overall threat landscape and includes selected examples to highlight what we are seeing.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Currently, attackers are using coronavirus themes for nearly all types of attacks, including (but not limited to) <a href="https://www.proofpoint.com/us/products/email-fraud-defense">business email compromise</a> (BEC), credential phishing, malware, and spam email campaigns. </span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">The targeting of these attacks has ranged from extremely broad to narrowly focused and campaign volumes have fluctuated between small and large. Attribution includes both well-known and unknown threat actors. Some of the well-known threat actors include TA505&nbsp;and TA542.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">We’ve observed attacks around the world, most notably in Italy, the Czech Republic, Japan, United States, Canada, Australia, and Turkey. In addition to English, attackers have used Italian, Czech, Japanese, Spanish, and French languages within their messages.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">While all industries have been targeted, we’ve seen specific targeting of <a href="https://www.proofpoint.com/us/solutions/healthcare-information-security">healthcare</a>, education, manufacturing, media, advertising, and hospitality organizations in certain campaigns.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Attackers are actively abusing the names and logos of many companies and organizations within these campaigns in an attempt to manipulate recipients. Of particular note is the spoofing and brand abuse of national and international health organizations around the world, including the World Health Organization (WHO), the United States Centers for Disease Control (CDC), and Canadian and Australian national health organizations.&nbsp; </span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Threat actors have launched coronavirus campaigns to spread remote access Trojans (RATs),&nbsp; keyloggers, information stealers, and bankers.&nbsp; We are also seeing credential phishing campaigns with this theme.&nbsp; For example, we observed attempts to harvest credentials&nbsp;for Facebook, DocuSign, Microsoft Outlook Web Access (OWA), Microsoft OneDrive, and universities around the world.&nbsp;</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">We expect attackers will continue to leverage coronavirus themes in their attacks for some time to come. Proofpoint researchers will continue to track closely and provide updates on our blog and through our </span></span></span><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><a href="https://twitter.com/threatinsight" style="color:#276d5b; text-decoration:underline">Threat Insight Twitter handle</a></span></span><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Below are several notable examples of what we’ve seen.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<h2><u><strong><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">BEC Attempt: “People tested positive downtown so I can’t go out right now”</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></strong></u></h2>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Proofpoint researchers found this email, dated March 5, 2020, that seeks to capitalize on fears around the spread of coronavirus in the United States. <h2><u><strong><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Malware: Remcos</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></strong></u></h2>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black"><strong>Key Points: </strong>Campaign impersonating Philippines Customs to deliver Remcos. </span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><img alt="Phillippine Customs COVID-19 Phishing Email Example" data-entity-type="file" data-entity-uuid="d47e7f5e-73a0-4033-b8be-25917a252b56" height="801" src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/image-20200318091010-9.png" width="905" loading="lazy"></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Remos Malware Summary:&nbsp;</span></span></span></span></b></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">On March 16, 2020 Proofpoint researchers observed an email campaign targeting various international companies. The emails impersonated the Philippines customs agency and claimed to contain information about new regulation of imports as well as suspended goods. This was an interesting lure since trade suspensions and regulations have potentially greater impact compared to even travel restrictions. </span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">These emails contained Microsoft OneDrive URLs leading to a compressed Remcos RAT executable.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<h2><u><strong><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Spam: Pandemic Survival Course</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></strong></u></h2>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Key Points</span></span></span></b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">: Spam campaign promoting a survival course containing everything you supposedly need to know about the coronavirus pandemic for $37. </span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><img alt="Coronavirus Survival Course Spam Site" data-entity-type="file" data-entity-uuid="d98cfd2f-a6be-4843-9fb0-43c3b432eb31" height="1386" src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/image-20200318091010-10.png" width="813" loading="lazy"><img alt="image-20200318091010-11" data-entity-type="file" data-entity-uuid="e5f858e2-8c1f-4826-be9a-c2146027667a" height="542" src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/image-20200318091010-11.jpeg" width="621" loading="lazy"><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959">&nbsp;</span></span></span></span><img alt="How to Survive Coronavirus Course Landing Page Spam" data-entity-type="file" data-entity-uuid="b70e1d5a-5a88-4db8-8547-8e1409524734" height="582" src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/image-20200318091010-12.png" width="617" loading="lazy"></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin-bottom:8px"><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Pandemic Survival Course Spam Summary:</span></span></span></span></b></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">In March 2020, Proofpoint researchers observed multiple spam campaigns peddling an informational course for US$37. The recipient must first watch a 30+ minute long video before getting the link to purchase the course.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">The email and the video try to seed fear, uncertainty, and doubt into the recipient about the government's ability to respond to the situation. They describe a coming pandemic and other events (millions of people dead, forced quarantine by the military, drug companies experimenting on humans with vaccines and so on).</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">The course supposedly offers information around dozens of topics such as: How to make a hazmat suit; should you own a gun; how to keep the army out of your home; what to do if urban warfare breaks out; what to do if an infected neighbor knocks on your door. We did not verify what happens after the payment, but we recommend to everyone to keep their money and not take the bait.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<h2><u><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Spam: Coronavirus Testing Kit</span></span></span></b></span></span></span></span></u></h2>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Key Points</span></span></span></b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">: Spam campaign asking media and advertising companies to promote a malicious website that supposedly sells testing kits in exchange for US$100.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><img alt="Fake Coronavirus Testing Kit Spam Email Example" data-entity-type="file" data-entity-uuid="061b96e9-08b5-487c-95f7-36b63295a520" height="600" src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/image-20200318091010-13.png" width="678" loading="lazy"></p>

<p style="margin-bottom:8px"><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Coronavirus Testing Kit Spam Summary:</span></span></span></span></b></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">In early March, Proofpoint researchers observed a small spam campaign targeting media and advertising companies in the United States. The email did not try to get the recipient to open the malicious URL, but instead asked the recipients to spread a website URL to their audience. Specifically, it asked them to place the URL and a short message on top of their most recent YouTube video description. The sender offered a payment of $100 in exchange for this.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">The website, coronavirusmedicalkit[.]com, offered to sell free COVID-19 testing kits. However, at the end of the ordering process a $10 fee was added (likely for shipping). As Better Business Bureau </span></span></span><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><a href="https://www.bbb.org/article/news-releases/21482-scam-alert-preparing-for-coronavirus-that-face-mask-could-be-a-con" style="color:#276d5b; text-decoration:underline"><span style="color:black"><span style="text-decoration:none"><span style="text-underline:none">put it</span></span></span></a></span></span><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">, a lot of times “these phony sellers take victims’ money and never deliver anything at all…These sites use tricks like limited time deals to entice you into ordering more.” This campaign is interesting because we do not often see direct outreach like this via email.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<h2 style="margin-top: 31px; margin-bottom: 8px;"><u><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Spam: Masks</span></span></span></span></b></span></span></span></span></u></h2>

<p style="margin-bottom:8px"><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Key Points: </span></span></span></span></b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">One of the most common spam types with Coronavirus as a lure are offers for masks. </span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><img alt="Corornavirus Mask For Sale Spam Email Example" data-entity-type="file" data-entity-uuid="5d342a9a-3caf-408b-a09a-a18416260e62" height="1353" src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/image-20200318091010-14.png" width="949" loading="lazy"></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><b><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="line-height:107%"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">Masks Spam Summary:</span></span></span></span></b></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:15pt"><span style="line-height:normal"><span style="font-family:Arial,sans-serif"><span style="color:#595959"><span style="font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Calibri&quot;,sans-serif"><span style="color:black">The sale of masks is one of the most popular types of spam campaigns capitalizing on the coronavirus situation by volume.
