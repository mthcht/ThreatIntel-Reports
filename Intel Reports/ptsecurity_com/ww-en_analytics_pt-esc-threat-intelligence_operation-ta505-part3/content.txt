<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	Network infrastructure analysis plays an important role in the study of malware distribution campaigns. Data on which IP addresses corresponded to a given domain name over time facilitate the identification of new malicious servers. In turn, retrospectively determining which domains were resolved to a given IP address provides new domains, for which the search procedure can be repeated, leading the process further. This information can be immensely helpful in establishing the geography of nodes, identifying &quot;favorite&quot; hosts and registrars, and determining which values an attacker characteristically enters into fields when registering domains.
</p>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	Metainformation that appears useless at first glance may very well prove its worth after a period of a days, weeks, or months. In the course of malware analysis, sooner or later the question of attribution inevitably arises, and indirect identifiers such as network indicators can go a long way in determining which criminal group a certain tool belongs to. 
</p>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	This article examines the most characteristic network infrastructure indicators of the TA505 group, as well as intersections between TA505 and another hacker group, Buhtrap.
</p>
<h2 class="break-words size_m__P_9U_ color_primary__z_39p margin_none__1sjaB">Domain name registrars</h2>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	In total, we analyzed 372 domains belonging to TA505 and identified 22 organizations that facilitated the acquisition of these domains. The resources most frequently used were the following:
</p>
<ul class="listStyles_list__w6p69 listStyles_ul__CFFG0 weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA margin_xs__lb5TS">
	<li>WhoisGuard, Inc. — 28 domain names</li>
	<li>Eranet International Limited — 26 domain names</li>
</ul>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	<a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="http://www.whoisguard.com/" href="http://www.whoisguard.com/">WhoisGuard</a>, an organization based in Panama, offers the service of concealing domain owners&#x27; registration data from public access. This is <a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="http://blog.ptsecurity.com/2019/07/ironpython-darkly-how-we-uncovered.html" href="http://blog.ptsecurity.com/2019/07/ironpython-darkly-how-we-uncovered.html">not the first</a> time we have seen their services used by hackers to establish anonymity and hinder investigations.
</p>
<figure> <img src="https://pt-corp.storage.yandexcloud.net/upload/corporate/ww-en/images/esc/operation-ta505-part3/01.jpg" alt="TA505 domains registered through WhoisGuard, sorted by malware family"/> <figcaption>TA505 domains registered through WhoisGuard, sorted by malware family</figcaption> </figure>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	TA505 has utilized other, similar services, though to a lesser extent. These include PROTECTSERVICE LTD, Whois Privacy Protection Foundation, and Domains by Proxy LLC.
</p>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	<a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="http://www.now.top/" href="http://www.now.top/">Eranet International Limited</a> is one of the largest registrars in Hong Kong. It should be noted that members of TA505 tended to use <a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamic_DNS" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamic_DNS">dynamic DNS</a> when registering domains with this provider. As a result, the IP addresses that their domain names were resolved to changed frequently, making them difficult to track.
</p>
<figure> <img src="https://pt-corp.storage.yandexcloud.net/upload/corporate/ww-en/images/esc/operation-ta505-part3/02.jpg" alt="TA505 domains registered through Eranet International Limited, sorted by malware family"/> <figcaption>TA505 domains registered through Eranet International Limited, sorted by malware family</figcaption> </figure>

<h2 class="break-words size_m__P_9U_ color_primary__z_39p margin_none__1sjaB">TA505 and Buhtrap</h2>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	On July 11, 2019, specialists from ESET released an <a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="https://www.welivesecurity.com/2019/07/11/buhtrap-zero-day-espionage-campaigns/" href="https://www.welivesecurity.com/2019/07/11/buhtrap-zero-day-espionage-campaigns/">article</a> about a recent attack carried out by the Buhtrap group using a zero-day vulnerability in the Win32k component of Windows. The article described a so-called &#x27;grabber&#x27; module used to harvest user passwords from email clients, browsers, and other sources. Later, we unearthed another similar module (MD5: c6e9d7280f77977a6968722e8124f51c) with the same C&amp;C server in its body (redmond.corp-microsoft[.]com).
</p>
<figure> <img src="https://pt-corp.storage.yandexcloud.net/upload/corporate/ru-ru/images/pt-esc/operation-ta505-part3/04.jpg" alt="C&amp;C server in the Buhtrap grabber module"/> <figcaption>C&amp;C server in the Buhtrap grabber module</figcaption> </figure>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	Running a query through the <a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="https://community.riskiq.com/search/redmond.corp-microsoft.com" href="https://community.riskiq.com/search/redmond.corp-microsoft.com">PaSiveTotal</a> resource reveals that this host has been rendered to the IP address 95[.]179.159.170 since June 6, 2019.
</p>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	Several days earlier, on July 2, 2019, specialists from Proofpoint <a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="https://www.proofpoint.com/us/threat-insight/post/ta505-begins-summer-campaigns-new-pet-malware-downloader-andromut-uae-south" href="https://www.proofpoint.com/us/threat-insight/post/ta505-begins-summer-campaigns-new-pet-malware-downloader-andromut-uae-south">released</a> a report regarding new tools used by the TA505 group, one of which is called Andromut (also <a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="https://blog.trendmicro.com/trendlabs-security-intelligence/latest-spam-campaigns-from-ta505-now-using-new-malware-tools-gelup-and-flowerpippi/" href="https://blog.trendmicro.com/trendlabs-security-intelligence/latest-spam-campaigns-from-ta505-now-using-new-malware-tools-gelup-and-flowerpippi/">known</a> as Gelup).  Andromut is a downloader for the FlawedAmmyy RAT. One of the variations of the downloader that we encountered (MD5: 0cbeb424d96e5c268ec2525d603f64eb) uses the domain compatexchange-cloudapp[.]net as its C&amp;C server.
</p>
<figure> <img src="https://pt-corp.storage.yandexcloud.net/upload/corporate/ru-ru/images/pt-esc/operation-ta505-part3/05.jpg" alt="C&amp;C server in Gelup from TA505, after decryption"/> <figcaption>C&amp;C server in Gelup from TA505, after decryption</figcaption> </figure>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	The <a class="linkStyles_links-gray__auxKT weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_links-gray__IEMoh linkStyles_underlined__4E3jf" title="https://community.riskiq.com/search/compatexchange-cloudapp.net" href="https://community.riskiq.com/search/compatexchange-cloudapp.net">PaSiveTotal</a> resource shows us that this host has been resolved to the IP address 95[.]179.159.170 since June 8, 2019.
</p>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	These two domains were registered with the same registrar (Tucows Domains Inc.) within two days of one another, and are resolved to the same IP address. Considering that both groups carried out attacks throughout June, it is reasonable to conclude that Buhtrap and TA505 used the same host as a C&amp;C server.
</p>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	It is also worth noting that the domain compatexchange-cloudapp[.]net was used not only in the downloader earlier discussed, but also in older versions of Buhtrap components.
</p>
<figure> <img src="https://pt-corp.storage.yandexcloud.net/upload/corporate/ru-ru/images/pt-esc/operation-ta505-part3/06.jpg" alt="First intersection of TA505 and Buhtrap found in network infrastructure"/> <figcaption>First intersection of TA505 and Buhtrap found in network infrastructure</figcaption> </figure>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	We later discovered another intersection between the two hacker groups. The domains of TA505&#x27;s Smoke Loader and a second grabber from Buhtrap displayed a similar congruence: the domain test-service012505[.]com from Smoke Loader (MD5: 5fc6f24d43bc7ca45a81d159291955d1) and the domain secure-telemetry[.] net from the gabber (MD5: 79d1a836423c7ee57b6127cf2930a9d9) have been resolved to the IP address 194[.]4.56.252 since June 17th and 16th, 2019, respectively.
</p>
<figure> <img src="https://pt-corp.storage.yandexcloud.net/upload/corporate/ru-ru/images/pt-esc/operation-ta505-part3/07.jpg" alt="Second intersection of TA505 and Buhtrap found in network infrastructure"/> <figcaption>Second intersection of TA505 and Buhtrap found in network infrastructure</figcaption> </figure>
<h2 class="break-words size_m__P_9U_ color_primary__z_39p margin_none__1sjaB">Conclusions</h2>
<p class="weight_regular__5MYqU size_l__TtckT margin_none__1sjaB color_secondary-plus__47VrA">
	This article has examined the network infrastructure of the hacker group TA505. Starting with a look at their preferred domain name registrars and the hosts of their C&amp;C servers, we unearthed interesting details in the client information provided by the group during domain registration. This could serve as a starting point for further investigations. We then discussed intersections that were discovered between the infrastructure of the TA505 and Buhtrap hacker groups. The incidence of shared servers between the two groups could have several explanations: the groups could have a bilateral agreement to share the servers, they could be managed and coordinated by a single entity, or they could both rent the servers from a third party (thereby economizing on expenditures). Our work investigating these groups will not end here. We will continue to monitor their activity and search for new information on their possible connections and collaboration.
</p>
