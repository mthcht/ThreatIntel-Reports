Thank you so much to the person who disinterestedly donated the attacked modem.</p><p><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhUY4m1_dlVCo1UX6OcECpi5tTS3UiRne-gwOVvZ3hnl7PQqXHJZRwQoSZP7T5HUefHaURJNNPuDIrBDTHZdctMrfZy3OcWZGjN-fOd5z3XWVJuOMgszfFix3exr6htcHmxNnp_UPkJZe2q7rvYWhN_UKY3KcPSiKshgZHDl5k5VDphrtpgyidwkkUrYg/s2770/dumping.png" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;"><img border="0" data-original-height="1770" data-original-width="2770" height="408" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhUY4m1_dlVCo1UX6OcECpi5tTS3UiRne-gwOVvZ3hnl7PQqXHJZRwQoSZP7T5HUefHaURJNNPuDIrBDTHZdctMrfZy3OcWZGjN-fOd5z3XWVJuOMgszfFix3exr6htcHmxNnp_UPkJZe2q7rvYWhN_UKY3KcPSiKshgZHDl5k5VDphrtpgyidwkkUrYg/w640-h408/dumping.png" width="640" /></a></p><p>I've been closely covering this issue since the beginning, providing a&nbsp;<a href="https://www.reversemode.com/2022/03/satcom-terminals-under-attack-in-europe.html" target="_blank">plausible theory</a> based on the information that was available at that time, and my experience in this field. Firmware version is 3.7.3.10.9, which seems to date back to late 2017.</p><p>Besides talking about a 'management network' and 'legitimate management commands', Viasat did not provide any specific details about this. In my previous blog post I introduced the theory that probably 'TR069' was the involved management protocol.</p><p>Obviously, I can't completely confirm this scenario but I'll try to elaborate my reasoning.</p><h3 style="text-align: left;"><b>Attacking via a management protocol</b></h3><p>I think there are two main options: either the attackers abused a MAC management protocol or an application layer one.</p><p>For the MAC case ('ut_mac' binary), in general terms, the attackers would have required an even more privileged access to either the NOC or the Ground Stations, probably in a persistent way via malware. I guess that this kind of privileged access would have been enough to limit the attack to Ukraine, instead of knocking out half Europe. As a result, I'm inclined to think this was not the case.</p><p>On the other hand, a 'misconfigured VPN' that enabled the attackers to reach the 'management segment' and execute 'commands' seems to be more related to an application layer management protocol: SNMP or TR069.</p><p><b><u>SNMP&nbsp;</u></b></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; Did the attackers have valid credentials (maybe stolen from either Skylogic or its partners) or they exploited a known vulnerability (assuming an 0day doesn't match a 'misconfigured VPN appliance' explanation )?&nbsp;</p><p>2. How exactly the attack <a href="https://ioda.caida.org/ioda/dashboard#view=inspect&amp;entity=asn/29286&amp;lastView=overview&amp;from=1645628090&amp;until=1645800890" target="_blank">propagated</a> to other countries, lasting for several hours. One of the affected persons I talked to got his modem knocked out around 9:00 am (GMT+1), several hours after the initial attack.</p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEiojxzNrYRBWiInFtBDK1xxy50N5SzDExxrZOyafRFqtw3bIGYFJtpD02xq5wwXjUIggV6NamfCqPpZ82wjltnjVUjk3hOwO7VKE-gKJzY1zFQeoQAew1s-EzfbhGWOBjuurfh2BToUUZ2eenSgMGGg87f-yRh8-Oorlse9uHjZuPN8wfw3X05KebzfyQ/s1884/ioda.png" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="912" data-original-width="1884" height="310" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEiojxzNrYRBWiInFtBDK1xxy50N5SzDExxrZOyafRFqtw3bIGYFJtpD02xq5wwXjUIggV6NamfCqPpZ82wjltnjVUjk3hOwO7VKE-gKJzY1zFQeoQAew1s-EzfbhGWOBjuurfh2BToUUZ2eenSgMGGg87f-yRh8-Oorlse9uHjZuPN8wfw3X05KebzfyQ/w640-h310/ioda.png" width="640" /></a></div><br /><p><br /></p><p>3. Before the destructive payload was executed, there was any other kind of malicious code running in the modems for a short period of time? Sentinelone published a very interesting <a href="https://www.sentinelone.com/labs/acidrain-a-modem-wiper-rains-down-on-europe/" target="_blank">research</a> on 'AcidRain', a wiper that is able to generate the same destructive pattern observed in the modem's flash memory.&nbsp;</p><p></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEiYa7R8_SpQfspA3RLP93a6vehVYeA6NuDK0KWjIXZhd-FKCeWE7iNwahn8HfMjmJHKIeTapX-DXzy2_np8wtaowJ-7YE2KnUxBypP3kyPBMae0-LspR85JC7iuOZuahQPHqojMiMYbEhhGuTJLL-I1gkJLxJTTqRU3dWpvHm9-Y_Xu8-r-nA7Xs0GOug" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="" data-original-height="266" data-original-width="642" height="133" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEiYa7R8_SpQfspA3RLP93a6vehVYeA6NuDK0KWjIXZhd-FKCeWE7iNwahn8HfMjmJHKIeTapX-DXzy2_np8wtaowJ-7YE2KnUxBypP3kyPBMae0-LspR85JC7iuOZuahQPHqojMiMYbEhhGuTJLL-I1gkJLxJTTqRU3dWpvHm9-Y_Xu8-r-nA7Xs0GOug" width="320" /></a></div><br /><p></p><p>Coincidentally, this wiper also has similarities with 'VPNfilter' malware.</p><p>4. Did the compromise of the management segment involve additional attacks besides the VPN issue?</p><p>Unfortunately these technical questions can only be answered by people with an insider knowledge. Let's see if Viasat is willing to provide further details on this case.</p><p><br /></p><p><b style="background-color: red;"><span style="color: white;">* Updated&nbsp; - The VPN Attack vector*&nbsp;</span></b></p><p>Viasat has not elaborated the VPN attack vector yet, but they acknowledged to journalists that the attack originated from the Internet. Viasat is also distancing itself from the fiasco by directly pointing to Skylogic and its ground infrastructure.</p><p>Although we're entering again the land of speculation, there are some factual bits that should be considered.</p><p>A simple recon of Skylogic's ground network (<a href="https://bgp.he.net/AS201935" target="_blank">AS201935</a>) reveals a couple of interesting things:</p><p>1. Skylogic relies on Fortigate appliances</p><p></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEiFnCn41pcfngE2Vo-FWIKOG_eEyb5fTkQQZXs4epIwOQ7TeABcmeGgYIL7iteNwpCiB8kLcoPpNAMUtfLiCzt-7d30jM0LtxnSKiJDO23BB-3C0apwSGQ-AErxzOI_dWvziJczYsaeRrryXFdNR9E0-RBHGLqOSjDipDwWIMsCr6ZZhIfVx_B9mKmcWw" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img data-original-height="666" data-original-width="1582" height="270" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEiFnCn41pcfngE2Vo-FWIKOG_eEyb5fTkQQZXs4epIwOQ7TeABcmeGgYIL7iteNwpCiB8kLcoPpNAMUtfLiCzt-7d30jM0LtxnSKiJDO23BB-3C0apwSGQ-AErxzOI_dWvziJczYsaeRrryXFdNR9E0-RBHGLqOSjDipDwWIMsCr6ZZhIfVx_B9mKmcWw=w640-h270" width="640" /></a></div><br /><p></p><p><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span><span>&nbsp; &nbsp; 'cgl-fw02' may be indicating the Skylogic's Cagliari teleport.</span><br /></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjaFhx8fA7hpIPcZ7o02Llod9vyLFcayXoa-se9_GfMw8tCcznTfCOJWEev9lkmENjxjDZmj5xWXd3eTvXHEEdxKfefjOdZGYSmckKAnrrlAZAbi3vA7eOhvVHSrwgXaT1YVl7UI7zRAgyV_BESX802T0-XZ-9RFATAEHYo7IlrPQU52L04K4jpY_j11A/s1482/cagliari.png" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="776" data-original-width="1482" height="336" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjaFhx8fA7hpIPcZ7o02Llod9vyLFcayXoa-se9_GfMw8tCcznTfCOJWEev9lkmENjxjDZmj5xWXd3eTvXHEEdxKfefjOdZGYSmckKAnrrlAZAbi3vA7eOhvVHSrwgXaT1YVl7UI7zRAgyV_BESX802T0-XZ-9RFATAEHYo7IlrPQU52L04K4jpY_j11A/w640-h336/cagliari.png" width="640" /></a></div><p>2.&nbsp; The route <a href="https://bgp.he.net/AS201935#_graph4" target="_blank">propagation</a> matches the <a href="https://ioda.caida.org/ioda/dashboard#view=inspect&amp;entity=asn/29286&amp;lastView=overview&amp;from=1645628090&amp;until=1645800890" target="_blank">attack</a>. Viasat's statement explicitly mentions that the attacker moved laterally until reaching the management network.</p><p><br /></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhE5NDYEssERc56erpsFqNO9BMSb3BbLQ_TZ_T27kTdLlYxQ0zy53uxDlmDI25cSUXZL8YvFUc2MOcEIAaddWxk2NxWlAoVeHasj4T2HZ7T6h4u1w8frKJWbKnHLXnbXfLA1NhUWdOQkL3JZLJcT0oo8Ao3vkwqNiVT_enzNvN1iaasdYhHp28tzOpbUA/s1550/toowayservice.png" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="1158" data-original-width="1550" height="478" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhE5NDYEssERc56erpsFqNO9BMSb3BbLQ_TZ_T27kTdLlYxQ0zy53uxDlmDI25cSUXZL8YvFUc2MOcEIAaddWxk2NxWlAoVeHasj4T2HZ7T6h4u1w8frKJWbKnHLXnbXfLA1NhUWdOQkL3JZLJcT0oo8Ao3vkwqNiVT_enzNvN1iaasdYhHp28tzOpbUA/w640-h478/toowayservice.png" width="640" /></a></div><br /><p></p><p><br /></p><p>It is also worth mentioning that, in 2021, there were different&nbsp;<a href="https://www.fortinet.com/blog/psirt-blogs/malicious-actor-discloses-fortigate-ssl-vpn-credentials" target="_blank">attack campaigns</a>&nbsp;and <a href="https://gist.github.com/crypto-cypher/f216d6fa4816ffa93c5270b001dc4bdc" target="_blank">leaks</a>&nbsp;targeting Fortinet VPN appliances. These attacks were carried out by groups of malicious actors that exploited multiple vulnerabilities that were discovered in these products.</p><p>Viasat's statement mentions a 'misconfigured VPN appliance', so if we consider that&nbsp;this definition may be a euphemism for an 'unpatched VPN appliance', then we may have a plausible attack vector. It is also possible that malicious actors may have previously collected valid VPN credentials as a result of these attacks.&nbsp;</p><p>Another interesting aspect that Viasat implicitly introduces in its statement is the potential security weaknesses that may be derived from the complexity of wholesale operations for a Satellite infrastructure.&nbsp; Down in this chain we find ground station operators, satellite service providers, distributors , resellers...</p><p>At some point they all need certain kind of access to provide their services, so this integration also may pose a challenge in terms of security. For instance, a publicly exposed server provides a glimpse of the Eutelsat's partners API capabilities.</p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEi6JhzuRJHCIZn7wsme3HGiVMWgm-JVhpV1fi_DuwRIznfOgN_93MS9yYLHSzZVzCfXBs7K2SIDPaDjk1_5UtnT1lsoxu16IS-rZniV34dJZy5aYydY5rQjUvol7SIxc8Q-zDLP33znRVGgEWtPiDUAWzJejCSFEqQeuXIlkX8IbwwE3KFGMF7_D-b3xA/s2902/wholesaleapi.png" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="1588" data-original-width="2902" height="350" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEi6JhzuRJHCIZn7wsme3HGiVMWgm-JVhpV1fi_DuwRIznfOgN_93MS9yYLHSzZVzCfXBs7K2SIDPaDjk1_5UtnT1lsoxu16IS-rZniV34dJZy5aYydY5rQjUvol7SIxc8Q-zDLP33znRVGgEWtPiDUAWzJejCSFEqQeuXIlkX8IbwwE3KFGMF7_D-b3xA/w640-h350/wholesaleapi.png" width="640" /></a></div><p>In general terms, it is also recommended to not expose an operator's desktop in corporate videos. It usually leaks information that may facilitate different kinds of attacks.</p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEgPQRjC2RnB99QoKRMiPbG4seVL-FhBwxqeQldE17T8M5uOh_ugRb5cvBtQU2GDn-U2eOpp0vKKVkLPruxi6YvBajh3UlCVFGNKFHH1hcpBaAwR_3EDu7wUbPbdqqCFc-DONdzuCE3YOhU0d3KvQYf3kxswhglZOXwac2FJWTwZcN5B3JxT4RJGs1-Z-Q/s3320/screenshot.png" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="1858" data-original-width="3320" height="358" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEgPQRjC2RnB99QoKRMiPbG4seVL-FhBwxqeQldE17T8M5uOh_ugRb5cvBtQU2GDn-U2eOpp0vKKVkLPruxi6YvBajh3UlCVFGNKFHH1hcpBaAwR_3EDu7wUbPbdqqCFc-DONdzuCE3YOhU0d3KvQYf3kxswhglZOXwac2FJWTwZcN5B3JxT4RJGs1-Z-Q/w640-h358/screenshot.png" width="640" /></a></div><br /><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br /></div><br /></div></div>
