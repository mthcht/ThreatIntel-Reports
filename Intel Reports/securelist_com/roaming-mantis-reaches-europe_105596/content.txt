																							<h2>Part VI. 2021 sees smishing and modified Wroba.g/Wroba.o extend attacks to Germany and France </h2>											
											<div class="js-reading-content">
												<div class="c-wysiwyg">
													<p>Roaming Mantis is a malicious campaign that targets Android devices and spreads mobile malware via <a href="https://encyclopedia.kaspersky.com/glossary/smishing/?utm_source=securelist&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=termin-explanation">smishing</a>. We have been tracking Roaming Mantis since 2018, and published five blog posts about this campaign:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://securelist.com/roaming-mantis-uses-dns-hijacking-to-infect-android-smartphones/85178/">Roaming Mantis uses DNS hijacking to infect Android smartphones</a></li>
<li><a href="https://securelist.com/roaming-mantis-dabbles-in-mining-and-phishing-multilingually/85607/">Roaming Mantis dabbles in mining and phishing multilingually</a></li>
<li><a href="https://securelist.com/roaming-mantis-part-3/88071/">Roaming Mantis, part III</a></li>
<li><a href="https://securelist.com/roaming-mantis-part-iv/90332/">Roaming Mantis, part IV</a></li>
<li><a href="https://securelist.com/roaming-mantis-part-v/96250/">Roaming Mantis, part V</a></li>
</ul>
<p>It&#8217;s been a while since the last blog post, but we&#8217;ve observed some new activities by Roaming Mantis in 2021, and some changes in the Android Trojan Wroba.g (or Wroba.o, a.k.a Moqhao, XLoader) that&#8217;s mainly used in this campaign. Furthermore, we discovered that France and Germany were added as primary targets of Roaming Mantis, in addition to Japan, Taiwan and Korea.</p>
<h2 id="geography-of-roaming-mantis-victims">Geography of Roaming Mantis victims</h2>
<p>Our latest research into Roaming Mantis shows that the actor is focusing on expanding infection via smishing to users in Europe. The campaign in France and Germany was so active that it came to the attention of the <a href="https://www.polizei-praevention.de/aktuelles/sms-mit-paketbenachrichtigungslink-verursacht-massenhafte-sms.html">German police</a> and <a href="https://cyberguerre.numerama.com/11670-votre-colis-a-ete-envoye-attention-a-ce-sms-il-cache-un-malware.html">French</a> media. They alerted users about smishing messages and the compromised websites used as landing pages.</p>

If a user clicks on the link and opens the landing page, there are two scenarios: iOS users are redirected to a phishing page imitating the official Apple website, while the Wroba malware is downloaded on Android devices.</p>

<h2 id="anti-researcher-tricks-in-the-landing-page">Anti-researcher tricks in the landing page</h2>
<p>Throughout 2020 and 2021, the criminal group behind Roaming Mantis made use of various obfuscation techniques in the landing page script in order to evade detection.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-105603" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-1024x624.png" alt="Variety of obfuscation techniques in the landing page script" width="1024" height="624" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-1024x624.png 1024w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-300x183.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-768x468.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-330x200.png 330w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-287x175.png 287w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-370x226.png 370w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-459x280.png 459w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04-800x488.png 800w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132125/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_04.png 1135w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Variety of obfuscation techniques in the landing page script</em></strong></p>
<p>In addition to obfuscation, the landing page blocks the connection from the source IP address in non-targeted regions and shows just a fake &#8220;404&#8221; page for these connections.</p>
<p>The user agent checking feature has not been changed in the landing page since 2019; it evaluates the devices by user agent, redirecting to the phishing page if the device is iOS-based, or delivering the malicious APK file if the device is Android-based.</p>
<h2 id="technical-analysis-loader-module-of-wroba-g-wroba-o">Technical analysis: loader module of Wroba.g/Wroba.o</h2>
<p>We performed in-depth analysis of Wroba.g/Wroba.o samples and observed several modifications in the loader module and payload, using <em>kuronekoyamato.apk</em> as an example. First, the actor changed the programming language from Java to Kotlin, a programming language designed to interoperate fully with Java. Then, the actor removed the multidex obfuscation trick. Instead of this, the data structure of the embedded payload (assetsrmocpdx15k7a5q) was also modified as follows:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-105604" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05.png" alt="Modified data structure of embedded payload" width="873" height="210" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05.png 873w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05-300x72.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05-768x185.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05-728x175.png 728w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05-370x89.png 370w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132212/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_05-800x192.png 800w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 873px) 100vw, 873px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Modified data structure of embedded payload</em></strong></p>
<p>The first eight bytes of the data are junk code (gray), followed by the size of payload (orange), a single-byte XOR key (red), the encrypted payload (green) and more junk code (gray). Furthermore, an ELF file, libarmeaib-v7alibdf.so, was embedded in the APK file: it uses Java Native Interface (JNI) for the second stage payload, for decryption and also part of the loading feature. The decryption process and algorithms are just three steps as follows:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-105605" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06.png" alt="Various obfuscation techniques in the landing page script" width="896" height="438" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06.png 896w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06-300x147.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06-768x375.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06-358x175.png 358w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06-370x181.png 370w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06-573x280.png 573w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132613/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_06-800x391.png 800w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 896px) 100vw, 896px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Various obfuscation techniques in the landing page script</em></strong></p>
<p>First, the loader function takes each section of data from the embedded data, except the junk data. Then, the encrypted payload is XORed using the embedded XOR key. After the XOR operation, as with previous samples, the data is decompressed using zlib to extract the payload, a Dalvik Executable (DEX) file.</p>

<h2 id="technical-analysis-payload-of-wroba-g-wroba-o">Technical analysis: payload of Wroba.g/Wroba.o</h2>
<p>Regarding the updates to the Wroba.g/Wroba.o payload, Kaspersky experts only observed two minor updates in the payload part. One of them is the feature for checking the region of the infected device in order to display a phishing page in the corresponding language. In the old sample, it checked for three regions: Hong Kong, Taiwan and Japan. However, Germany and France were added as new regions. From this update, together with the map above, it is clear that Germany and France have become the main targets of Roaming Mantis with Wroba.g/Wroba.o.</p>
<p>Another modification is in the backdoor commands. The developer added two backdoor commands, &#8220;get_photo&#8221; and &#8220;get_gallery&#8221;, as well as removing the command &#8220;show_fs_float_window&#8221;. Overall, there are 21 embedded backdoor commands.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-105606" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-1024x705.png" alt="List of embedded backdoor commands with the two new commands 'get_gallery' and 'get_photo'" width="1024" height="705" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-1024x705.png 1024w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-300x206.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-768x529.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-254x175.png 254w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-370x255.png 370w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-407x280.png 407w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07-800x551.png 800w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2022/01/24132741/Roaming_Mantis_part_VI_07.png 1068w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>List of embedded backdoor commands with the two new commands &#8216;get_gallery&#8217; and &#8216;get_photo&#8217;</em></strong></p>
<p>These new backdoor commands are added to steal galleries and photos from infected devices. This suggests the criminals have two aims in mind. One possible scenario is that the criminals steal details from such things as driver&#8217;s licenses, health insurance cards or bank cards, to sign up for contracts with QR code payment services or mobile payment services. The criminals are also able to use stolen photos to get money in other ways, such as blackmail or sextortion. The other functions of the payload are unchanged. For more details, please see our previous blogposts mentioned above.</p>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>It has been almost four years since Kaspersky first observed the Roaming Mantis campaign. Since then, the criminal group has continued its attack activities by using various malware families such as HEUR:Trojan-Dropper.AndroidOS.Wroba, and various attack methods such as phishing, mining, smishing and DNS poisoning. In addition, the group has now expanded its geography, adding two European countries to its main target regions. We predict these attacks will continue in 2022 because of the strong financial motivation.</p>
