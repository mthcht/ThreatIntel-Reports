class="su-heading su-heading-style-default su-heading-align-center" id style=font-size:13px;margin-bottom:20px><div
class=su-heading-inner><strong>A taste of the latest release of QakBot &#8211; one of the most popular and mediatic trojan bankers active since 2007.</strong></div></div><p
style="text-align: justify;">The malware <strong><a
href=https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.qakbot>QakBot</a></strong>, also known as Qbot, Pinkslipbot, and Quakbot is a banking trojan that has been made headlines since 2007. This piece of malware is focused on stealing banking credentials and victim&#8217;s secrets using different techniques tactics and procedures (TTP) which have evolved over the years, including its delivery mechanisms, C2 techniques, and anti-analysis and reversing features.</p><p
style="text-align: justify;">Emotet is known as the most popular threat distributing QakBot in the wild, nonetheless, Emotet has been taken down recently, and QakBot operators are using specially target campaigns to disseminate this threat around the globe. Figure 1 shows two email templates distributing QakBot in Portugal in early May 2021.</p><p><a
href=https://seguranca-informatica.pt/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/email-template-qbot-portugal.png><img

style="text-align: justify;">Additionally, QakBot is able to move laterally on the internal environment for stealing sensitive data, making internal persistence, or even for deploying other final payloads like ransomware. In recent <a
href=https://www.hornetsecurity.com/en/security-information/qakbot-malspam-leading-to-prolock/ >reports</a>, it could be used to drop other malware such as ProLock and Egregor ransomware. At the moment, and after the Emotet takedown, QakBot becoming one the most prominent and observed threats allowing criminals to gain a foothold on internal networks. In the next workflow, we can learn how the QakBot infection chain works.</p><p
style="text-align: center;"><a
href=https://seguranca-informatica.pt/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/modus_operandi__qakbot.jpg><img
decoding=async class="size-full wp-image-9007 aligncenter" src=https://seguranca-informatica.pt/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/modus_operandi__qakbot.jpg alt width=651 height=429 srcset="https://seguranca-informatica.pt/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/modus_operandi__qakbot.jpg 651w, https://seguranca-informatica.pt/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/modus_operandi__qakbot-300x198.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 651px) 100vw, 651px"></a><em><strong><br>
Figure 2: </strong>High-level diagram of QakBot malware and its capabilities.</em></p><p>&nbsp;</p><p
style="text-align: justify;">QakBot is disseminated these days using target phishing campaigns in several languages, including Portuguese. The infection chain starts with an URL in the email body that downloads a zip archive containing an XLM or XLSM file (Excel) that takes advantage of XLM 4.0 macros to download the 2nd stage from the compromised web servers.</p><p

style="text-align: justify;">QakBot is a sophisticated trojan designed to collect banking information from victims&#8217; devices. This piece of malware is targeting mostly US organizations and it is equipped with a variety of evasion and info-stealing routines as well as worm-like functions to make it persistent. In <a
href=https://www.hornetsecurity.com/en/security-information/qakbot-malspam-leading-to-prolock/ >recent reports</a>, it could be used to drop other malware such as ProLock, Egregor ransomware.</p><p
style="text-align: justify;">QakBot is a challenging threat with capabilities to avoid dynamic analysis in automatic sandboxes with the delayed executions present in its dropper as well as other tricks.
