					<h1 class="entry-title">An insider insights into Conti operations &#8211; Part one</h1><p class="et_pb_title_meta_container"><span class="published">17 Aug, 2021</span></p>
				</div>
				
			</div>
			</div>
				
				
				
				
			</div><div class="et_pb_row et_pb_row_2_tb_body">

				<div class="et_pb_row et_pb_row_0">

				<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_0  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is the first of two blog posts, where we focus on the Conti ransomware group whose training material was recently leaked on a cybercrime forum. To provide some context to this analysis, we describe Conti&#8217;s evolution and success since its origin. We then contextualize the leaks thanks to our observations on underground forums and analyze it in terms of threat intelligence. The second blog post will give some details on the techniques used by Conti operators and how to detect them.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></h3>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">The origin of Conti ransomware</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Conti and Ryuk were developed and operated by a group dubbed Wizard Spider by CrowdStrike (aka UNC1878, Grim Spider, Conti gang) and some affiliates. Wizard Spider started its activity in 2016 by conducting financial fraud campaigns using the TrickBot banking trojan<a href="https://www.crowdstrike.com/blog/wizard-spider-adversary-update/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">¹</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">. The link between Conti and Wizard Spider was confirmed by Clearsky, following a bitcoin transaction after a successful ransomware attack<a href="https://www.clearskysec.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/Conti-Ransomware.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">²</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">In August 2018, the actor previously using TrickBot started to use a new ransomware called Ryuk to target large organizations, asking for high ransom amounts. Wizard Spider seemed to follow the Big Game Hunting (BGH) trend started by BitPaymer’s gang one year earlier<a href="https://www.crowdstrike.com/blog/big-game-hunting-the-evolution-of-indrik-spider-from-dridex-wire-fraud-to-bitpaymer-targeted-ransomware/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">³</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Ryuk&#8217;s activity made the project famous in the ransomware business. According to Coveware, in Q1 2020, the average ransomware payment on behalf of the group was over $1.3 million. During this period, we can note the attacks against major US companies such as Electronic Warfare Associates (EWA), a US Government contractor<a href="https://www.trendmicro.com/vinfo/us/security/news/cybercrime-and-digital-threats/ryuk-ransomware-infects-us-government-contractor" target="_blank" rel="noopener">⁴</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_0 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img fetchpriority="high" decoding="async" width="750" height="496" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20750%20496'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-srcset="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image6.png.webp 750w,https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image6-480x317.png.webp 480w" data-lazy-sizes="(min-width: 0px) and (max-width: 480px) 480px, (min-width: 481px) 750px, 100vw" class="wp-image-4226" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image6.png.webp" /><noscript><img fetchpriority="high" decoding="async" width="750" height="496" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image6.png.webp" alt="" title="" srcset="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image6.png.webp 750w,https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image6-480x317.png.webp 480w" sizes="(min-width: 0px) and (max-width: 480px) 480px, (min-width: 481px) 750px, 100vw" class="wp-image-4226" /></noscript></span>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_1  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Average ransomware payment of Phobos, Ryuk &amp; Sodinokibi in Q4 2019 and Q1 2020<a href="https://www.coveware.com/blog/q1-2020-ransomware-marketplace-report" target="_blank" rel="noopener">⁵</a></span></i></p></div>
			</div>
			</div>
				
				
				
				
			</div><div class="et_pb_row et_pb_row_1">
				<div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_4_4 et_pb_column_1  et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough et-last-child">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_2  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The group has constantly evolved its arsenal, reacting to attempts to block them. In 2020, they developed BazarLoader, which has a high level of obfuscation. They also regularly added known vulnerabilities such as Eternal Blue, Zerologon, and more recently, PrintNightmare to their arsenal. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Their loaders were often delivered through phishing email or credentials previously obtained from Emotet or IcedID activity. The affiliates probably used accesses sold by initial access brokers.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Conti appears in February 2020 as a Ryuk successor using the new data blackmailing technique<a href="https://www.areteir.com/is-conti-the-new-ryuk/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">⁶</a> </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">(aka double extortion technique). They created a website to publish stolen data in case of non-payment of the ransom and to have a good-looking chat interface to communicate with victims and others. According to ransom notes SEKOIA studied, Ryuk operators used to communicate with victims through secure email services such as Protonmail or Tutanota. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Threatening to leak sensitive files is great to increase the pressure on companies and therefore increase the probability that the ransom will be paid and to increase its amount. Note that Wizard Spider seems to consider the file decryptor and the deletion of the stolen files as two different services.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_1 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image17.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image17.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>

				<div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_4_4 et_pb_column_2  et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough et-last-child">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_5  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The double extortion technique used by Conti has apparently paid off: the group claims more than 150 successful attacks and $20M of paid revenue by the end of 2020<a href="https://www.clearskysec.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/Conti-Ransomware.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">⁷</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Based on our observations, Conti is the most prolific group since January 2021, with more than 300 publicly disclosed ransomware attacks this year.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">This success is partly due to the efficiency of the group&#8217;s tools. Indeed, in 2020, the Conti ransomware was one of the fastest to encrypt a computer by running 32 concurrent threads, using AES-256 keys bundled with a RSA-4096 public key. The encryption and data exfiltration speed has since been a marketing argument in the ransomware community and was greatly improved by other groups. One of the Conti ransomware specificities is that it can be used in command line to encrypt the local hard drive or network shares. </span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_3 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image1-1.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image1-1.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_6  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Comparative table created by LockBit 2.0 group (available on their website)</span></i></p></div>
			</div>
			</div>
				
				
				
				
			</div><div class="et_pb_row et_pb_row_3">
				<div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_4_4 et_pb_column_3  et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough et-last-child">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_7  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><span>Another specificity that indicates a continuity between the activities of Ryuk and Conti is Ryuk&#8217;s habit of demanding ransom payments proportional to the revenues of the targeted company that has continued with the Conti ransomware. Once their affiliates compromise a </span>target, they send the operators a report containing information about the victim (name, website address, number of servers and endpoint locked, amount of stolen data, and target’s revenue) to help during the ransom negotiation.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Conti’s negotiators are experienced and patient. They use the anchor technique by setting a very high first price and negotiating it. They use a service-oriented rhetoric, calling the victim “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">customer</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” and themself “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">support</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">”.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Unlike other ransomware gangs, Conti did not hold back from attacking the hospitals during the COVID-19 crisis⁸</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></h2>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></h2>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">An internal discord at the origin of the Conti&#8217;s training material leaks</span></h2>
<h3 style="text-align: justify;"></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">On August 5, 2021, a XSS</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> cybercrime forum member known as “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">m1Geelka</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” leaked a Conti ransomware group’s training material. The “manual” includes some insights on the modus operandi of one of the most successful ransomware cartels at the moment.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">From our observations of the discussions between different actors, “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">m1Geelka</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” is </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">believed to be one of the Windows Administrators of the group. After allegedly working for Conti (or as he states, “I got in there to find out how they work”)</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">, “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">m1Geelka</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” judged the remuneration formula to be unfair and decided to do justice to the group’s &#8220;</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">partners</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">”. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">In fact, the cybercriminal community has repeatedly voiced that Conti&#8217;s alleged remuneration was inadequate, given the qualifications they are looking for. Starting from $1,500 and from $2,000 for technical profiles, wages are, however, constantly being adjusted upwards, and accompanied by regular bonuses, all paid in BTC &#8211; the group states. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Even so, “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">m1Geelka</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” was promptly expelled from the Russian-speaking underground community. He has broken one unwritten rule that dictates the law in this medium: conflicts have to be solved through private arbitration processes.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">This has also driven us to pay close attention to the most recent activity of a Conti representative on a forum where he was particularly active from June to August 2021. “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">IT_Work</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” is the online persona of a suspected threat actor who handled the group&#8217;s expansion this summer.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_4 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image7.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image7.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_8  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Conti’s representative profile on XSS forum, banned since August 6, 2021</span></i></p></div>

			</div><div class="et_pb_row et_pb_row_4">
				<div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_4_4 et_pb_column_4  et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough et-last-child">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_9  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">As commercial activities related to ransomware are now prohibited on this platform, no advertisements for the used software, nor any specifications about the targeted countries or industries were seen. Instead, we observed a massive recruitment campaign on the XSS cybercriminal forum which is highly popular among ransomware operators seeking to create new partnerships. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">“</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">We are a small recruitment team</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” &#8211; “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">IT_Works</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” states. He announced “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">lots of vacancies</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” in June 2021, so candidates were encouraged to apply for job openings or to make spontaneous applications. “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">No formalization under the Labor Code</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">” is stipulated, as to comfort some and prevent others. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">There were over a dozen job openings on behalf of Conti spotted in less than two months, revealing a highly specialized and organized group structure. As commonly observed among ransomware groups originating from Russia or countries within the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS), the communication is performed in Russian and the job listings are addressed to “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Russian speakers only!</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">”.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_5 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image16.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image16.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_10  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">An example of a job listing posted by a Conti representative on June 11 on a Russian-speaking cybercriminal forum. Translated from Russian, the post reads: </span></i><i><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span></i><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“</span></i><b><i>Vacancy for the position of Business Analysts</i></b><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">. </span></i><b><i>Required Skills</i></b><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">: Business English (reading) required, conversational is a big plus; Knowledge of business particularities in the U.S.; Analytical skills, attention to detail; Advanced PC user. </span></i><b><i>Responsibilities</i></b><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">: Analysis of B2B markets; Analysis of companies&#8217; financial statements, and other public data obtained from government institutions; Drawing up cases on key players on the financial and industrial markets [&#8230;]”</span></i></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_11  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A close look at these messages allows us to draw up a rough picture of the Conti’s internal structure, which can be imaged as follows:</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_6 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image2-1.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image2-1.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>

				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">An overview of how the Conti Group is structured, based on statements recently made by its representatives on different cybercriminal forums</span></i></p></div>
			</div>

				<div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_4_4 et_pb_column_5  et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough et-last-child">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_13  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is slightly out of the ordinary job offers that other ransomware groups publish (most often for pentester positions). Of particular curiosity is the position of Asterisk </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Administrator. Based on a July 2021 announcement by Conti, a dedicated Asterisk VoIP service was in development, probably to initiate phone conversations with the victims or the victim’s partners or employees, in order to put more pressure on them. Threat actors are particularly interested in the “Auto Redial” feature of the Asterisk framework to put the phone number on automatic dial repeatedly, until the called party picks up the phone.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The group is also looking for Web Designers with “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">really creative ideas</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“ and UI/UX Designers “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">to design layouts of websites and individual user interfaces of web applications</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">”.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">To study potential victim’s activity or to analyze the already attacked ones, Business Analysts are wanted. They must be proficient in English and know the business particularities in the U.S, have good analytical skills and “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">pay attention to detail</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">”. Business Analysts working for Conti prospect the B2B market, collect and analyze companies&#8217; financial statements and other public data obtained from government institutions, and they are also drawing up cases on key players on the financial and industrial markets, according to “</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">IT_Works</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">”.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is also quite unique is the well-structured corporate approach Conti adopted: their &#8220;</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">partners</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8221; have paid vacations and sick leaves. They usually have a 3pm-1am, Monday to Friday work schedule, remote only.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_14  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What do the Conti leaks tell us?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Following the first leak release, we decided to analyze its content and tried to assess how useful it could be in terms of threat intelligence and detection.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The archive, retrieved by vx-underground<a href="https://vxug.fakedoma.in/tmp/ManualsAndSoftware.rar" target="_blank" rel="noopener">⁹</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">, contains a majority of text files written in russian, a few archives, binaries, scripts, and softwares (</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">e.g.</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Cobalt Strike 4.3, Router Scan), and what looks like an unstructured manual explaining to Conti affiliates how to operate.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_7 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image5.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image5.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_15  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Archive content</span></i></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_16  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A public quick English translation<a href="https://github.com/silence-is-best/files/blob/main/translate_f.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">¹⁰</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> has been made available to organize the leak with three main attack steps: Increasing privileges and information collection, Uploading data and Lock. For each step, each manual provides one or more tools/techniques to reach the objective with a kind of best practices approach and a collection of the best tools to use.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">There is no new tool or technique to discover, everything is quite old and renowned (</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">e.g.</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Mimikatz) and should already be detected. Although they are up-to-date with the latest vulnerability and have a manual for “PrintNightmare” (CVE-2021-34527, that is still not fixed by a proper patch from Microsoft). Obviously they also try to use Microsoft built-in tools as much as possible to blend-in with legitimate activities in order to avoid detection (</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">e.g.</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> powershell, wmi).</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Some recommendations are made in terms of operational security in the manual files translated as “Anonymity for the paranoid” and “Personal safety” (not available in the public English translation):</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_with_border et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_17  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A couple of notes on posts about anonymity for the paranoid:</span></p>
<ol style="text-align: justify;">
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;"> The task is not to hide (it still won&#8217;t work), but to merge with the crowd. So by disabling webrtc, Javascript, Flash, etc. just attract more attention to yourself. You should NOT DISCONNECT, but CHANGE what allows you to be detected.</span></li>
</ol>
<ol start="2" style="text-align: justify;">
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Concerning Kali and other operating systems for hackers. There is a group of people (Hackers) that needs to be tracked. Technically, this problem is difficult to solve. It&#8217;s easier to play on human weakness (laziness) and gather everyone together by providing a properly advertised, convenient, ready-made and popular solution. I think the idea is clear. I advise you to use Debian or build something of your own.</span></li>
</ol></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_with_border et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_18  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">I think everyone here works through a virtual machine. Therefore, I advise you to install the virtual machine on the encrypted volume using VeraCrypt.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">1 download Veracrypt</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">2 you will need to allocate space on your disk for a file / or encrypt the entire disk at once</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">An important rule is that you will have to install the virtual machine again, because, unfortunately, when you encrypt your old working virtual machine, an insurmountable error will appear in the code and it will no longer start. This is not a big problem, because you can get all your files from the image of your old virtual machine via 7ZIP.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_19  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Because they are hunting for administrator accounts, they are also cautious about their reaction. In the following extract from the “Hunting admins, please read, very useful !!” manual, a very explicit warning is made to dissuade an attacker to directly login to a computer session of an administrator:</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_with_border et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_20  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Next is an IMPORTANT POINT.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">First of all, beginners try to raise a session there and VERY OFTEN catch an alert. Alert at the admin = cutting out of the network, loss of time, nerves. Do not do this!</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">What we&#8217;re going to do is poll it through the file system.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_21  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">But at the same time they could go for a live brute force of accounts when required, and as noticed by other researchers<a href="https://twitter.com/TheDFIRReport/status/1423365874585640961" target="_blank" rel="noopener">¹¹</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">, use the same example directory (</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">i.e.</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> “ProgramData”) for all their command outputs which probably leads to a lot of copy/paste.</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_8 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image9.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image9.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_22  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“ProgramData” directory usage example</span></i></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_23  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Their discovery, collection and exfiltration methods match a lot with other ransomware groups and most of the time the techniques described aim for efficiency more than stealthiness.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Overall the archive reveals an interesting insider look into some ransomware attack operations where the attackers look for the weakest points of defense. It also confirms that most of the techniques from these groups are known and give lots of detection opportunities. This should again remind us as defenders where to focus on.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The second leak<a href="https://twitter.com/vxunderground/status/1426422201574694915" target="_blank" rel="noopener">¹²</a></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> was not as useful: these are 27GBs of mainly tutorial video files from several sources (free or paying ones), teaching about penetration testing (</span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">e.g.</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Metasploit, Cobalt Strike, network) and reverse engineering. Some sources are in English while others are in Russian but none seem specific to Conti. Indeed, the content of this leak confirms that Conti recruiters are also looking for beginners who will then be trained by following these tutorials, as indicated on a cybercriminal forum:</span></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_image et_pb_image_9 et_pb_image_sticky">
				
				
				
				
				<span class="et_pb_image_wrap "><img decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" title="" data-lazy-src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image8.png" /><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://www.sekoia.io/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/image8.png" alt="" title="" /></noscript></span>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_24  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p style="text-align: center;"><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Translated from Russian, the post reads: </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">“Recruitment of pentesters continues. [&#8230;] Whether you&#8217;re a professional or a beginner, it doesn&#8217;t matter. We have an individual approach to everyone. We will teach and help, we need guys who want to progress in a long-term cooperation!”.</span></em></p></div>
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_25  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
				
				
				
				
				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2 style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Conclusion</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Conti leaks are a great source of knowledge to find out more about how ransomware cartels operate overall. It gives good insights into how they handle their operations, how they are organized and the techniques being used in these operations. This will likely raise the interest of other threat actors who might enter the ransomware scene by embracing a modus operandi that, up until now at least, has worked very well.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the second part of this blog series we will cover some techniques used by Conti in the first leak and the detection opportunities for each one.
