            <div>The Splunk Threat Research Team is monitoring open channel intelligence and government alerts indicating the possibility of malicious campaigns using destructive software in relation to ongoing geopolitical events. Based on historical data of named geopolitical actors, the use of destructive payloads has been observed in past campaigns. These destructive payloads aim to disable targeted hosts beyond recovery and seek to disrupt, deny, and degrade an organization’s technology and services, especially Operational Technology which is the software and hardware directly related to the monitoring and operation of industrial systems (i.e Utilities such as telecommunications, electricity, water, gas, etc).</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <p>If recent Ransomware campaigns are an indication of the effects malicious campaigns against healthcare, technology, food supply, and gas supply can have in real life (Colonial pipeline outage affected <a href="https://nypost.com/2021/05/08/cyberattack-shuts-down-pipeline-that-supplies-east-coast-with-fuel/">45% of U.S East Coast fuel supply</a>), then destructive payloads whose sole use is to render hosts unusable should be considered a possibility under the current geopolitical indicators.</p>
        <p><strong>The Attack:</strong> The focus of this threat advisory is on a recently reported destructive payload by <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/security/blog/2022/01/15/destructive-malware-targeting-ukrainian-organizations/">Microsoft MSTIC</a> under the name of WhisperGate. We break down the different components and functions of how this payload works and provide a series of detections to mitigate and defend against this threat.</p>
        <p>Although we cannot prevent patient 0, we can, however, measure and recover execution artifacts which if used timely and operationalized as analytics and playbooks can provide analysts a tool to isolate, contain and prevent further damage. Further on, this data may help understand the extent and the TTPs of current and future campaigns where these payloads may be in use.</p>
        <p>Ransomware is by itself a destructive payload, however, some past campaigns have shown the use of multiple payloads some of them with Ransomware characteristics used as decoys, and others with the same Ransomware characteristics, however, they execute destructive payloads at targeted organizations (i.e Hard disk erasure).</p>

  (Processes.process = "*ping*" Processes.process = *-n* Processes.process="* Nul*"Processes.process="*&#x26;gt;*")
  by Processes.parent_process_name Processes.parent_process Processes.process_name Processes.original_file_name Processes.process Processes.process_id Processes.process_guid Processes.user Processes.dest 
  | `drop_dm_object_name("Processes")` 
 | `security_content_ctime(firstTime)`
  |`security_content_ctime(lastTime)` 
</code></pre>

        <h2 id="mitigation">Mitigation</h2>
        <p>As outlined in CISA Alert (<a href="https://us-cert.cisa.gov/ncas/alerts/aa22-011a">AA22-011A</a>) and other <a href="https://us-cert.cisa.gov/ncas/current-activity/2022/01/18/cisa-urges-organizations-implement-immediate-cybersecurity">CISA</a> recently released a communication on how to Implement Cybersecurity Measures in order to protect against potential critical threats, here are some steps organizations can take right now in order to protect themselves.</p>
        <ul>
          <li>
            <p>Ensure software is up to date, prioritize updates that address known exploited vulnerabilities.</p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>Splunk ESCU has extensive coverage of destructive software including ransomware and crime carrier payloads. Download ESCU and perform some preventative detection and monitoring for these threats.</p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>Test, verify, and validate your perimeter defenses and remote access policies</p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>Apply equivalent security policies within your organization perimeter to your Cloud resources.</p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>Ensure there are disaster recovery, <a href="/en_us/blog/learn/business-continuity-vs-business-resilience.html">business continuity</a>, and incident response resources on standby in case of intrusion or attack.</p>
          </li>
          <li>
            <p>Follow CISA recommendations as outlined in:</p>
            <ul>
              <li><a href="https://www.cisa.gov/sites/default/files/publications/CISA_Insights-Implement_Cybersecurity_Measures_Now_to_Protect_Against_Critical_Threats_508C.pdf">https://www.cisa.gov/sites/default/files/publications/CISA_Insights-Implement_Cybersecurity_Measures_Now_to_Protect_Against_Critical_Threats_508C.pdf</a></li>
              <li><a href="https://www.cisa.gov/uscert/ncas/analysis-reports/ar21-013a">https://www.cisa.gov/uscert/ncas/analysis-reports/ar21-013a</a></li>
              <li><a href="https://www.cisa.gov/cyber-hygiene-services">https://www.cisa.gov/cyber-hygiene-services</a></li>
            </ul>
          </li>
        </ul>
