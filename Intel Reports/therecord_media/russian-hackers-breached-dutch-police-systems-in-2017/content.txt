class="article__content"><h1>Russian hackers breached Dutch police systems in 2017</h1><span class="wysiwyg-parsed-content"><p>Hackers working on behalf of Russian intelligence services breached the internal network of Dutch police in 2017 during the country&#x27;s investigation of the MH-17 crash.</p>
<p>The intrusion was kept under wraps by Dutch investigators until this week when multiple sources revealed the incident to Dutch newspaper <a href="https://www.volkskrant.nl/nieuws-achtergrond/russen-zaten-ten-tijde-van-mh17-onderzoek-door-hack-diep-in-systemen-politie~b0e044e1/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">the Volkskrant</a>.</p>
<h3 id="h-breach-occurred-via-police-academy-server">Breach occurred via Police academy server</h3>
<p>According to the newspaper&#x27;s reporting, the attack was traced back to September 2017, when Russian hackers used a vulnerability in an &quot;exotic software&quot; to breach a server belonging to the Dutch Police Academy, from where they pivoted to the main Dutch police network.</p>
<p>The intrusion was uncovered by AIVD, the Dutch intelligence service, after it saw a Dutch police IP address communicating with known malicious servers operated by Russian state-sponsored threat actors.</p>
<p>The AIVD, which <a href="https://nos.nl/nieuwsuur/artikel/2213767-dutch-intelligence-first-to-alert-u-s-about-russian-hack-of-democratic-party" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">previously breached a Russian intelligence service itself in 2014</a>, alerted Dutch police.</p>
<p>Volkskrant said that due to a lack of monitoring/logging, the AIVD and Dutch Police have very little knowledge of what the hackers did inside the police network.</p>
<p>Sources also described several moments of friction between the two Dutch agencies when it came to deciding on how to handle the intrusion and subsequent clean-up, with the AIVD wanting to keep the hackers under surveillance while police officials wanted them removed from their systems due to the possibility of compromising sensitive cases.</p>
<h3 id="h-intrusion-linked-to-russian-intelligence">Intrusion linked to Russian intelligence</h3>
<p>Volkskrant said that sources linked the 2017 Dutch police hack to <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/actor/apt_29" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">APT29 (Cozy Bear)</a>, a well-known hacking group that the <a href="https://therecord.media/white-house-formally-blames-russian-intelligence-service-svr-for-solarwinds-hack/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">White House linked earlier this year to the Russian Foreign Intelligence Service</a>, also known as the SVR.</p>
<p>However, in a subsequent article update, Volkskrant said that attribution of the hack is far from accurate, as some sources also linked the attack to <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/actor/sofacy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">APT28 (Fancy Bear)</a>, a hacking group that was previously linked to the GRU, the Russian military intelligence service.</p>
<p>Neither Dutch police nor the AIVD confirmed or commented on the newspaper&#x27;s reporting.</p>
<p>Either way, investigators were positive the attack was carried out by Russian intelligence as Dutch authorities were investigating <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines_Flight_17" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">the case of Malaysia Airlines Flight 17 (MH17)</a>, which they concluded was downed by a missile shot by rogue Russian-backed rebel forces active in eastern Ukraine.</p>
<p>During the investigation into the flight&#x27;s crash, which was led by Dutch authorities, several Dutch organizations were targeted by both APT28 and APT29 on multiple occasions.</p>
<p>As part of the Russian government&#x27;s desperate attempts at gaining insights into the investigation, Russian intelligence agents also traveled to the Netherlands to hack a police station and the <a href="https://www.government.nl/latest/news/2018/10/04/netherlands-defence-intelligence-and-security-service-disrupts-russian-cyber-operation-targeting-opcw" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW)</a>, a notorious incident for which <a href="https://therecord.media/eu-extends-sanctions-against-chinese-russian-and-n-korean-hackers-for-another-year/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">the EU imposed sanctions against</a> the Russian intelligence agents in 2020.</p>
<p>Tensions between Russia and the Netherlands remain high due to the MH-17 investigation.</p>
<p>A recent <a href="https://www.aivd.nl/onderwerpen/jaarverslagen/jaarverslag-2020/internationale-dreigingen-en-politieke-veiligheidsbelangen" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">AIVD report</a> claimed that Russia still ran influence operations in 2020, trying to discredit the findings of its MH-17 investigation.</p>
<p>Earlier this year, the Netherlands also said it discovered and <a href="https://www.aivd.nl/onderwerpen/spionage/nieuws/2020/12/10/aivd-rolt-spionagenetwerk-op-in-nederland-twee-russische-inlichtingenofficieren-moeten-het-land-verlaten" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">kicked out two SVR agents</a> working at the Russian embassy in The Hague under diplomatic accreditation.</p>
<p>Dutch officials, <a href="https://www.aivd.nl/onderwerpen/spionage/vraag-en-antwoord/waarom-is-spionage-nog-niet-strafbaar" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">led by the AIVD</a>, are also currently looking into making espionage a crime.</p>
