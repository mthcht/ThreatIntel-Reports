<p>In the last year ThreatFabric saw a radical shift in the approach towards mobile malware from criminals. Criminals have started paying closer attention to the world of Mobile banking, abandoning more rudimental approaches in favor of a more refined and professional philosophy.</p>

We have mentioned this actor previously in our blog about<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.threatfabric.com/blogs/bugdrop-new-dropper-bypassing-google-security-measures.html">BugDrop</a>: the products developed and distributed by this group have been circulating for the entirety of 2022, while the actors themselves surfaced by claiming the ownership of the malware in May.<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/hadoken.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=hadoken.png" alt="hadoken" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/hadoken.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=hadoken.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/hadoken.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=hadoken.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/hadoken.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=hadoken.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/hadoken.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=hadoken.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/hadoken.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=hadoken.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/hadoken.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=hadoken.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br>The main product of this group is<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.threatfabric.com/blogs/xenomorph-a-newly-hatched-banking-trojan.html">Xenomorph, a Android banking trojan discovered by ThreatFabric in February 2022</a>. This malware family has been a work in progress for the entirety of 2022, and despite being distributed in small campaigns, it never truly reached the volume of other malware families on the threat landscape, such as Octo or more recently Hook.</p> 
<p>Xenomorph campaigns have always been characterized by<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>short and contained distribution efforts</strong>, first via GymDrop, a dropper operation created and managed by the same group, and later via<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.threatfabric.com/blogs/zombinder-ermac-and-desktop-stealers.html"><strong>Zombinder</strong>, another distribution vector that we covered on a previous article in December 2022</a>. In either case, the short bursts of activity were indicative of short test runs opposed to a real large scale distribution with fraudulent intent.</p> 
<p>However, things are very likely to change in the near future: ThreatFabric’s analysts have discovered a new variant of this malware family, which we classify as<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Xenomorph.C</strong>.</p> 
<p>This new version of the malware adds many new capabilities to an already feature rich Android Banker, most notably the introduction of a very extensive<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>runtime engine powered by Accessibility services</strong>, which is used by actors to implement a complete<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>ATS framework</strong>. With these new features, Xenomorph is now able to completely automate the whole fraud chain, from infection to funds exfiltration, making it one of the<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>most advanced and dangerous</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>Android Malware trojans in circulation.</p> 
<p>In addition, the samples identified by ThreatFabric featured configurations with Target lists made of<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>more than 400 banking and financial institutions</strong>, including several<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>cryptocurrency wallets</strong>, with an increase of more than 6 times with comparison to its previous variants, including financial institutions from all continents.</p> 
<p>In addition, after discovering some samples belonging to this new variant, our researchers also discovered the<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>website</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>dedicated to the<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>advertisement</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>of this Android banker, indicating clear intentions of entering the<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>MaaS landscape</strong>, and start large scale distribution.<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ad.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=ad.png" alt="ad" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ad.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=ad.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ad.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=ad.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ad.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=ad.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ad.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=ad.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ad.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=ad.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ad.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=ad.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br>This functionality is typical of more advanced malware families, such as Gustuff and SharkBot, which have caused thousands of euros worth of damage towards their targeted institutions.</p> 
<p>In this article we will cover the main new features of this variant, and how these new variations can elevate Xenomorph’s threat level.</p> 
<h2 id="distribution">Distribution</h2> 
<h3 id="test-samples">Test Samples</h3> 
<p>ThreatFabric was able to identify also some samples connected to<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>test</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>campaigns: in these cases, the samples seem to be linked with distribution abusing third party hosting services, more specifically<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Discord Content Delivery Network (CDN)</strong>. This is not the first time we see malware using this sort of legitimate hosting services: it not uncommon to see malware authors use services such as Discord CDN or GitHub repositories to hide in plain sight their products.<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cdn.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=cdn.png" alt="cdn" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cdn.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=cdn.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cdn.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=cdn.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cdn.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=cdn.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cdn.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=cdn.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cdn.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=cdn.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cdn.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=cdn.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br>The reasons for using this sort of service are quite straight forward: these are very common services, which are very<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>reliable</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>and used by millions of people. In addition, it is<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>free</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>to open an account and use it to distribute malware and there are no limitation on the number of accounts. Finally, it is<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>very common</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>for devices to connect to such services, so it is less likely that a security service might flag connections to these domains as suspicious.</p> 
<p>In this specific case it is likely that these samples, which are not really part of any campaign, were simply hosted on Discord CDN for sharing purposes, and not for distribution.</p> 
<h3 id="zombinder-campaign">Zombinder Campaign</h3> 
<p>The first variants of Xenomorph were distributed by<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>GymDrop</strong>, in February 2022. Later in the year we saw the Hadoken group switch distribution medium, trying out first<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>BugDrop</strong>, and finally landing on<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Zombinder</strong>. In our case, Xenomorph v3 is deployed by a<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Zombinder</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>app “bound” to a legitimate currency converter, which downloads as an “update” an application posing as Google Protect:<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/zombinder.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=zombinder.png" alt="zombinder" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/zombinder.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=zombinder.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/zombinder.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=zombinder.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/zombinder.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=zombinder.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/zombinder.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=zombinder.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/zombinder.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=zombinder.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/zombinder.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=zombinder.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br>This seems to be the method of choice with the third version of Xenomorph, abandoning previous in-house developed techniques. Despite this, actors behind Zombinder have claimed to have stopped providing the service, indicating that there might be once again a switch in distribution in future builds of Xenomorph.</p> 
<h2 id="targets">Targets</h2> 
<p>Xenomorph, since its first appearance, has revolved around<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>gathering PII</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>such as usernames and passwords using<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>overlay attacks</strong>.</p> 
<p>Over the course of 2022, Xenomorph has maintained a relatively stable set of targets in its configuration, with specific interest in<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Spain</strong>,<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Portugal</strong>, and<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Italy</strong>, with the latest campaigns also introducing<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Belgian</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>and<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Canadian</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>institutions, together with some cryptocurrency wallets.</p> 
<p>The first sample of this new variant analyzed by ThreatFabric continued this trend, featuring the same list of targets as the previous versions observed. However, another sample, seemingly belonging to the same campaign, but sporting the tag “<strong>xeno3-test</strong>”, contained a much larger list of targets, counting<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>more than 400 institutions</strong>, more than 6 times the number of targets available in the first sample.</p> 
<p>In the case of Android Banking malware served as<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>MaaS</strong>, it is relatively common that<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>different campaigns</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>of the same malware variant will have<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>different targets</strong>, based on the requirements of the actors managing it. In many cases, actors who develop malware outsource the job of maintaining overlays up-to-date with the latest designs of all the different banking application that they target. There are several actors who sell this sort of service in hacking forums.<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/targets-02.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=targets-02.png" alt="targets-02" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/targets-02.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=targets-02.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/targets-02.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=targets-02.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/targets-02.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=targets-02.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/targets-02.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=targets-02.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/targets-02.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=targets-02.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/targets-02.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=targets-02.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br>Considering the “xeno3-test” tag, it is likely that the application belongs to a test build, which might feature the<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>actual list of possible targets</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>from which renters can choose from. Both lists will be available in the Appendix section of this article.</p> 
<h2 id="capabilities">Capabilities</h2> 
<p>The first variant of Xenomorph discovered in February 2022 lacked a large amount of features, such as accessibility logging and remote actions to abuse Accessibility Services to perform fraud. It is clear that ThreatFabric detected these first samples while the malware was still undergoing a clear development phase.</p> 
<p>After a few months of inactivity since its initial discovery, a new variant of the malware was discovered by ThreatFabric researchers in June 2022. It included a complete overhaul of the code base,<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>increasing the modularity</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>of the source code in order to make the malware more flexible and easier to update. This was very likely a initial test phase of Xenomorph, which introduced the support for remote actions thanks to the introduction of a Accessibility Services powered runtime engine, which could be used to simulate actions to impersonate the victim.</p>

<h3 id="ats-framework">ATS Framework</h3> 
<p>As we covered in previous articles, the term<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>ATS (Automated Transfer Systems)</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>is used to define a set of features that allow criminals to automatically complete fraudulent transactions on infected devices. Such systems are able to<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>automatically</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>extract credentials, account balance, initiate transactions, obtain MFA tokens and finalize the fund transfers,<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>without the need of human interaction from an operator</strong>.</p> 
<p>Scripts are received in JSON format, are processed, and converted into a list of operations to be executed by the engine on the device. Here is an example of the structure of such scripts:<br><br></p> 
<pre>{<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; "module": "&lt;MODULE_NAME&gt;",<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; "version": 1,<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; "parameters": [...], // LIST OF PARAMETERS<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; "requires": [...], // LIST OF REQUIRED CONDITIONS<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; "triggerConditions": [...], // LIST OF TRIGGER CONDITIONS<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; "terminator": {<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; ...<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; }, // IS TERMINATOR ENABLED<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; "operations": [...] // LIST OF OPERATIONS TO BE EXECUTED (ATS)<br>}</pre> 
<p><br>With the help of such systems, the malware present on an infected device can<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>easily extract the required PIIs and use them to perform all sorts of criminal activity</strong>.</p> 
<p>The engine used by Xenomorph stands out from its competition thanks to the extensive selection of possible actions that are programmable and can be included in ATS scripts, in addition to a system that allows for<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>conditional execution</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>and<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>action prioritization</strong>. To illustrate the capabilities of this engine, we will take as an example a script extracted from Xenomorph’s config and used to<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>extract MFA codes</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>from Google’s authenticator application.</p> 
<p>Banks are slowly abandoning the use of SMS to perform Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). As an alternative, many institutions seem to have opted for the use of<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Authenticators applications</strong>. However, such applications are often used on the same device used to complete the transaction. A modern banking malware installed on an infected device is able to initiate a fraudulent transaction abusing the targeted banking application, and at the same time use the authenticator app to read the required authentication codes.</p> 
<p>In the case of Xenomorph, criminals created a ATS module for exactly this purpose: the code collection module is triggered whenever the authenticator app is launched by the malware, using quite flexible conditional trigger conditions, as shown in the image below:<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ats-condition.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=ats-condition.png" alt="ats-condition" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ats-condition.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=ats-condition.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ats-condition.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=ats-condition.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ats-condition.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=ats-condition.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ats-condition.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=ats-condition.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ats-condition.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=ats-condition.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/ats-condition.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=ats-condition.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br>The engine provides quite a large set of customizable options, including for example logical operators. This allows criminals to create complex conditions to take care of all possible scenarios, increasing the effectiveness of each infection.</p> 
<p>If these conditions are satisfied, the malware will proceed and extract codes which follow a specific structure, which in the case of authenticator codes consists in two groups made out of three digits, as shown in the following image:<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/authenticator.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=authenticator.png" alt="authenticator" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/authenticator.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=authenticator.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/authenticator.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=authenticator.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/authenticator.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=authenticator.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/authenticator.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=authenticator.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/authenticator.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=authenticator.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/authenticator.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=authenticator.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br>This is just an example of ATS script. <h3 id="cookie-stealer">Cookie Stealer</h3> 
<p>Xenomorph’s latest version also added<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Cookie stealer</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>capabilities to its already very extensive arsenal of weapons. After being introduced in the world of Android bankers by S.O.V.A. in September 2021, this feature was also added to the list of features of other families such as SharkBot, and now Xenomorph.</p> 
<p>Session Cookies allow users to maintain open sessions on their browsers without having to re-input their credentials repeatedly. A malicious actor in possession on a valid session cookie has effectively access to the victim’s logged in web session.</p> 
<p>Xenomorph, just like the other malware families previously mentioned, starts a browser with<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>JavaScript interface enabled</strong>. The malware uses this browser to display the targeted page to the victim, with the intent of tricking users into logging into the service whose cookie Xenomorph is trying to extract.<br><br><img loading="lazy" src="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cookies.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=cookies.png" alt="cookies" width="1920" height="1080" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1920px;" srcset="https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cookies.png?width=960&amp;height=540&amp;name=cookies.png 960w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cookies.png?width=1920&amp;height=1080&amp;name=cookies.png 1920w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cookies.png?width=2880&amp;height=1620&amp;name=cookies.png 2880w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cookies.png?width=3840&amp;height=2160&amp;name=cookies.png 3840w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cookies.png?width=4800&amp;height=2700&amp;name=cookies.png 4800w, https://www.threatfabric.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Threatfabric/images/blog/xenomorph-v3/cookies.png?width=5760&amp;height=3240&amp;name=cookies.png 5760w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px"><br><br></p>

<h2 id="conclusions">Conclusions</h2> 
<p>The Xenomorph saga highlights once more that actors are switching their focus on mobile malware. The efforts of Hadoken Security Group showcase how criminals are adopting more structured development cycles and programming philosophies to create increasingly more dangerous malware families. The latest version of Xenomorph included large improvements from its previous iteration, adding<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Automated Transfer System (ATS)</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>capabilities, which elevate the threat level of this family even more.</p> 
<p>Xenomorph v3 is capable of<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>performing the whole fraud chain</strong>, from infection, with the aid of<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>Zombinder</strong>, to the automated transfer using ATS, passing by PII exfiltration using Keylogging and Overlay attacks. In addition, the Threat Actor behind this malware family has started actively publicizing their product, indicating a<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>clear intention to expand the reach of this malware</strong>. ThreatFabric expects Xenomorph to increase in volume, with the likelihood of being one again distributed via droppers on the Google Play Store.</p>
