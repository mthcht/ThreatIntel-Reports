<div  class='av-special-heading av-i1gbtj-849342ba0cb8d1f0bd8671adefd81535 av-special-heading-h2 blockquote modern-quote  avia-builder-el-1  el_after_av_heading  el_before_av_textblock '><h2 class='av-special-heading-tag'  itemprop="headline"  >Abstract﻿</h2><div class="special-heading-border"><div class="special-heading-inner-border"></div></div></div>
<section  class='av_textblock_section av-k67lksdf-4f4b8b41311b1cc383b53ba7d86aaa5e'  itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost" ><div class='avia_textblock'  itemprop="text" ><p>SectorJ04 is a Russian-based cybercrime group that began operating about five years ago and conducted hacking activities for financial profit using malware such as banking trojans and ransomware against national and industrial sectors located across Europe, North America and West Africa.</p>
<p>In 2019, the SectorJ04 group expanded its hacking activities to cover various industrial sectors located across Southeast Asia and East Asia, and is changing the pattern of their attacks from targeted attacks to searching for random victims. This report includes details related to the major hacking targets of the SectorJ04 group in 2019, how those targets were hacked, characteristics of their hacking activities this year and recent cases of the SectorJ04 group&#8217;s hacking.</p>
</div></section>
<div  class='av-special-heading av-g3ytt3-3b0a2fb0f39d276007aaf1d05098eaa4 av-special-heading-h2 blockquote modern-quote  avia-builder-el-3  el_after_av_textblock  el_before_av_textblock '><h2 class='av-special-heading-tag'  itemprop="headline"  >SectorJ04 group activity range and hacking methods</h2><div class="special-heading-border"><div class="special-heading-inner-border"></div></div></div>
<section  class='av_textblock_section av-k67ll6rc-e16608a5ec8ec471572c070f9bc46a85'  itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost" ><div class='avia_textblock'  itemprop="text" ><p>The SectorJ04 group has maintained the scope of its existing hacking activities while expanding its hacking activities to companies in various industrial sectors located in East Asia and Southeast Asia. There was a significant increase in their hacking activities in 2019, especially those targeting South Korea. They mainly utilize spam email to deliver their backdoor to the infected system that can perform additional commands from the attacker&#8217;s server.</p>
</div></section>
<section  class='av_textblock_section av-k67llhvl-8ce3a8d76f7a8804022052e156e8b6a9'  itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost" ><div class='avia_textblock'  itemprop="text" ><p> <br />
<strong>Main countries and sectors targeted</strong></p>
<p>The SectorJ04 group&#8217;s preexisting targets were financial institutions located in countries such as North America and Europe, or general companies such as retail and manufacturing, but they recently expanded their areas of activity to include the medical, pharmaceutical, media, energy and manufacturing industries. They do not appear to place much restrictions on the sectors targeted. The following are the sectors and countries under which SectorJ04 group was found in 2019.</p>
</div></section>

<strong>Hacking Techniques</strong><br />
 <br />
The SectorJ04 group mainly utilizes a spear phishing email with MS Word or Excel files attached, and the document files downloads the Microsoft Installer (MSI) installation file from the attacker server and uses it to install backdoor on the infected system. As anti-virus programs have recently begun to detect MSI files, in some instances macro scripts contained in the malicious documents would install backdoors directly onto infected systems without using MSI files.</p>
</div></section>

<section  class='av_textblock_section av-k67lreb3-2391c602aa08ec99f741dedb01ef1372'  itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost" ><div class='avia_textblock'  itemprop="text" ><p>The SectorJ04 group mainly used their own backdoor, ServHelper and FlawedAmmyy RAT, for hacking. They also used the Remote Manipulator System (RMS) RAT, a legitimate remote management software created in Russia. Backdoors are installed in infected systems and they also distributed email stealers, botnet malware and ransomware through those backdoors.</p>
<p>They were recently confirmed to use additional backdoor called AdroMut and FlowerPippi, which is used to install other backdoor such as FlawedAmmyy RAT on behalf of the MSI file, or to collect system information and send it to the attacker&#8217;s server.</p>

<div  class='av-special-heading av-c154bb-e447f051066941d92d4b0daafac9ab11 av-special-heading-h2 blockquote modern-quote  avia-builder-el-55  el_after_av_textblock  el_before_av_textblock '><h2 class='av-special-heading-tag'  itemprop="headline"  >Characteristics of hacking activities of SectorJ04 group in 2019﻿</h2><div class="special-heading-border"><div class="special-heading-inner-border"></div></div></div>
<section  class='av_textblock_section av-3qin6tj-d303c1c969434c0b498947290d63aac1'  itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost" ><div class='avia_textblock'  itemprop="text" ><p>The following are the features of the first half of 2019 activities identified through the analysis of the SectorJ04 group&#8217;s hacking activities.</p>
<ul>
<li>Increased hacking activities targeting East and Southeast Asia</li>
<li>Changes in spam email format and hacking methods</li>
<li>Changes in targets of hacking from specific organizations and industry groups to large number of irregular ones</li>
</ul>
<p>Although the SectorJ04 group mainly targeted countries located in Europe or North America, it has recently expanded its field of activities to countries located in Southeast Asia and East Asia. In particular, the frequency of hacking attacks targeting South Korea has increased, and spam emails targeting China were found in May.</p>
<p>The changes could also be seen in attachments to spam emails used by attackers. Existing spam emails used attachments in the form of malicious documents, but attachments with HTM and HTML extensions were also found and the text included links to download malicious documents directly.</p>
<p>The SectorJ04 group&#8217;s initial spam emails had no mail content or only short sentences, but the latest spam emails found were elaborately written and included images. A new type of backdoor called AdroMut and a new malware called FlowerPippi was also found coming from SectorJ04.</p>
<p>Prior to 2019, the SectorJ04 group conducted large-scale hacking activities for financial gain using exploit kits on websites to install ransomware, such as Locky and GlobeImporter, along with its banking Trojan, on its victims computers. But after 2019 the group has changed its hacking strategy to attack using spam email. In particular, a number of remote control malware are utilized to gain access to resources such as email accounts and system login information from the infected machine to send more spam emails and distribute their malware.</p>
</div></section>
<section  class='av_textblock_section av-3nq9i87-738f67428a16ff70a18e6357eb676ab2'  itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost" ><div class='avia_textblock'  itemprop="text" ><p> <br />
<strong>Increased hacking activities targeting East and Southeast Asia</strong><br />
 <br />
The hacking activities of SectorJ04 group, which targeted South Korea in the first half of 2019, have been continuously discovered. The emails found were written in relation to invoice and tax accounting data, and were attached the MS Word or Excel files with malicious macros inserted. Malicious documents written in Korean have the same characteristics as other language hacking activities under the theme of MS Office.</p>
</div></section>

<section  class='av_textblock_section av-xb1kc7-ff6539bae69b59c30bbd965266dbd70e'  itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost" ><div class='avia_textblock'  itemprop="text" ><p><strong>Hacking activity using same email content as the past</strong><br />
 <br />
In early August, the SectorJ04 group carried out extensive hacking activities targeting the users around the world, including South Korea, India, Britain, the United States, Germany, Canada, Argentina, Bangladesh and Hong Kong.</p>
<p>Their activities were particularly heavy in healthcare-related areas such as healthcare, pharmaceuticals, biotechnology and healthcare-wage management, as well as energy-related companies such as gas and wind power. Also, they continued their attacks on preexisting hacking target areas such as manufacturing, distribution and retail.</p>
<p>The contents of the text written in French and English were found in the spam email, and an MS Word file with random numbers was used as an attachment. All emails found in the hacking activity had the same text content.</p>
