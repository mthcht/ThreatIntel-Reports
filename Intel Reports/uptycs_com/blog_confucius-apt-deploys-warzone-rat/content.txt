        <div class="content-wrapper blog--post-content-section">
          <article class="blog-post">
            
            <div class="blog-post__tags">
              Tags
              
              <a class="blog-post__tag-link" href="https://www.uptycs.com/blog/threat-research-report-team/tag/threats" rel="tag">Threats</a>
              
            </div>
             
            <div class="blog-post__body">
              <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p><em>Research by <a href="https://www.uptycs.com/blog/author/abhijit-mohanta">Abhijit Mohanta</a> and <a href="https://www.uptycs.com/blog/author/ashwin-vamshi">Ashwin Vamshi</a></em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Uptycs' threat research team <a href="https://www.uptycs.com/blog/warzone-rat-comes-with-uac-bypass-technique">published a piece</a> about Warzone RAT and its advanced capabilities in November 2020. During the first week of January 2021, we discovered an ongoing targeted attack campaign related to Confucius APT, a threat actor / group primarily targeting government sectors in South Asia. This attack was identified by our in-house osquery-based sandbox that triggered a detection on Warzone RAT activity.</p>
<!--more-->
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Based on our <a href="/products/use-cases/threat-hunting" rel="noopener" target="_blank">threat intelligence</a> systems, we were able to confirm that the threat actor is trying to circumvent attacks with decoys that deliver the next stage payload via the <a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1221/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">template injection technique</a> and a short C2 TTL (Time to Live).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Technical Analysis</h2>
<p>Our in-house sandbox, which uses Uptycs EDR for detection, detected a Warzone RAT payload in the attack kill chain of the decoy document “China Cruise Missiles Capabilities-Implications for the Indian Army.docx” (hash: b9b5a9fa0ad7f802899e82e103a6c2c699c09390b1a79ae2b357cacc68f1ca8e).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This attack document was crafted by the attacker group to entice the victims or targets into opening a file related to the ongoing India China border tension.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-1.png" alt="Screenshot from the China Cruise Missiles Capabilities-Implications for the Indian Army.docx decoy" width="624" style="width: 624px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1.png?width=312&amp;name=Figure-1.png 312w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-1.png 624w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1.png?width=936&amp;name=Figure-1.png 936w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1.png?width=1248&amp;name=Figure-1.png 1248w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-1.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1.png?width=1872&amp;name=Figure-1.png 1872w" sizes="(max-width: 624px) 100vw, 624px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 1: Screenshot from the "China Cruise Missiles Capabilities-Implications for the Indian Army.docx" decoy.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>We believe the decoy lure must have been copied from <a href="https://www.orfonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/ORF_IssueBrief_427_ChinaCruiseMissiles.pdf">this PDF</a>, which contains a study by Kartik Bommakanti for the Observer Research Foundation (ORF).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Attack Kill Chain</h2>
<p>The decoy lure was a 16-page document that would have skipped the eye of static heuristic engines because they generally scan suspicious files based on the number of pages (malicious documents are usually one page).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Upon execution, the document used template injection to download the next stage RTF exploit that downloaded the final stage Warzone payload using a DLL embedded in the RTF exploit. The attack kill chain of the different phases of the attack is detailed in figure 2, below.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-2.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-2.png" alt="Attack kill chain." width="624" style="width: 624px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-2.png?width=312&amp;name=Figure-2.png 312w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-2.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-2.png 624w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-2.png?width=936&amp;name=Figure-2.png 936w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-2.png?width=1248&amp;name=Figure-2.png 1248w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-2.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-2.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-2.png?width=1872&amp;name=Figure-2.png 1872w" sizes="(max-width: 624px) 100vw, 624px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 2: Attack kill chain of the different phases of the attack.<br><br></em></p>
<p>The various phases of the attack are as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>Victim opens the Word document</li>
<li>Document downloads template RTF</li>
<li>Exploit in RTF is triggered and bing.dll is dropped and executed</li>
<li>Bing.dll downloads Warzone RAT</li>
</ul>
<p>Using the template injection technique, the next stage payload is downloaded via the word/_rels/settings.xml.rels file present in the document structure as shown in figure 3, below.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3.png?width=625&amp;name=Figure-3.png" alt="setting.xmls.rels containing link to template." width="625" style="width: 625px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3.png?width=313&amp;name=Figure-3.png 313w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3.png?width=625&amp;name=Figure-3.png 625w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3.png?width=938&amp;name=Figure-3.png 938w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3.png?width=1250&amp;name=Figure-3.png 1250w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3.png?width=1563&amp;name=Figure-3.png 1563w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3.png?width=1875&amp;name=Figure-3.png 1875w" sizes="(max-width: 625px) 100vw, 625px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 3: setting.xmls.rels containing link to template.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The downloaded template (hash: 2f5fc653550b0b5d093427263b26892e3468e125686eb41206319c7060212c40) is an RTF file containing exploit code for the old vulnerability “CVE-2018-0802” in the Microsoft equation editor (EQNEDT32.exe). This is evident from the CLSID present in the RTF file “7b0002CE02-0000-0000-C000-000000000046,” which is related to the equation editor. The RTF contains a DLL embedded in an OLE object as shown in figure 4, below.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-4.png" alt="DLL embedded in OLE." width="624" style="width: 624px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4.png?width=312&amp;name=Figure-4.png 312w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-4.png 624w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4.png?width=936&amp;name=Figure-4.png 936w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4.png?width=1248&amp;name=Figure-4.png 1248w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-4.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4.png?width=1872&amp;name=Figure-4.png 1872w" sizes="(max-width: 624px) 100vw, 624px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 4: DLL embedded in OLE.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The embedded DLL file, bing.dll (SHA-256: 07277c9f33d0ae873c2be3742669594acc18c7aa93ecadb8b2ce9b870baceb2f), which is executed upon successful exploitation, contains an export “mark” that is responsible for downloading the Warzone payload. Figure 5, below, shows the code that downloads the Warzone payload.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-5.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-5.png" alt="Downloader code in DLL." width="624" style="width: 624px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-5.png?width=312&amp;name=Figure-5.png 312w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-5.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-5.png 624w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-5.png?width=936&amp;name=Figure-5.png 936w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-5.png?width=1248&amp;name=Figure-5.png 1248w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-5.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-5.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-5.png?width=1872&amp;name=Figure-5.png 1872w" sizes="(max-width: 624px) 100vw, 624px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 5: Downloader code in DLL.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Warzone payload is saved to the %ProgramData% folder as update.exe (SHA-256: 4500851dad1ac87165fc938fe5034983c10423f800bbc2661741f39e43ab8c8d) as shown in the above figure. In order to maintain persistence, an LNK file named update.lnk pointing to update.exe is dropped to startup folders - “%AppData%Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs\Startup”.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Warzone RAT was caught by Uptycs in November. It has capabilities to log keystrokes, steal passwords, capture the webcam, and it has the ability to bypass UAC on Windows 10. You can read more details about Warzone RAT in our <a href="https://www.uptycs.com/blog/warzone-rat-comes-with-uac-bypass-technique">blog post</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Similar Themed Attacks Delivering Warzone RAT</h2>
<p>We identified three similar DLL files in our threat intelligence systems with the same imphash: 58f8f4bdb6d7059247f4fe90a8ba9477. Using this data, we identified three more decoy documents most likely used for different targets using these DLL files as the next stage payloads.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The first decoy document was observed in October 2020.</p>
<ul>
<li>File name: Testing.docx</li>
<li>Hash: a3cd781b14d75de94e5263ce37a572cdf5fe5013ec85ff8daeee3783ff95b073</li>
<li>RTF hash: 686847b331ace1b93b48528ba50507cbf0f9b59aef5b5f539a7d6f2246135424</li>

<li>C2: recent[.]wordupdate[.]com</li>
</ul>

<p><span style="color: #4d4d4d;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-6.png" alt="China preparing for war in the Taiwan Strait decoy." width="624" style="width: 624px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6.png?width=312&amp;name=Figure-6.png 312w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-6.png 624w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6.png?width=936&amp;name=Figure-6.png 936w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6.png?width=1248&amp;name=Figure-6.png 1248w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-6.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6.png?width=1872&amp;name=Figure-6.png 1872w" sizes="(max-width: 624px) 100vw, 624px"></span></p>

<p>The second decoy was observed in November 2020. Interestingly, this decoy had the same hash of the next stage RTF and the DLL payloads used in the first decoy document.</p>
<ul>
<li>File name: Suparco Vacancy Notification.docx</li>
<li>Hash: 59ccfff73bdb8567e7673a57b73f86fc082b0e4eeaa3faf7e92875c35bf4f62c</li>

<p>Also in November 2020, we identified another highly targeted decoy:</p>
<ul>
<li>Hash: 59cd62ad204e536b178db3e2ea10b36c782be4aa4849c10eef8484433a524297</li>
<li>RTF hash: 3ce48f371129a086935b031333387ea73282bda5f22ff78c85ee7f0f5e4625fe</li>

<p>This decoy focused on another attention-grabbing topic—what to expect from Joe Biden, the new president of the United States, related to top nuclear weapons issues. The DLL file connected to the same C2 and contained the same PDB path in the above two documents.</p>
<p><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-8.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-8.png" alt="Top nuclear weapons issues decoy." width="624" style="width: 624px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-8.png?width=312&amp;name=Figure-8.png 312w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-8.png?width=624&amp;name=Figure-8.png 624w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-8.png?width=936&amp;name=Figure-8.png 936w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-8.png?width=1248&amp;name=Figure-8.png 1248w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-8.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-8.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-8.png?width=1872&amp;name=Figure-8.png 1872w" sizes="(max-width: 624px) 100vw, 624px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 8: Top nuclear weapons issues decoy.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Based on the decoys and the topics, we believe the campaign is ongoing with selected targets. As the C2 TTL is short lived, we believe the threat actor is tailoring the attacks to selected targets and taking down their attack elements.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Targeted attacks will always try to leverage the latest news with high media attention to tailor attacks. The Warzone RAT was deployed as the final stage payload to monitor and carry surveillance on the victim's machine. While traditional solutions have a detection stance against such threats, it is always recommended to have a layered security approach that has advanced analytics and granular visibility of targeted attacks and the next stage payloads used in their attack kill chains.</p>
