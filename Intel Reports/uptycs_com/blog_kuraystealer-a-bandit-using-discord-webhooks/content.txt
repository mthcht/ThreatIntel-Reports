          <article class="blog-post">
            
            <div class="blog-post__tags">
              Tags
              
              <a class="blog-post__tag-link" href="https://www.uptycs.com/blog/threat-research-report-team/tag/threats" rel="tag">Threats</a>
              
            </div>
             
            <div class="blog-post__body">
              <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p style="font-size: 14px;"><em>Research by: Ashwin Vamshi and Shilpesh Trivedi</em></p>
<p style="font-size: 14px;">&nbsp;</p>
<!--more-->
<p>Uptycs' threat research team has recently discovered a new malware builder—a tool sold to criminals to make it easier to build malware—we have named KurayStealer that has password stealing and screenshot capabilities. KurayStealer is a builder written in Python which harvests the passwords and screenshots and sends them to the attackers’ Discord channel via webhooks.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>It is available as a free and commercial (VIP) software. This was discovered through the intelligence monitoring rules in our threat intelligence systems. Based on the source code and the OSINT intelligence, we have evidence that the creator of this builder is of Spanish origin and has also started selling paid versions of password stealers with added functionalities.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This blog post details the working of the KurayStealer and also shares insights into the author behind this malware.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2 style="font-size: 36px;">Discovery of KurayStealer<span style="font-size: 20px;"></span></h2>
<p>Uptycs <a href="/products/use-cases/threat-hunting" rel="noopener" target="_blank">threat intelligence</a> systems detected the first sample of KurayStealer on 27 April 2022. Based on our OSINT research, this builder was first advertised on 23 April 2022 via the Youtube and Discord handle carrying the name “Portu.”</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2 style="font-size: 36px;">KurayStealer Operation</h2>
<p>The builder KurayStealer was identified in our intelligence systems on 27 April 2022 (hash - 8535c08d7e637219470c701599b5de4b85f082c446b4d12c718fa780e7535f07) with filename <strong>c2.py</strong>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The builder was written in Python and works in Python 3.0 (a.k.a. "Python 3000" or "Py3k").</p>
<p>Upon execution, the builder checks for the universally unique identifier (UUID) using the command “wmic csproduct get UUID” (see Figure 1).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/1.uuid.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank" linktext=""><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/1.uuid.png?width=4998&amp;name=1.uuid.png" alt="1.uuid" width="4998" loading="lazy" style="width: 4998px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/1.uuid.png?width=2499&amp;name=1.uuid.png 2499w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/1.uuid.png?width=4998&amp;name=1.uuid.png 4998w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/1.uuid.png?width=7497&amp;name=1.uuid.png 7497w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/1.uuid.png?width=9996&amp;name=1.uuid.png 9996w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/1.uuid.png?width=12495&amp;name=1.uuid.png 12495w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/1.uuid.png?width=14994&amp;name=1.uuid.png 14994w" sizes="(max-width: 4998px) 100vw, 4998px"></a><br><em>Figure 1: KurayStealer UUID check</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This check is performed to verify the generated UUID matches the list of UUIDs in the https://pastebin[.]com/raw/Hc9W90Ld to determine if the user is a free or VIP user (see Figure 2 ).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/2.Builder.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2.Builder.png?width=955&amp;name=2.Builder.png" alt="2.Builder" width="955" loading="lazy" style="width: 955px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2.Builder.png?width=478&amp;name=2.Builder.png 478w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2.Builder.png?width=955&amp;name=2.Builder.png 955w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2.Builder.png?width=1433&amp;name=2.Builder.png 1433w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2.Builder.png?width=1910&amp;name=2.Builder.png 1910w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2.Builder.png?width=2388&amp;name=2.Builder.png 2388w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2.Builder.png?width=2865&amp;name=2.Builder.png 2865w" sizes="(max-width: 955px) 100vw, 955px"></a><br><em>Figure 2: Builder asking to enter the user type and Discord Webhook</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Based on the free or VIP user and the input of the webhook, a file named DualMTS.py, DualMTS_VIP.py is dropped in the machine. We have used the free version of the module for demonstrating the functionality of the builder.</p>
<p>DualMTS.py attempts to replace the string “api/webhooks” with “Kisses” in BetterDiscord in an attempt to bypass the protection and send webhooks seamlessly (see Figure 3).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/3.bypass.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/3.bypass.png?width=3332&amp;name=3.bypass.png" alt="3.bypass" width="3332" loading="lazy" style="width: 3332px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/3.bypass.png?width=1666&amp;name=3.bypass.png 1666w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/3.bypass.png?width=3332&amp;name=3.bypass.png 3332w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/3.bypass.png?width=4998&amp;name=3.bypass.png 4998w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/3.bypass.png?width=6664&amp;name=3.bypass.png 6664w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/3.bypass.png?width=8330&amp;name=3.bypass.png 8330w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/3.bypass.png?width=9996&amp;name=3.bypass.png 9996w" sizes="(max-width: 3332px) 100vw, 3332px"></a><br><em>Figure 3: Bypassing BetterDiscord protections</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The file DualMTS.py then attempts to take the screenshot of the machine using the python module “pyautogui”. Alongside this, it also takes the geo-location of the machine. The snippet of this operation is shown in the figure below (see Figure 4).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/4.%20geo-screenshot.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/4.%20geo-screenshot.png?width=1857&amp;name=4.%20geo-screenshot.png" alt="4. geo-screenshot" width="1857" loading="lazy" style="width: 1857px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/4.%20geo-screenshot.png?width=929&amp;name=4.%20geo-screenshot.png 929w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/4.%20geo-screenshot.png?width=1857&amp;name=4.%20geo-screenshot.png 1857w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/4.%20geo-screenshot.png?width=2786&amp;name=4.%20geo-screenshot.png 2786w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/4.%20geo-screenshot.png?width=3714&amp;name=4.%20geo-screenshot.png 3714w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/4.%20geo-screenshot.png?width=4643&amp;name=4.%20geo-screenshot.png 4643w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/4.%20geo-screenshot.png?width=5571&amp;name=4.%20geo-screenshot.png 5571w" sizes="(max-width: 1857px) 100vw, 1857px"></a><br><em>Figure 4: Screenshot and geo-location in the builder</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>It also harvests the passwords and tokens from a list of 21 software packages as follows: Discord, Lightcord, Discord PTB, Opera, Opera GX, Amigo, Torch, Kometa, Orbitum, CentBrowser, 7Star, Sputnik, Vivaldi, Chrome SxS, Chrome, Epic Privacy Browser, Microsoft Edge, Uran, Yandex, Brave, Iridium.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The harvested information including computername, geo-location, ipaddress, credentials and the screenshot of the victim machine is sent over to the Discord channel via webhooks (see Figure 5).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/5.webhook.png?width=496&amp;height=449&amp;name=5.webhook.png" alt="5.webhook" width="496" height="449" loading="lazy" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 496px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/5.webhook.png?width=248&amp;height=225&amp;name=5.webhook.png 248w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/5.webhook.png?width=496&amp;height=449&amp;name=5.webhook.png 496w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/5.webhook.png?width=744&amp;height=674&amp;name=5.webhook.png 744w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/5.webhook.png?width=992&amp;height=898&amp;name=5.webhook.png 992w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/5.webhook.png?width=1240&amp;height=1123&amp;name=5.webhook.png 1240w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/5.webhook.png?width=1488&amp;height=1347&amp;name=5.webhook.png 1488w" sizes="(max-width: 496px) 100vw, 496px"><br><em>Figure 5 : Credentials sent over Discord webhook</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Uptycs EDR armed with YARA process scanning detected the KurayStealer with a threat score of 10/10 (see Figure 6).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/6.yara.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/6.yara.png?width=1151&amp;name=6.yara.png" alt="6.yara" width="1151" loading="lazy" style="width: 1151px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/6.yara.png?width=576&amp;name=6.yara.png 576w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/6.yara.png?width=1151&amp;name=6.yara.png 1151w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/6.yara.png?width=1727&amp;name=6.yara.png 1727w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/6.yara.png?width=2302&amp;name=6.yara.png 2302w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/6.yara.png?width=2878&amp;name=6.yara.png 2878w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/6.yara.png?width=3453&amp;name=6.yara.png 3453w" sizes="(max-width: 1151px) 100vw, 1151px"></a><br><em>Figure 6: Uptycs EDR detection of KurayStealer</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Additionally, Uptycs EDR contextual detection provides l details about the detected malware. Users can navigate to the advanced threat section and click on the icon to learn about the behavior and the operation of the malware.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2 style="font-size: 36px;">KurayStealer OSINT</h2>
<p>Upon analyzing the builder code, we identified a snippet claiming the module was written “Suleymansha &amp; Portu.” While the builder claims to be written by Suleymansha &amp; Portu, we have seen several other similar versions floating around in public repositories like github.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Based on the working and implementation, the KurayStelaer builder has several components of different password stealers using Discord tokens as command and control (C2) channels for harvesting victim data.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The builder code also contained the Discord channel invite link https://Discord[.]gg/AHR84u767J belonging to the creators behind this builder. The channel mentions a post of the commercial version of this builder at different pricing options (see Figure 7).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/7.commercial.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/7.commercial.png?width=1544&amp;name=7.commercial.png" alt="7.commercial" width="1544" loading="lazy" style="width: 1544px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/7.commercial.png?width=772&amp;name=7.commercial.png 772w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/7.commercial.png?width=1544&amp;name=7.commercial.png 1544w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/7.commercial.png?width=2316&amp;name=7.commercial.png 2316w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/7.commercial.png?width=3088&amp;name=7.commercial.png 3088w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/7.commercial.png?width=3860&amp;name=7.commercial.png 3860w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/7.commercial.png?width=4632&amp;name=7.commercial.png 4632w" sizes="(max-width: 1544px) 100vw, 1544px"></a><em>Figure 7 : Commercial versions</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Upon looking into the channel, we found that the profile for the Discord user “portu” contains a Discord channel name, Shoppy link, and YouTube link.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>We visited the YouTube link and identified the location of the user in Spain and found a video demonstrating KurayStealer (see Figure 8).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/2-1.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2-1.png?width=940&amp;name=2-1.png" alt="2-1" width="940" loading="lazy" style="width: 940px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2-1.png?width=470&amp;name=2-1.png 470w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2-1.png?width=940&amp;name=2-1.png 940w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2-1.png?width=1410&amp;name=2-1.png 1410w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2-1.png?width=1880&amp;name=2-1.png 1880w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2-1.png?width=2350&amp;name=2-1.png 2350w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/2-1.png?width=2820&amp;name=2-1.png 2820w" sizes="(max-width: 940px) 100vw, 940px"></a><br><em>Figure 8: Portu’s YouTube channel and demo video for KurayStealer&nbsp;</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Alongside this, the shoppy profile link of “Portu” contained several other tools the author planned to create and their commercial offerings (see Figure 9).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2617658.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/2617658/10.cart.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/10.cart.png?width=1329&amp;name=10.cart.png" alt="10.cart" width="1329" loading="lazy" style="width: 1329px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/10.cart.png?width=665&amp;name=10.cart.png 665w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/10.cart.png?width=1329&amp;name=10.cart.png 1329w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/10.cart.png?width=1994&amp;name=10.cart.png 1994w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/10.cart.png?width=2658&amp;name=10.cart.png 2658w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/10.cart.png?width=3323&amp;name=10.cart.png 3323w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/10.cart.png?width=3987&amp;name=10.cart.png 3987w" sizes="(max-width: 1329px) 100vw, 1329px"></a><br><em>Figure 9: Portu Shoppy profile and offerings</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>At the time of this writing, the author has deleted the YouTube video from his channel. The Discord channel of the authors also had an announcement on 26 April 2022 of a ransomware in the making. Based on the announcement and the observations, we believe that the authors might come up with newer versions of password stealers and other malware.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2 style="font-size: 36px;">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Our research on KurayStealer backed with OSINT highlights the rise in prevalence of password stealers using Discord tokens as a C2 for harvesting the victims’ credentials. Enterprises must have tight security controls and multi-layered visibility and security solutions to identify and detect such attacks. Uptycs’ EDR correlation engine detected the KurayStealer activity by correlating generic behavioral rules and YARA process scanning capabilities.&nbsp;</p>
