Kaspersky, which published a report on the methods and targets of Turla (which did not at the time mention Finland) last autumn, has outlined the methods used by the attackers.</p><p class="sc-kiMgGE sc-bhgfbr eQvHnw iEywHx yle__article__paragraph">First, cyber spies start by gathering information about the target via sources like social media, public websites and internal phone directories.</p><p class="sc-kiMgGE sc-bhgfbr eQvHnw iEywHx yle__article__paragraph">Second, they choose a few employees to whom they send emails which include a link to an apparently interesting website, which appears to be connected somehow to the targets everyday life or area of specialization. The hackers have however embedded malware in the site concerned.</p><p class="sc-kiMgGE sc-bhgfbr eQvHnw iEywHx yle__article__paragraph">Third, when the employee visits the site, the malware ends up on his or her computer.</p><p class="sc-kiMgGE sc-bhgfbr eQvHnw iEywHx yle__article__paragraph">That allows in the attacker, as happened to the Finnish Foreign Ministry in 2013.
